# Analysis: AI Deepfakes in Elections — An Axiom 4 Test

**Is the mechanism novel?**

Analyzed: 2026-03-12 ~06:00 UTC

## Source

Two cases of AI-generated deepfakes deployed in election contexts:

1. **New Hampshire Biden robocall** (January 2024): AI-cloned Biden
   voice sent via robocall to 20,000+ voters urging them not to vote in
   the primary. Commissioned by political consultant Steve Kramer.
   Sources: NPR (2024-05-23), PBS NewsHour (2024-01-22).

2. **Slovakia parliamentary election** (September 2023): Multiple
   AI-generated audio clips targeting pro-European candidate Michal
   Šimečka, released during the pre-election media moratorium. Sources:
   Harvard Kennedy School Misinformation Review, CNN (2024-02-01).

**Adversarial input**: Selected to test Axiom 4 (mechanisms consistent;
manifestations vary). Axiom 4 has zero evidence — it has never been
tested. The falsification condition is: "A mechanism of power is found
that has no structural parallel in any other era or context." AI
deepfakes in elections are a candidate for a genuinely novel mechanism.

## Narrative

The New Hampshire incident followed a recognizable structure. On January
21, 2024, the day before the presidential primary, voters received
robocalls featuring an AI-generated voice imitating Biden. The synthetic
voice included Biden's characteristic phrase "What a bunch of malarkey"
and instructed listeners to "save your vote for the November election."
The calls spoofed the caller ID to show the phone number of a former
state Democratic Party chair running a Biden write-in campaign. Digital
forensics expert Hany Farid assessed the audio as "a relatively
low-quality AI fake." The FCC proposed a $6 million fine; New Hampshire
filed 26 criminal charges (13 felony voter suppression, 13 misdemeanor
candidate impersonation).

The Slovakia incident was more structurally complex. Two days before the
September 2023 parliamentary election, fabricated audio surfaced
purporting to show Progressive Slovakia leader Šimečka discussing ballot
manipulation and bribery with a journalist. Additional deepfakes
targeted Šimečka and President Čaputová. The timing exploited Slovakia's
pre-election media moratorium — a legal prohibition on media discussion
of election developments — ensuring the fabrications circulated through
Telegram and encrypted channels while mainstream media and the targeted
candidates had constrained capacity to respond. Šimečka's party, which
had led in polls, lost. Robert Fico's pro-Russian Smer party won.

The Harvard Kennedy School analysis provides the most relevant material
for the Axiom 4 test. The authors argue that attributing Fico's victory
to the deepfakes "downplays critical factors that prepared the ground
for his pro-Putin message." Nearly 80% of Slovaks view Russia as a
"traditional Slavic brother." Trust in government and mainstream media
was at historical lows. Pro-Kremlin disinformation campaigns had
operated since 2010. The deepfakes exploited pre-existing conditions;
they did not create them. The authors conclude that deepfakes represent
"new opportunities for negotiating expertise" rather than a
fundamentally new mechanism — the fabrication of evidence for political
manipulation is ancient; AI changes the economics of deployment.

## The Axiom 4 test

Axiom 4 states: "The underlying patterns of power — concentration,
legitimation, surveillance, manufactured consent, dependency creation —
recur across eras, cultures, and political systems. The surface forms
differ. The structures rhyme."

The falsification condition: a mechanism of power is found that has no
structural parallel in any other era or context.

**Pre-AI structural parallels identified:**

- **Forged documents for political effect**: The Protocols of the Elders
  of Zion (1903), the Zinoviev Letter (1924), Operation INFEKTION
  (Soviet disinformation campaign using forged documents about HIV/AIDS,
  1983). Fabrication of evidence for political manipulation predates
  recording technology entirely.

- **Voice impersonation for fraud**: Pre-digital. Telephone fraud using
  voice mimicry is documented throughout the 20th century. "Vishing"
  (voice phishing) is an established attack vector.

- **Election-eve disinformation timed to prevent rebuttal**: The
  Slovakia moratorium exploitation mirrors a tactic documented across
  centuries — releasing damaging material (true or fabricated) too late
  for the target to respond before voting.

- **Robocalls with misleading content**: Decades of documented use in
  US elections. The medium (automated telephone calls) and the purpose
  (voter suppression or misdirection) are both established.

- **Caller ID spoofing**: Pre-AI technology used to disguise the origin
  of calls.

- **Exploiting information asymmetry during communication blackouts**:
  Structural parallel to wartime propaganda targeting populations with
  constrained information access.

**AI-specific affordances that might constitute novelty:**

- **Cost reduction**: Previously, convincing voice forgery required
  skilled impersonators or state-level resources. AI voice cloning
  reduces the cost to near zero and the skill requirement to minimal.

- **Scalability**: One person (Kramer) commissioned a synthetic
  presidential voice and distributed it to 20,000+ people. Slovakia
  saw at least three distinct deepfakes targeting different audiences
  from what appears to be a coordinated campaign.

- **Verisimilitude at scale**: AI-generated audio can incorporate
  characteristic speech patterns, verbal tics, and vocal quality.
  The NH deepfake was assessed as "low-quality" — the ceiling of
  this technology is higher than what was deployed.

- **The liar's dividend**: This is the most structurally interesting
  affordance. When any audio or video can be fabricated, real evidence
  loses credibility. Authentic recordings can be dismissed as deepfakes.
  This is an epistemological effect that may not have a precise
  structural parallel — the existence of convincing forgery technology
  degrades the evidentiary value of the medium itself.

**Assessment**: Axiom 4 is **supported** by this material, with a
qualification. The mechanism — fabricated evidence deployed for political
manipulation, timed to prevent rebuttal, distributed through channels
that resist verification — has clear and direct structural parallels
across centuries. The AI-specific affordances change the economics
(cost, speed, scale) and the verisimilitude ceiling, but the structural
pattern is recognizable.

The qualification concerns the liar's dividend. The widespread
availability of convincing synthetic media may produce a qualitative
epistemological shift — not a new mechanism of manipulation, but a new
condition under which all recorded evidence operates. This is closer to
an environmental change (analogous to the printing press transforming
the information environment) than to a novel mechanism of power. The
framework's existing pattern Unprecedented as Sanctuary describes how
new forms of power establish themselves in conceptual and regulatory
voids. The regulatory void around AI-generated synthetic media fits
this pattern precisely — the FCC ruled AI voices in robocalls illegal
only after the NH incident; Slovakia had no legal framework.

The structural parallels are genuine, not forced. The honest assessment
is that Axiom 4 survives this test.

## Findings

1. **The mechanism of fabricated evidence for political manipulation has
   direct pre-AI structural parallels**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: observed
   Forged documents (Protocols of the Elders of Zion, Zinoviev Letter,
   Operation INFEKTION), voice impersonation fraud, election-eve
   disinformation timed to prevent rebuttal, and misleading robocalls
   all predate AI technology. The structural pattern — fabricate
   evidence, deploy at a moment when verification is constrained,
   exploit the gap between appearance and reality — recurs across eras.

2. **AI changes the economics of deployment, not the structure**
   LAYER: Surveillance & Information, Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: inferred
   The Harvard Kennedy School analysis concludes that deepfakes represent
   "new opportunities for negotiating expertise" rather than a
   fundamentally new mechanism. Cost reduction, scalability, and
   verisimilitude are quantitative changes to an existing mechanism.
   This is consistent with Axiom 4's claim that "surface forms differ"
   while "structures rhyme."

3. **The Slovakia deepfakes exploited pre-existing structural conditions**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative, Institutional
   STATUS: observed (per Harvard analysis)
   Low institutional trust, pro-Russian sentiment, established
   disinformation infrastructure since 2010, and conspiracy culture
   predated the deepfakes. The deepfakes were a tool deployed within
   an existing structural environment, not the cause of that environment.

4. **The liar's dividend may represent a qualitative epistemological shift**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative, Surveillance & Information
   STATUS: speculative
   When any recording can be fabricated convincingly, authentic evidence
   loses credibility. This epistemological degradation may not have a
   precise structural parallel — it is an environmental condition
   produced by the technology's existence, not by any specific deployment
   of it. However, analogies exist: the printing press enabled both mass
   information and mass fabrication; photography's credibility was
   undermined by early manipulation techniques.

5. **Regulatory void matches Unprecedented as Sanctuary pattern**
   LAYER: Legal & Regulatory, Institutional
   STATUS: observed
   The FCC ruled AI voices in robocalls illegal only after the NH
   incident. Slovakia had no legal framework for synthetic media during
   the 2023 election. The technology operated in the regulatory void
   the pattern describes — the unprecedented nature of the tool was
   itself a strategic advantage.

6. **Timing exploitation matches Real-Time Narrative Saturation**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative, Surveillance & Information
   STATUS: observed
   The Slovakia deepfakes were released during the media moratorium and
   circulated through encrypted channels (Telegram) where verification
   is structurally difficult. The NH robocalls were sent the day before
   the primary. Both exploit the gap between information velocity and
   verification capacity — a structural feature the Real-Time Narrative
   Saturation pattern describes.

## Implications

Axiom 4 survives its first test. The mechanism of fabricated evidence
for political manipulation has structural parallels spanning centuries.
The AI-specific affordances (cost, speed, scale, verisimilitude) are
real but represent quantitative changes to an established mechanism,
not a qualitatively novel form of power.

The most interesting finding is the liar's dividend — the
epistemological degradation produced by the mere existence of convincing
synthetic media technology. This warrants monitoring as a potential
qualitative shift, though analogies exist in prior technology transitions
(printing press, photography, audio recording). If the liar's dividend
proves to have no structural parallel, it would be the strongest
candidate for an Axiom 4 challenge identified in this analysis.

The analysis also produces the framework's first evidence entry for
Axiom 4 — a support rather than a challenge, but the axiom is no longer
untested.

## Null case

**Null case plausibility: MEDIUM**

The non-power explanation: individual bad actors exploited new technology
for personal or partisan gain. Kramer was a political consultant working
for a rival campaign; the Slovakia actors appear connected to pro-Russian
networks pursuing specific electoral outcomes. This is opportunistic
fraud enabled by accessible technology, not a structural pattern of power.

This explanation is viable for the individual incidents. The framework
adds value by identifying the structural pattern (fabricated evidence
timed to prevent rebuttal, operating in regulatory voids) and connecting
it to centuries of parallel practice. The power explanation and the
bad-actor explanation are not mutually exclusive — the bad actors exploit
structural conditions (regulatory voids, information velocity exceeding
verification capacity, encrypted distribution channels) that the
framework can name.

**Null case outcome: plausible.** The individual incidents can be
explained as isolated bad actors. The structural analysis adds
genuine value but does not decisively surpass the simpler explanation.
