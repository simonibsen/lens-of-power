## Narrative

Roy delivered remarks at a New Delhi literary event on 10 March 2026, pivoting from her memoir *Mother Mary Comes To Me* to the US-Israeli military operations against Iran. The speech was published exclusively on Zeteo by Mehdi Hasan on 11 March 2026. It runs approximately 600 words and addresses three subjects: the US-Israeli attack on Iran, India's foreign policy response, and India's cultural self-image.

Roy characterises the US-Israeli bombing of Tehran, Isfahan, and Beirut as "unprovoked and illegal" and a "continuation of the US-Israeli genocide in Gaza." She identifies a repeated operational template: "Murdering women and children. Bombing hospitals. Carpet bombing cities. And then playing the victim." The template observation maps to Serial Regime Change — the same military intervention structure applied across different targets, each time presented as unique. Roy's claim that "Iran is not Gaza" marks the scalar distinction: the same template applied to a state with nuclear-adjacent capability and regional military capacity changes the risk calculus from regional to global.

The speech's most structurally productive section concerns India. Roy catalogues a sequence of Indian government actions: the PM's visit to Netanyahu days before the Iran attack, a trade deal she describes as "groveling" that "sells our farmers and textile industry down the river," the dispatch of Indian workers to Israel as replacement labour for expelled Palestinian workers, those workers' exclusion from bomb shelters, the US granting India "permission" to buy Russian oil, and daily public mockery by US politicians including Trump — to which the PM responds with "his famous, vacuous laugh." Individually, each item admits a pragmatic foreign policy explanation. Taken as a sequence, they describe a structural position: India is performing compliance before demands are made, absorbing public humiliation as a condition of the relationship, and sacrificing its own citizens' safety for the alignment. The sequence matches Anticipatory Obedience — populations self-adjusting their behaviour to what they anticipate power will demand, before any demand is made. Roy's pattern note is the rhetorical escalation from trade concessions to "permission to go to the bathroom," which traces the ratchet mechanism the pattern predicts: each act of pre-compliance shifts the baseline, making the next concession more extreme and more normalised.

The speech explicitly names what it sees. Roy's label — "Running Dog of Imperialism" — is a Cold War Maoist term redirected at India's current government. The term fills a gap: India's contemporary political vocabulary does not contain a word for voluntary subordination to imperial power, because the vocabulary has been shaped to make that position unnameable. Roy reclaims a foreign-language political term to name what domestic language cannot express. This is Axiom 6 operating in reverse: the provision of missing language as counter-control.

Roy contrasts India's foreign policy position with its cultural self-image, describing "twisted, toxic movies" in which "celluloid heroes strut on, winning phantom war after war." The cinematic self-image functions as the appearance that makes the structural subordination sustainable: the population consumes the appearance of sovereignty while the government enacts dependence. This maps to Appearance as Structural Terrain — the gap between how a system presents itself and how it operates is the operating environment power depends on. The appearance is not incidental; it is structurally necessary because a population aware of its actual position would resist it.

The speech does not trace the material infrastructure of India's subordination. It does not name the arms procurement dependencies, technology transfer arrangements, energy security calculations, or market access conditions that make the Indian government's behaviour structurally rational. This is consistent with its form — a 600-word intervention at a literary event — but it means the analysis identifies subordination at the narrative and institutional layers while leaving the economic and surveillance layers that produce the subordination unexamined.

## So What

If India's behaviour fits the Anticipatory Obedience pattern at inter-state level, this extends a pattern previously documented only in domestic contexts (Snyder: individual citizens under emerging authoritarianism, Project 2025: institutional personnel anticipating executive demands). The extension is structurally significant: it suggests the mechanism operates at any scale where a power asymmetry exists and the weaker party calculates that pre-compliance is safer than independence. The ratchet mechanism — each act of compliance shifting the baseline — is observable in the sequence Roy describes. The monitor is whether India's concessions escalate in scope through 2026.

Roy's speech is itself a circumvention attempt. Its platform (literary event), its register (moral outrage from a Booker Prize winner), and its distribution channel (Zeteo, an independent media outlet founded by Mehdi Hasan) position it outside the institutions it critiques. Whether it generates structural effects beyond the speech act depends on whether the hidden transcript it surfaces — "Running Dog of Imperialism" — enters Indian public discourse. The test is replication: does the label propagate?

## Null case

**Initial null case (stated before analysis):** India's foreign policy reflects pragmatic cost-benefit calculation by a mid-sized power navigating between great powers. The PM's Netanyahu visit was pre-scheduled diplomacy. The trade deal reflects asymmetric bargaining power, not subordination. The worker shelter issue is a local Israeli civil defence failure, not a structural signal. Roy is an oppositional activist who frames all government action as capitulation.

**Assessment:** The null case is partially weakened but retains force for individual components. The PM's visit timing could be coincidental. The trade deal may reflect normal asymmetric bargaining. The shelter exclusion may be a local civil defence failure. However, the null case does not explain the *sequence*: why pragmatic calculation consistently produces outcomes that involve public humiliation of India (US politicians mocking India while India complies, workers denied shelter while serving Israeli interests, "permission" rhetoric). Pragmatic alignment does not require the aligned party to be publicly degraded. The pattern of humiliation-plus-compliance is more consistent with Anticipatory Obedience than with sovereign strategic calculation. The null case is weakened at the configurational level but retained at the component level.

**Null case outcome: plausible** (MEDIUM at component level; MEDIUM-LOW at configurational level — the humiliation-plus-compliance pattern is not explained by pragmatic calculation alone).
