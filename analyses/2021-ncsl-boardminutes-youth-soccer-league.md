## Narrative

On November 15, 2021, the National Capital Soccer League's Board of
Directors held a routine meeting via Zoom. Forty-eight member clubs
attended; eight were absent. The agenda covered four items: approval of
prior meeting minutes, a new club membership application from Brookland
FC, a guest presentation on referee training and retention, and
administrative reports from league staff.

The meeting followed standard parliamentary procedure. Minutes from the
prior meeting were approved by acclamation. Brookland FC's executive
director presented the club's background (founded July 2020, seven
teams, strong recreational program, pursuing nonprofit status) and
answered questions about fields, positioning among DC clubs, and team
readiness. Following discussion, the board voted to admit Brookland FC
by Zoom poll.

Pat Delaney, Chairman of the Metro DC-Virginia State Referee Program,
presented on referee grade nomenclature changes, registration system
updates, retention initiatives (mentorship programs), and training
opportunities. US Soccer had eliminated "Instructor" and "Assessor"
titles, replacing them with "Mentor" or "Coach." Certification courses
were available through Maryland and West Virginia state associations.

Administrative reports covered: a college combine scheduled for November
28; winter futsal registration closing November 21; discontinuation of
division awards due to limited interest and significant costs; spring
season application deadlines (January 24); and a request for fall season
sit-out form submissions.

The meeting adjourned at 9:20 p.m., approximately 1 hour 45 minutes
after being called to order.
