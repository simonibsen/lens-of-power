## Briefing: Meta's Three Governing Policies as a Unified Control Apparatus

DATE: 2026-03-01
SOURCE TYPE: Corporate policy documents (Terms of Service, Privacy Policy,
Community Standards)
SOURCE POSITION: Position 1 — Ruler / Operator

### Key findings

1. Meta's three policy documents are not independent legal instruments but
   a unified governance apparatus that establishes authority (Terms of
   Service), authorizes extraction (Privacy Policy), and governs discourse
   (Community Standards) for approximately one-third of the world's
   population. Each document's stated purpose inverts its actual function.

2. The January 2025 moderation changes — eliminating fact-checking, rolling
   back hate-speech restrictions, replacing professional moderation with
   unpaid Community Notes — are undermining propaganda per Stanley's
   typology: the ideal of free expression invoked to degrade the
   conditions free expression requires. The inauguration-adjacent timing
   suggests political repositioning rather than intellectual conviction.

3. The "accept or leave" binary at the core of the Terms of Service is a
   manufactured choice. When Meta is embedded in social, professional, and
   civic infrastructure — and is the de facto internet in much of the
   Global South — leaving is not a meaningful alternative. The choice is
   the appearance; the structural dependency is the reality.

### Who benefits / who bears cost

| Actor | Gains | At whose expense |
|-------|-------|------------------|
| Meta (shareholders, Zuckerberg) | ~$160B annual revenue from behavioral data; unilateral content licensing including AI training rights; reduced moderation costs; political alignment with incoming administration | ~3.3 billion users whose data is extracted, content is licensed, and speech is governed without representation |
| Advertising clients | Precision-targeted behavioral advertising based on comprehensive surveillance | Users whose behavior is predicted and modified without informed consent |
| Incoming US administration (2025) | Reduced content moderation on politically favorable topics; platform aligned with political agenda | Marginalized communities who relied on hate-speech enforcement; users in authoritarian contexts |
| Community Notes contributors | Perceived civic participation; sense of agency | Their labor is unpaid; Meta retains all data and revenue; they absorb the cost of moderation previously borne by professionals |

### What is absent

- The voice of ~3 billion users in the drafting of the terms that govern them
- Evidence supporting "too many mistakes" — the stated rationale for removing fact-checking
- The experience of users in the Global South where Meta is primary internet infrastructure
- Any alternative to the binary "accept or leave" — no negotiated terms, no collective bargaining, no tiered consent
- The economic analysis of the moderation rollback

### Findings

1. **The policies compose a unified governance apparatus**
   LAYER: All non-Physical layers
   STATUS: inferred
   The three documents are not independent — they operate as mutually reinforcing components of a single system. The Terms of Service establish legal authority and extract content rights. The Privacy Policy authorizes behavioral surveillance and data extraction. The Community Standards govern permissible discourse. Together they constitute a governance framework whose subjects have no vote, no representation, and no meaningful capacity to refuse. The coherence of the system is the finding — each document enables the others.

2. **The Terms of Service function as a unilateral contract of adhesion, not a mutual agreement**
   LAYER: Legal & Regulatory, Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: observed
   The Terms present unilateral conditions as bilateral agreement. The user "agrees" by using the platform. The terms are retroactively applied to existing content. They bind non-users who view content through shared links. Meta may deny opt-out requests for AI training of user content. The binary "accept or leave" eliminates negotiation. When Meta is embedded in social, professional, and civic infrastructure, the "leave" option is structurally coercive for most users.

3. **The Privacy Policy authorizes comprehensive behavioral surveillance while using the language of user empowerment**
   LAYER: Surveillance & Information, Thought & Narrative, Economic
   STATUS: observed (data collection) / inferred (the framing as empowerment is inversion)
   Meta's tracking pixel is embedded on ~30% of the world's top websites. Data is harvested from non-users. Hospital patient information has been transmitted through the pixel. Offline purchase data is collected. AI conversations are stored and used for ad targeting. This is described as "personalized experience," "transparency," and "control." The gap between the extraction apparatus and its description is the Appearance as Structural Terrain pattern in operation.

4. **The January 2025 moderation changes are undermining propaganda — invoking free expression to degrade the conditions it requires**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative, Institutional
   STATUS: inferred
   Zuckerberg's "More Speech and Fewer Mistakes" announcement invokes free expression values while removing the infrastructure informed public discourse requires: professional fact-checking eliminated, proactive moderation of non-severe content ended, restrictions on political hate speech removed. This is undermining propaganda per the framework's typology: the ideal invoked in a way that degrades the conditions the ideal requires. The inauguration-adjacent timing suggests political repositioning, though the structural function is the same whether the framing is sincere or strategic.

5. **Community Notes transfers moderation costs to unpaid users while Meta retains all revenue**
   LAYER: Economic, Institutional
   STATUS: inferred
   The shift from professional fact-checking to Community Notes presents as democratization of epistemic authority. Its observable economic effect is the transfer of moderation labor from paid professionals to unpaid volunteers, while Meta retains 100% of advertising revenue. The structural parallel to Fanon's relay class is suggestive: a population performing governance labor under Meta's terms, serving Meta's interests while believing they serve their own community.

6. **The opacity of the documents is functional, not incidental**
   LAYER: Surveillance & Information, Legal & Regulatory
   STATUS: inferred
   Facebook.com blocks automated retrieval of its own policies. The Terms of Service are written at a length and complexity that precludes user comprehension. The Privacy Policy describes a surveillance apparatus in language designed to reassure rather than inform. Whether this opacity is deliberate or institutional artifact, its function is the same: it prevents the informed scrutiny that accountability requires.

7. **Network effects make "accept or leave" a manufactured binary**
   LAYER: Economic, Institutional
   STATUS: inferred
   The Terms present a choice: accept or leave. When Meta's platforms are embedded in social networks, professional contacts, community organizing, news distribution, and — in many countries — basic internet access, leaving is not a meaningful alternative. The choice is the appearance; the structural dependency is the reality. This is Axiom 9 — compliance manufactured without explicit coercion through the engineering of dependency.

## Narrative

Meta's three policy documents — Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, and
Community Standards — present themselves as independent legal instruments
serving distinct functions: contractual agreement, data protection, and
content governance. Examined together through the framework's taxonomy,
they compose a unified governance apparatus operating across five of six
power layers simultaneously, with each document's stated purpose inverting
its actual function.

The Terms of Service describe themselves as an "agreement" while
eliminating the conditions agreement requires: negotiation, informed
consent, and the capacity to refuse. The Privacy Policy frames
comprehensive behavioral surveillance — cross-platform tracking, a pixel
embedded on 30% of the world's top websites, offline data collection,
non-user monitoring — as "personalized experience" and "transparency."
The Community Standards present corporate governance of planetary-scale
discourse as "community" values. Each document's language performs the
opposite of its function. This is the Inversion of Stated Purpose
pattern in its most comprehensively documented instance to date,
operating simultaneously through legal, narrative, economic,
institutional, and surveillance layers.

The January 2025 moderation changes — announced days before Trump's
inauguration — represent a qualitative shift within this apparatus. Meta
eliminated third-party fact-checking, replaced it with unpaid user-driven
"Community Notes," removed restrictions on political discourse topics
including immigration and gender identity, and refocused automated
moderation exclusively on "high severity" violations. Zuckerberg framed
the changes as "more speech and fewer mistakes," invoking free expression
values while dismantling the institutional infrastructure those values
depend on. This is undermining propaganda as described by Stanley:
the ideal used to degrade the conditions the ideal requires. The
"cultural tipping point" language and inauguration-adjacent timing
suggest political repositioning rather than intellectual conviction,
though the sincerity of the framing is less important than its structural
function — whether sincere or strategic, the observable effect is the
reduction of accountability infrastructure.

The system's most structurally significant feature may be the
manufactured binary at its core: accept or leave. When Meta is embedded
in social networks, professional contacts, community organizing, news
distribution, marketplace activity, and — in much of the Global South —
basic internet access, the "leave" option is not a meaningful
alternative. The choice is the appearance; the structural dependency is
the reality. Approximately one-third of the world's population is
governed by terms they did not write, cannot modify, and in practice
cannot refuse, drafted by a corporation whose revenue depends on the
behavioral data extracted from that population.

## Step 1: DECOMPOSE

### Claims — Terms of Service (effective January 1, 2025)

- Continued use of the platform constitutes acceptance of terms (stated as: fact)
- Meta receives a broad license to "use, distribute, share, copy, sell" all user content "in whole or in part, in any way it wants" (stated as: fact)
- This license extends to private messages (stated as: fact)
- The license extends to AI training; Meta may deny user opt-out requests (stated as: fact)
- Terms bind non-users who merely view content through shared links (stated as: fact)
- Previously posted content is retroactively subject to new terms (stated as: fact)
- Users who disagree must stop using the platform (stated as: fact — binary framing)
- Data scraping by third parties is prohibited; data collection by Meta is authorized (stated as: fact — asymmetry)

### Claims — Privacy Policy (2025 updates)

- Meta collects data across its platforms, across the web via tracking pixel (embedded on ~30% of top websites), and from offline sources (stated as: fact)
- Data from non-users is collected, including contact details and browsing history (stated as: fact)
- AI conversation data may be used for personalization and ad targeting (stated as: fact)
- Conversations about sensitive topics (health, religion, politics) won't be used for ads but "may still be stored or used internally" (stated as: fact)
- Users have "more control" and "more transparency" over their data (stated as: benefit)
- Third-party sharing rules are "tightened" (stated as: improvement)

### Claims — Community Standards (with January 2025 changes)

- Four principles: Authenticity, Safety, Privacy, Dignity (stated as: values)
- Third-party fact-checking program ended; replaced with "Community Notes" (stated as: policy)
- Restrictions on "mainstream political discourse" topics removed, including immigration and gender identity (stated as: free speech expansion)
- Automated moderation refocused exclusively on "high severity violations" (terrorism, CSAM, fraud); user reports now required for other violations (stated as: improvement)
- Previous moderation approach "too often became a tool to censor" and resulted in "too many mistakes" (stated as: fact — unsupported by data)
- Trust & safety team relocated from California to Texas (stated as: fact)
- Zuckerberg: the 2024 election was a "cultural tipping point" (stated as: rationale)

### Framing

Three distinct registers deployed in a coordinated pattern:
- *Terms of Service*: Bureaucratic-legal neutrality. Presents unilateral terms as bilateral agreement. Designed to produce acceptance through inertia, not comprehension.
- *Privacy Policy*: Consumer empowerment. Extraction apparatus described in the language of user benefit — "personalized experience," "transparency," "control."
- *Community Standards announcement*: Liberation narrative. Rolling back institutional infrastructure framed as expanding freedom. "More speech" is the headline; less accountability is the function.

### Emotional register

- Terms of Service: Intentional flatness — the absence of emotion is the mechanism. Legal language anesthetizes evaluation.
- Privacy Policy: Reassurance — "you're in control."
- Community Standards announcement: Exhilaration — "more speech," ending "censorship," a "cultural tipping point."

### Propaganda classification

(per instruments/propaganda-typology.md)

- Terms of Service: Integration / political / vertical / rational / undermining. Invokes "agreement" while eliminating user agency.
- Privacy Policy: Integration / political / vertical / rational / undermining. Invokes "control" and "transparency" while expanding extraction.
- Community Standards announcement: Compound — agitation (framing previous moderation as "censorship") + integration (normalizing reduced oversight as freedom) / political / vertical / rational+irrational (free speech appeal operates at both levels) / undermining. Invokes free expression to reduce the infrastructure of accountability.

The compound type on the Community Standards — agitation + integration, rational + irrational, undermining — is a structurally significant multi-axis propaganda formation.

### Newspeak detections

(per instruments/newspeak-checklist.md)

- **Euphemistic substitution**: Surveillance → "personalized experience." Data extraction → "transparency." Content licensing → "agreement." Removing fact-checking → "more speech." Behavioral prediction → "relevant advertising."
- **Redefinition**: "Consent" = continued use of platform. "Agreement" = unilateral terms imposed on a population with no negotiating power. "Community" notes = unpaid labor by users under Meta's terms. "Censorship" = professional fact-checking.
- **Complexity as obscurity**: Terms of Service designed at a length and reading level that no reasonable person reads. The complexity functions to prevent scrutiny (checklist item #5 precisely describes this).
- **Binary framing**: Accept or leave. "Censorship" vs. "free speech" with no middle ground. No option for negotiated terms, partial consent, or collective bargaining.
- **Forced affirmation**: Continued use = acceptance. Cannot participate without affirming the terms.

Compound signal: Euphemism + Redefinition + Complexity + Binary framing operating simultaneously across the Terms of Service. Per the instrument: "Compound linguistic manipulation is rarely accidental."
