## Narrative

The material is 122 pages of Santa Barbara City Council minutes spanning
nine regular meetings and seven special/closed sessions from May through
August 2018. The document contains approximately 150 agenda items covering
municipal governance: infrastructure contracts (bridge replacements,
wastewater treatment, fire alerting systems), budget hearings (FY 2019
operating and capital budget), lease agreements (Boys and Girls Club,
airport tenants, Paseo Nuevo), environmental regulation (polystyrene food
containers, plastic straws), charter amendments (district elections under
the California Voting Rights Act, even-numbered year elections), advisory
group appointments, investment reports, employee recognition, and
litigation management.

The proceedings follow a consistent structure: staff recommendation, cited
documents, public comment period, named speakers, formal motion, recorded
vote with roll call. Recusals are documented with stated reasons
(Councilmember Sneddon recusing from the fire assessment district due to
homeownership in the district; City Attorney Calonne recusing for the
same reason). Dissenting votes are named. Motions are amended through
identified procedural steps. Closed sessions follow Government Code
citation requirements.

Two items have power-dynamics dimensions that the framework can identify.
The sit-lie ordinance extension (SBMC Chapter 9.97) to the 00-100 block
of East Haley Street criminalizes sitting or lying on specific sidewalks,
a measure that disproportionately affects unhoused populations. The item
was processed through public comment (two speakers), committee referral,
and a unanimous vote. The district elections charter amendment under the
California Voting Rights Act represents a structural change in
representation — shifting from at-large to district elections, with
procedural disagreements visible in split votes and withdrawn amendments.
Both items proceeded through the full democratic process with public
participation, documented deliberation, and recorded votes.

The power explanation does not surpass the null case for this material.
The document records a municipal government conducting routine business
through established democratic procedures. The framework's analytical
apparatus can identify power-dynamics dimensions in specific items (the
sit-lie ordinance, the district elections process, the Community Choice
Energy feasibility study, the library funding dispute with the county),
but these dimensions are visible to participants, processed through
transparent procedures, and subject to public scrutiny. This is a
functional institution doing what it claims to do.

## Findings

1. **Sit-lie ordinance extension targets unhoused populations**
   LAYER: Legal & Regulatory, Physical & Coercive
   STATUS: observed
   The council extended SBMC Chapter 9.97 to prohibit sitting or lying on
   the sidewalk along the 00-100 block of East Haley Street. The ordinance
   criminalizes a basic physical activity associated with houselessness.
   The vote was unanimous. Two members of the public spoke.

2. **District elections transition follows external legal mandate**
   LAYER: Institutional, Legal & Regulatory
   STATUS: observed
   The charter amendment process to implement district elections was
   responsive to the California Voting Rights Act, not initiated by the
   council. Procedural disagreements (split votes on ballot measure format,
   withdrawn amendments on vacancy provisions) are visible in the record.
   The deliberation appears genuine — not performative.

3. **Recusals and dissent are documented transparently**
   LAYER: Institutional
   STATUS: observed
   Multiple recusals with stated reasons (homeownership in assessed
   district), named dissenting votes (Councilmember Dominguez on library
   funding, Councilmember Rowse on plastic straw regulation), and
   withdrawn motions are recorded. This is the documentation structure
   of an institution operating within its stated norms.

## Implications

The framework correctly identifies power-dynamics dimensions in specific
items (the sit-lie ordinance, the district elections shift) but these
operate at the level of individual policy choices within a functional
democratic process, not at the level of structural concealment or
institutional capture. The sit-lie ordinance is the clearest instance of
power operating through law to manage a population — but it was enacted
through transparent procedure with public participation.

This analysis demonstrates that the framework can distinguish between
material where power dynamics are the primary explanation and material
where they are a secondary dimension within routine governance. The null
case wins for the document as a whole.

adopting ordinances, holding public hearings, processing budget items, and

add analytical value for the document as a whole. The framework finds
