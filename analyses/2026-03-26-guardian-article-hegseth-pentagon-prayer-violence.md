## Narrative

Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth led a prayer service at the Pentagon on March 26 — his first since the Iran war began — in which he prayed for "overwhelming violence of action against those who deserve no mercy" and that "every round find its mark against the enemies of righteousness." He attributed the prayer to a military chaplain who delivered it after the capture of Venezuela's Maduro. The prayer service was held before military and civilian Pentagon workers. All monthly prayer services since their inception in May 2025 have been presided over by evangelicals; CREC pastors have appeared at least three times.

The previous day, Hegseth announced structural changes to the military chaplain corps: reducing religious affiliation codes from approximately 200 to 31, replacing officer rank insignia with religious insignia on chaplain uniforms, and characterising the existing corps as "infected by political correctness and secular humanism" and "watered down" to "nothing more than therapists." The official DoD framing presents the insignia change as enabling junior personnel to approach chaplains more comfortably; the structural effect is to invert the chaplain's institutional identity from military officer with a religious function to religious figure within a military setting.

In February, Hegseth invited Doug Wilson — founder of CREC, self-described Christian nationalist, advocate for repealing women's suffrage, and proponent of criminalising homosexuality — to deliver a 15-minute sermon at the Pentagon, broadcast on the department's internal TV network. Wilson compared the Pentagon prayer services to the Day of Pentecost. Hegseth is a member of a Wilson-affiliated church in Tennessee and moved there to enrol his children in a school within Wilson's Christian educational network.

The prayer's language performs a specific function: it converts a geopolitical war (the Iran conflict) into a cosmic struggle between righteousness and its enemies. A policy choice that can be debated is reframed as a divine mandate that cannot be questioned. The institutional infrastructure — monthly services, chaplain reforms, internal broadcast — makes this reframing durable rather than episodic. Americans United for Separation of Church and State filed FOIA lawsuits against both the Defence and Labour departments seeking records of the services, including any complaints from employees. Rachel Laser identified the coercive dynamic: "there is pressure on federal employees to attend in order to appease their bosses — especially since these services occur amidst the Trump administration's campaign to punish anyone who doesn't comply."

## Null case

**Initial null case**: Hegseth is a sincere Christian expressing his faith in his workplace. Military leaders have invoked God before battle throughout US history. The prayer services are voluntary. The chaplain reforms are administrative streamlining — 82% of religious service members identify with six categories, making 200 codes impractical. The insignia change helps junior personnel approach chaplains more comfortably.

**Assessment**: The null case accounts for individual prayer and the insignia rationale in isolation. It does not account for the configuration: (1) all presiding figures are evangelical despite the military's religious diversity; (2) the defence secretary's personal pastor — an advocate for Christian theocracy and repealing women's suffrage — delivers sermons broadcast on DoD internal TV; (3) the reduction from 200 to 31 codes occurs simultaneously with the characterisation of the existing system as "infected"; (4) the prayer services operate during a period of mass federal terminations, creating the coercive dynamic Laser identifies; (5) the pattern is spreading to other departments (Labor); (6) the prayer content explicitly merges religious mandate with wartime violence. The individual components might each be explained by personal faith, administrative efficiency, or military tradition. The configuration — the simultaneous restructuring of institutional infrastructure, personnel, and narrative toward a single theological tradition during wartime — is not explained by the null case.

**Null case plausibility**: MEDIUM for individual components (sincere faith, administrative streamlining). LOW for the configuration (the simultaneity, the Wilson connection, the exclusively evangelical presiding, the wartime framing, the cross-department spread, and the coercive attendance context collectively exceed what personal faith or administrative logic explains).
