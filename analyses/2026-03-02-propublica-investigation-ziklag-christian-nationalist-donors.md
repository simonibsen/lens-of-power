### Key finding

Ziklag's internal documents constitute a primary-source blueprint for
deliberate, multi-layer institutional capture. The Seven Mountain Mandate
maps 1:1 onto the framework's taxonomy of power layers, and the
organization's three 2024 operations (Checkmate, Steeplechase, Watchtower)
demonstrate how Economic, Legal & Regulatory, Institutional, Thought &
Narrative, and Surveillance & Information layers are coordinated through a
single strategic framework — all sheltered by a 501(c)(3) charity
designation that provides donor anonymity and tax-deductible contributions.

### Findings

1. **The Seven Mountain Mandate as articulated Layered Redundancy**
   LAYER: All six
   STATUS: observed
   Ziklag's internal documents describe a 30-year plan to "take dominion"
   over seven societal sectors (arts/media, business, church, education,
   family, government, science/technology). This maps directly to the
   framework's taxonomy: education = Thought & Narrative, government =
   Institutional + Legal & Regulatory, media = Thought & Narrative +
   Surveillance & Information, business = Economic. This is the first
   material in the framework's corpus where Layered Redundancy is not
   analytically inferred but *articulated as strategy* by the actors
   themselves.

2. **501(c)(3) as structural concealment**
   LAYER: Legal & Regulatory, Surveillance & Information, Economic
   STATUS: observed
   The charity designation provides three simultaneous benefits: donor
   anonymity (Legal & Regulatory shields Surveillance & Information),
   tax deductions (Economic subsidy from public), and reputational
   "halo effect" (Thought & Narrative). Six independent legal experts
   confirmed the political activity is inconsistent with 501(c)(3)
   status. The organization's general counsel responded to ProPublica's
   findings with a non-denial denial: "some of the statements in your
   email are correct. Others are not," then declined to specify which.

3. **Operation Checkmate: voter purging as "election integrity"**
   LAYER: Legal & Regulatory, Surveillance & Information
   STATUS: observed
   Ziklag plans to invest $800K in EagleAI to remove "up to one million
   ineligible registrations and around 280,000 ineligible voters" in
   Arizona, Nevada, Georgia, and Wisconsin. The Arizona target —
   "secure 10,640 additional unique votes" — mirrors Biden's 2020
   margin of 10,447 to the nearest hundred. This is documented in
   internal strategy documents, not inferred. The stated language of
   "integrity" and "ineligible" frames voter removal as maintenance
   rather than suppression.

4. **Operation Watchtower: anti-trans messaging as voter turnout mechanism**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: observed
   Internal recordings show Wallnau explicitly framing anti-trans ballot
   initiatives as an electoral strategy: "People that are lethargic about
   the election or, worse yet, they're gonna be all Trump-traumatized
   with the news cycle — this issue will get people to come out and
   vote." The anti-trans position is instrumentalized — its political
   function is voter mobilization, regardless of participants' sincerity
   about the issue itself. Ziklag planned initiatives in seven states
   selected for competitive races, not for trans population density.

5. **Operation Steeplechase: churches as political mobilization infrastructure**
   LAYER: Institutional, Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: observed
   Ziklag coordinates church voter mobilization through partnerships with
   Turning Point USA's faith division, Ralph Reed's Faith and Freedom
   Coalition, and the America First Policy Institute. Internal documents
   describe "extensive pastor and church outreach through pastor summits,
   church-focused messaging and events." This converts religious
   institutional infrastructure into political mobilization apparatus —
   the Institutional layer (church authority) is leveraged for Thought &
   Narrative control (voter direction).

6. **The Koch model, Christianized**
   LAYER: Economic, Institutional
   STATUS: observed (founding narrative) + inferred (structural parallel)
   Ziklag's founder Ken Eldred explicitly conceived the organization as
   "a donor network like the one created by Charles and David Koch but
   for Christians." The organizational model — confidential high-net-worth
   donor network funding coordinated multi-front institutional campaigns —
   replicates the infrastructure documented in the Powell Memo extraction
   and Hartmann's analysis. Revenue growth from $1.3M (2018) to $12M
   (2022) demonstrates successful scaling.

7. **Education mountain: articulated institutional destruction**
   LAYER: Institutional, Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: observed
   Peter Bohlinger, Ziklag's education mountain chair, stated the goal
   "is to take down the education system as we know it today." This
   connects directly to the DoE Kirk banner analysis (2026-03-02): Kirk
   spoke at Ziklag events, receives Ziklag funding through Turning Point
   USA, and is now honored by the federal education institution his
   funder plans to dismantle. The education mountain strategy encompasses
   promotion of homeschooling as "fundamental right" and opposition to
   public education as a system.

8. **IRS institutional abdication**
   LAYER: Institutional, Legal & Regulatory
   STATUS: inferred
   Six legal experts independently concluded Ziklag's activities test or
   violate 501(c)(3) law. A former IRS exempt-organizations director
   said "they're planning an election effort — that's not a 501(c)(3)
   activity." The IRS did not respond to ProPublica's questions. The
   observable pattern is institutional non-enforcement — the regulatory
   body responsible for the legal boundary is absent. Whether this
   represents resource constraints, political pressure, or deliberate
   non-action cannot be determined from available evidence.

9. **"Spiritual warfare" as mobilization framing**
   LAYER: Thought & Narrative
   STATUS: observed
   Ziklag's internal communications consistently frame political activity
   in spiritual terms: "spiritual battle," "powers of darkness," "evil
   force," "tyranny," "the kingdom." Executive Director Drew Hiss
   described the political opposition as "a controlling, evil, diabolical
   presence, really, with tyranny in mind." This framing performs
   multiple functions: it elevates political disagreement to cosmic
   conflict (making compromise impossible), transforms partisan activity
   into religious duty (making non-participation a moral failure), and
   places political opponents outside the category of legitimate
   interlocutors (they are not wrong — they are evil).

10. **Barrett confirmation: claimed judicial capture**
    LAYER: Institutional, Legal & Regulatory
    STATUS: observed (the claim) + inferred (its significance)
    Ziklag's internal newsletter claims that "a coalition of groups it
    assembled played a hugely significant role in the selection, hearings
    and confirmation process" of Amy Coney Barrett to the Supreme Court.
    If accurate, this connects Ziklag to the judicial capture pipeline
    documented in Whitehouse's *The Scheme* — the same network of
    dark money, orchestrated amicus briefs, and manufactured cases that
    Whitehouse traces from donor to docket.

11. **The sincerity problem**
    LAYER: Thought & Narrative
    STATUS: inferred
    The strongest version of the null case is that participants are
    genuinely motivated by religious conviction. Stanley's sincerity
    problem (principles/stanley-how-propaganda-works.md) is directly
    relevant: undermining propaganda is most effective when the speaker
    sincerely believes in the ideal being invoked. The participants'
    sincerity does not change the structural function of the operation —
    genuine belief that voter purging serves "integrity" does not make it
    less of a purge — but it does make the operation more resistant to
    exposure, because exposure of "hypocrisy" fails when there is no
    hypocrisy to expose.

12. **Donor anonymity as Division of Learning**
    LAYER: Surveillance & Information, Economic
    STATUS: observed
    Ziklag is "a private, confidential, invitation-only community" with a
    $25M+ net worth threshold. Donors are shielded by 501(c)(3) law from
    public disclosure. The operational asymmetry is structural: Ziklag
    uses EagleAI to profile millions of voters while the public cannot
    identify who funds the profiling. This is the Division of Learning
    applied to political infrastructure — those who can see cannot be
    seen; those who are profiled cannot see who profiles them.

### Null case

**The non-power explanation**: This is a group of sincere religious
donors exercising First Amendment rights to fund causes they believe
in, operating within the ambiguous gray areas of tax-exempt law that
many organizations test. The "conspiracy" reading is overblown — this
is standard political organizing dressed in religious language.

**Assessment**: LOW for the overall operation; MEDIUM for individual
components.

The null case fails for the overall operation because the organization's
own internal documents articulate electoral objectives in unambiguous
terms: "The goal is to win." "If 75,000 people wins the White House,
then how do we get 150,000 people so we make sure we win?" "This ballot
initiative can deliver swing states." Six independent legal experts
confirmed this violates 501(c)(3) law. The secrecy contradicts the
First Amendment defense — legitimate civic participation does not require
confidential, invitation-only operation.

The null case has partial merit for individual components: religious
conviction is likely genuine for most participants; some voter roll
maintenance is legitimate; opposition to specific education policies
is a valid political position; the 501(c)(3) boundary is genuinely
unclear for some activities. These partial merits do not explain the
overall structure — the coordination, the electoral targeting, the
secrecy, and the 30-year institutional capture plan.

## Narrative

ProPublica and Documented obtained thousands of internal documents from
Ziklag, a 501(c)(3) charity whose members include ultra-wealthy Christian
families (net worth threshold: $25 million). The documents describe a
30-year strategic plan organized around the Seven Mountain Mandate — a
theological framework for establishing Christian dominion over seven
societal sectors: arts and media, business, church, education, family,
government, and science and technology.

For the 2024 election cycle, Ziklag organized three operations. Operation
Checkmate funds "election integrity" groups, including $800,000 for
EagleAI to mass-challenge voter registrations in Arizona, Nevada,
Georgia, and Wisconsin. The Arizona target — 10,640 additional votes —
matches Biden's 2020 margin to the nearest hundred. Operation
Steeplechase mobilizes church congregations through partnerships with
Turning Point USA, the Faith and Freedom Coalition, and the America
First Policy Institute. Operation Watchtower funds anti-transgender
ballot initiatives in seven states selected for competitive races; in
internal recordings, Ziklag's spiritual adviser describes the anti-trans
message as a "wedge issue" to motivate voters "lethargic about the
election."

The entire operation is structured as a tax-exempt charity. Donors
receive tax deductions and anonymity. The organization's internal
rhetoric frames political opposition as a "spiritual battle" against
"powers of darkness." Six independent legal experts reviewed the
materials and concluded the activities test or violate the law governing
501(c)(3) organizations. The IRS did not respond to questions.

The analysis identifies five primary layers of power operating
simultaneously: Thought & Narrative (Seven Mountain cultural capture,
spiritual warfare framing, anti-trans messaging strategy), Economic
(ultra-wealthy donor funding, tax-exempt subsidy), Legal & Regulatory
(501(c)(3) exploitation, voter roll challenges, ballot initiatives),
Institutional (church mobilization, judicial capture, education system
targeting), and Surveillance & Information (EagleAI voter profiling,
donor anonymity). Physical & Coercive power is active but absent from
the source — voter disenfranchisement is a form of coercion, and the
political coalition Ziklag funds includes the infrastructure for
enforcement operations documented in other analyses.

The structural significance of this material is that Layered Redundancy
— a pattern the framework ordinarily identifies through analytical
inference — is here articulated as explicit strategy by the actors
themselves. The Seven Mountain Mandate is a documented blueprint for
operating across all societal layers simultaneously, and the three
named operations demonstrate how that blueprint translates to
coordinated action.

The organization's founder explicitly modeled it on the Koch donor
network. Its funding recipients include Alliance Defending Freedom
(connected to the judicial capture pipeline documented in Whitehouse's
*The Scheme*) and Turning Point USA (whose leader Charlie Kirk is
now honored by the Department of Education, as documented in the
Kirk banner analysis). The connections suggest this is not an isolated
organization but a node in a larger infrastructure of funded
institutional capture that the framework has been documenting across
multiple independent analyses and sources.

   the Christian nationalist movement.

Amendment rights to fund causes they believe in, operating within the

"conspiracy" reading is overblown — this is standard political
organizing dressed in religious language, no different structurally from
liberal donor networks.

individual components.
