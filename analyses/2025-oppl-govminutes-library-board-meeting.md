## Narrative

The material is a 79-page meeting packet for the Oak Park Public Library Board of Library Trustees regular meeting on October 28, 2025. The packet includes the agenda, minutes from four prior meetings (Finance Committee, Strategic Planning, Regular Board, Governance Committee), a budget memorandum with two draft budgets (Draft 2 and Draft 3 for FY2026), a confidentiality of circulation records policy, the executive director's report, staff reports (strategic priorities, core use statistics, additions and separations, staff changes), September 2025 financial reports with bank reconciliations, and a resolution on disbursements.

The proceedings follow a consistent structure. The agenda allocates specific time blocks for each item. Motions are made by named trustees and seconded by others. Votes are recorded. Committee meetings document attendees, discussion topics, and action items. The budget process is transparent — staff present drafts with line-item detail, the board requests changes, and subsequent drafts incorporate those changes with explanations. The budget memorandum from the Director of Finance & HR explains the annual process, the legal framework (Illinois library law, village levy requirements, Cook County Treasurer), and itemizes changes between drafts.

The budget itself is straightforward. Revenue is 96% property tax ($11.9M of $12.5M total). The proposed 5.0% property tax levy increase translates to approximately $25 more per household annually ($488.92 to $513.58). Expenditure increases are explained: compensation adjustments, a Director of Equity and Anti-Racism position, vendor price increases and tariff costs for print materials, and growing community demand for digital content. Capital expenditures include building improvements ($575K) and technology projects ($60K). The library faces a delayed second property tax installment from Cook County and discusses contingency options (Tax Anticipation Warrant, village loan). These are routine municipal finance management decisions.

The strategic planning session minutes document a SOAR exercise (Strengths, Opportunities, Aspirations, Results) with identifiable participants, substantive discussion, and concrete outputs. The governance committee reviews policies (Workplace Violence, Volunteer Policy) and discusses an evaluation template. The executive director reports on staff communication regarding ICE presence in the community, an IMLS update, and participation in the Illinois Library Association conference.

Two items contain identifiable power-dynamics dimensions. The Anti-Racism & Equity strategic priority includes equity audits of policies, staff affinity groups, and a Director of Equity and Anti-Racism position — this represents an institution attempting to examine its own structural biases, which is a power-aware governance function. The ICE discussion at the Intergovernmental Committee and Board meetings addresses how governing bodies should communicate about immigration enforcement — a point where federal coercive power intersects with local institutional governance. Both items are processed through normal democratic channels with documented discussion.

The power explanation does not surpass the null case for this material. The document records a library board conducting routine governance through established democratic procedures.
