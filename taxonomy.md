# Taxonomy of Power

> Role: This document defines the layers through which power and control
> operate. When analyzing material, use these layers as a checklist.
> Identify which layers are active, which mechanisms are at play, and
> — critically — how the layers interact with each other.

---

## Layers

### 1. Thought & Narrative

Control of what people believe, what they consider possible, and what they
accept as normal.

**Operates through**: media, education, religion, advertising, algorithmic
curation, entertainment, public relations, think tanks

**Mechanisms**:
- **Framing**: Selecting which aspects of reality to emphasize or minimize
- **Omission**: Controlling what is never discussed
- **Repetition**: Establishing ideas as truth through volume
- **Manufactured consent**: Engineering public agreement with elite interests
- **Overton window management**: Defining the range of "acceptable" positions
- **Linguistic redefinition**: Changing the meaning of words to reshape thought
- **False equivalence**: Presenting asymmetric positions as equally valid
- **Narrative sequencing**: Controlling the order in which facts are revealed

> DETECTION PROMPT: "Is someone shaping what the audience believes,
> what they consider normal, or what they think is possible?"

### 2. Economic

Control through dependency, access to resources, and the structure of
incentives.

**Operates through**: financial systems, labor markets, debt instruments,
property law, trade policy, monetary policy, corporate structure

**Mechanisms**:
- **Dependency creation**: Making survival contingent on compliance
- **Extraction**: Transferring value from producers to controllers
- **Artificial scarcity**: Restricting supply to maintain control over access
- **Debt leverage**: Using obligation to constrain future choices
- **Wage suppression**: Maintaining precarity to ensure labor compliance
- **Monopoly/monopsony**: Eliminating alternatives
- **Regulatory capture**: Using regulation to entrench incumbents
- **Financialization**: Abstracting real value into controllable instruments

> DETECTION PROMPT: "Is someone controlling access to resources, creating
> dependency, or structuring incentives to produce compliance?"

### 3. Legal & Regulatory

Control through rules, their selective enforcement, and the friction of
compliance.

**Operates through**: legislation, regulation, licensing, judicial
interpretation, administrative law, international treaties

**Mechanisms**:
- **Selective enforcement**: Applying rules unevenly based on who is targeted
- **Complexity as barrier**: Making compliance costly to exclude small actors
- **Criminalization**: Defining resistance or alternatives as illegal
- **Procedural burden**: Using process to exhaust challengers
- **Legal asymmetry**: Ensuring unequal access to legal resources
- **Preemptive regulation**: Outlawing things before they become threats
- **Jurisdictional arbitrage**: Exploiting gaps between legal systems

> DETECTION PROMPT: "Are rules being used to constrain some actors while
> enabling others? Is the process itself the punishment?"

### 4. Institutional

Control through organizations that persist beyond individuals and accumulate
structural authority.

**Operates through**: government agencies, corporations, NGOs, academia,
professional bodies, standards organizations, international bodies

**Mechanisms**:
- **Gatekeeping**: Controlling who may participate
- **Credentialing**: Requiring institutional approval to be considered legitimate
- **Agenda-setting**: Determining what questions are asked
- **Standard-setting**: Defining what counts as acceptable practice
- **Revolving doors**: Blurring the boundary between regulator and regulated
- **Institutional capture**: Redirecting an institution to serve interests other than its stated mission
- **Path dependency**: Using established infrastructure to make alternatives unviable

> DETECTION PROMPT: "Is an institution shaping who gets access, what
> counts as legitimate, or what options are available?"

### 5. Surveillance & Information

Control through observation, data collection, and asymmetric access to
information.

**Operates through**: state surveillance, corporate data collection, social
monitoring, credit scoring, record-keeping, algorithmic profiling

**Mechanisms**:
- **Panopticon effect**: Modifying behavior through awareness of being watched
- **Chilling effect**: Suppressing speech or action through fear of consequences
- **Profiling**: Categorizing individuals for differential treatment
- **Predictive systems**: Pre-empting behavior based on modeled probability
- **Information asymmetry**: Knowing more about subjects than they know about the observer
- **Opacity**: Making the surveillance system itself invisible or incomprehensible
- **Data accumulation**: Building dossiers that create future leverage

> DETECTION PROMPT: "Is someone watching, collecting, profiling, or
> predicting? Is the subject aware? Can they see back?"

### 6. Physical & Coercive

Control through force or the credible threat of force.

**Operates through**: military, police, private security, incarceration,
border control, asset seizure

**Mechanisms**:
- **Direct force**: Physical violence or restraint
- **Deterrence**: Threat of force to prevent action
- **Containment**: Restricting movement or access to spaces
- **Displacement**: Removing populations from territory or resources
- **Militarization**: Applying military methods to civil contexts
- **Delegation**: Outsourcing force to private or informal actors
- **Escalation architecture**: Structuring responses so minor resistance triggers major force

> DETECTION PROMPT: "Is force being used, threatened, or structurally
> implied? Who has the monopoly on violence in this context?"

## Layer interaction analysis

When multiple layers are active, map their interactions using this format:

```
[Layer A] --enables--> [Layer B]
[Layer B] --legitimizes--> [Layer C]
[Layer C] --enforces--> [Layer A]
```

Look for:
- **Reinforcement loops**: Layer A strengthens Layer B which strengthens Layer A
- **Dependency chains**: Control at Layer A is prerequisite for control at Layer B
- **Concealment**: One layer provides the visible justification while another does the actual work
- **Escalation paths**: How resistance at one layer triggers response at another
