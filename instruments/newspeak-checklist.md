# Instrument: Newspeak Checklist

> Role: Use this instrument to detect language being used as an instrument
> of control. Apply during ANALYZE step 1 (DECOMPOSE) and step 6 (ABSENT)
> to identify linguistic manipulation in the material being analyzed.
>
> Derived from: principles/orwell-1984.md (P1: Language reduction as
> thought elimination)

SOURCE: Synthesized from George Orwell's *1984* (1949) and "Politics
and the English Language" (1946)
TAXONOMY LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Legal & Regulatory,
Institutional
ANALYTICAL USE: During ANALYZE steps 1, 2, and 6 — detect language
manipulation in source material. Also useful in EXTRACT mode step 2
when identifying mechanisms of control in a body of work.

---

## Detection categories

### 1. Vocabulary reduction

The range of expressible ideas is being narrowed by eliminating words
or making them unavailable.

**Check for**:
- Are specific words being removed from acceptable usage?
- Are distinctions between related concepts being collapsed?
  (e.g., merging "freedom from" and "freedom to" into a single
  undifferentiated "freedom")
- Are technical or precise terms being replaced by vague ones?
- Is nuance being treated as suspicious or elitist?

**Ask**: "What idea has become harder to express because the words
for it are disappearing or being stigmatized?"

### 2. Euphemistic substitution

Harsh realities are being obscured by softer language that prevents
emotional and moral evaluation.

**Check for**:
- Violence described in clinical or technical terms
  ("neutralize", "collateral damage", "enhanced interrogation")
- Economic harm described in positive framing
  ("right-sizing", "restructuring", "flexibility")
- Displacement described as movement
  ("relocation", "resettlement", "urban renewal")
- Surveillance described as care
  ("monitoring for your safety", "personalized experience")

**Ask**: "If the euphemism were replaced with a direct description
of what is actually happening, would the audience react differently?"

### 3. Redefinition

Existing words are being assigned new meanings that serve power,
while retaining the emotional associations of the old meaning.

**Check for**:
- Words with positive associations being attached to systems of control
  ("freedom" meaning free markets, "security" meaning surveillance,
  "reform" meaning cuts, "innovation" meaning deregulation)
- Words with negative associations being attached to resistance
  ("extremist", "radical", "disruptive" applied to challengers
  of established power)
- Historical terms being repurposed to foreclose their original meaning

**Ask**: "Who redefined this term, and what did it used to mean?
What becomes unsayable under the new definition?"

### 4. Forced affirmation

The requirement to use specific language that encodes ideological
commitments, where refusal to use the language is itself treated
as transgression.

**Check for**:
- Mandatory terminology in institutional settings
- Social or professional penalties for using older/alternative terms
- Language that requires affirming a contested premise as a
  precondition for participating in discussion
- Framing where questioning the terminology is equated with
  opposing the stated value it represents

**Ask**: "Is the language itself a loyalty test? Can someone
participate in this discussion without accepting the framing
embedded in the required terms?"

### 5. Complexity as obscurity

Language is being made deliberately complex or technical to prevent
understanding by those affected.

**Check for**:
- Legal or regulatory language that is incomprehensible to its subjects
- Financial instruments described in terms that obscure their function
- Policy described in acronyms and jargon that exclude public evaluation
- Terms of service that no reasonable person would read

**Ask**: "Is the complexity necessary for precision, or does it
function to prevent scrutiny? Who benefits from this being hard
to understand?"

### 6. Binary framing

Complex situations are reduced to two options, eliminating the
possibility of alternatives.

**Check for**:
- "Either/or" framing that excludes third possibilities
- "With us or against us" rhetoric
- Presenting one policy as the only alternative to a feared outcome
- Framing that treats a spectrum as having only two positions

**Ask**: "What options have been eliminated by this framing?
Who benefits from the excluded middle?"

### 7. Emotional substitution

Precise analytical language is being replaced by emotional signaling
that bypasses evaluation.

**Check for**:
- Arguments that rely on emotional reaction rather than evidence
- Labels that trigger emotional response instead of describing
  ("hero", "monster", "threat", "crisis")
- Urgency framing that discourages deliberation
- Moral language used to prevent cost-benefit analysis

**Ask**: "If the emotional charge were removed, what is the
actual claim being made? Is the emotion a substitute for evidence?"

---

## Compound signals

The most significant findings involve multiple categories operating
together. Common compounds:

- **Euphemism + Redefinition**: A harmful practice is given a new
  name that not only softens it but makes it sound beneficial
- **Reduction + Binary framing**: Vocabulary is narrowed AND the
  remaining terms are organized into a false binary
- **Forced affirmation + Emotional substitution**: Mandatory
  language carries emotional charge that prevents analytical
  evaluation of the thing being affirmed
- **Complexity + Euphemism**: The system is too complex to understand
  AND the simplified version uses language that hides its function

> When two or more categories compound, flag it as a high-priority
> finding. Compound linguistic manipulation is rarely accidental.

---

## How to apply this instrument

1. Read the material with each category as a filter
2. Flag specific phrases, terms, or framings that match
3. For each flag, apply the category's ASK question
4. Check for compound signals
5. Assess: Is the linguistic manipulation incidental (sloppy
   communication) or structural (serving identifiable interests)?
6. Include findings in the relevant methodology step output
