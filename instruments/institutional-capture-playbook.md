# Instrument: Institutional Capture Playbook

> Role: Use this instrument to detect whether institutional capture is in
> progress, assess how far the pipeline has advanced, and identify which
> stages are active. The playbook catalogs ten stages of institutional
> capture synthesized from six independently documented domains. Each
> stage appears normal in isolation — the capture is visible only when
> the stages are recognized as a coordinated sequence.
>
> Derived from: Cross-referencing six documented capture domains in the
> framework corpus. No single source documents all ten stages; the
> complete pipeline is visible only through synthesis.

SOURCE: Lewis F. Powell Jr., confidential memorandum (1971);
Senator Sheldon Whitehouse, *The Scheme* (2022);
ProPublica/Documented, Ziklag investigation (2024);
Heritage Foundation, *Mandate for Leadership 2025* (2023);
Variety, Ellison/Paramount-WBD investigation (2026);
Guriev and Rachinsky, "The Role of Oligarchs in Russian Capitalism" (2005)

TAXONOMY LAYERS: Institutional (primary), Legal & Regulatory, Economic,
Surveillance & Information, Thought & Narrative

ANALYTICAL USE: During ANALYZE steps 3 (LOCATE — identify which capture
stages are active), 4 (CONNECT — trace the pipeline across stages), and
6 (ABSENT — identify which stages are operating but not visible in the
material)

## Stages

### Stage 1: Legitimation Framing

- **Name**: Legitimation Framing
- **Description**: A threat narrative or constitutional argument is
  constructed that makes institutional capture appear as restoration,
  defense, or democratic accountability. The framing must precede the
  capture — it creates the political conditions under which the
  subsequent stages appear justified rather than aggressive.
- **Detection**: Language of defense against attack ("under broad
  attack," "the Left has captured"), restoration rhetoric ("returning
  power to the people," "restoring constitutional order"), existential
  threat framing that justifies extraordinary measures. Ask: "Is the
  proposed seizure of institutional control framed as the correction of
  a prior illegitimate seizure?"
- **Power function**: Makes the capture invisible as capture. If
  consolidation is framed as restoration, resistance to it appears as
  defense of illegitimate power rather than defense of institutional
  independence. The framing converts offense into defense at the
  narrative level before any structural change occurs. Connects to
  [Inversion of Stated Purpose](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#inversion-of-stated-purpose)
  and [Appearance as Structural Terrain](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#appearance-as-structural-terrain).
- **Documented in**: Powell Memo P1 (business "under broad attack" from
  consumer advocates and environmentalists — defensive framing for an
  offensive institutional campaign), Project 2025 P5 (unitary executive
  theory frames seizure of agency independence as constitutional
  restoration — "The Administrative State holds 100 percent of its power
  at the sufferance of Congress, and its insulation from presidential
  discipline is an unconstitutional fairy tale")

### Stage 2: Funding Infrastructure

- **Name**: Funding Infrastructure
- **Description**: An opacity architecture is constructed that severs the
  traceable connection between funders and institutional outcomes. The
  funding flows through intermediary organizations — donor-advised funds,
  501(c)(3)/(c)(4) entities, trade associations — that provide legal
  anonymity at each transfer. The infrastructure enables coordination
  without visible coordination.
- **Detection**: Intermediary organizations that receive large anonymous
  donations and redistribute to operational groups. Tax-exempt
  organizations engaged in activities that test or exceed their legal
  designation. Multiple nominally independent organizations funded by
  the same undisclosed sources. Ask: "Can the funding chain be traced
  from donor to institutional outcome? If not, what legal structures
  prevent tracing?"
- **Power function**: The opacity is not incidental — it is the
  structural condition that allows subsequent pipeline stages to appear
  independent. If the same funder were visibly behind personnel
  selection, installation campaigns, outcome signaling, and input
  manufacturing, the coordination would be obvious. The funding
  infrastructure makes coordination invisible by making its funding
  source invisible. Connects to [Division of Learning](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#the-division-of-learning)
  and [Wealth Defense Industry](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#the-wealth-defense-industry).
- **Documented in**: Whitehouse P1 (Donors Trust/Donors Capital Fund
  pipeline — $17M anonymous donation funding Garland blockade and
  Gorsuch confirmation), Ziklag (501(c)(3) charity with $25M+ net worth
  threshold, revenue $1.3M to $12M in four years, six legal experts
  concluded activities test or violate tax-exempt status, IRS
  non-response), Variety/Ellison ($150M Free Press acquisition as
  investment vehicle for editorial capture)

### Stage 3: Long-Term Institutional Investment

- **Name**: Long-Term Institutional Investment
- **Description**: Permanent organizations are built that generate
  favorable research, litigation, personnel, and narrative continuously
  and autonomously. Unlike reactive political action (fighting each
  battle individually), institutional investment produces self-sustaining
  infrastructure that compounds returns over decades — the institutional
  equivalent of r > g.
- **Detection**: Think tanks, legal organizations, academic centers,
  fellowship programs, and professional networks that have operated for
  decades producing consistently aligned outputs. The test is
  *autonomy*: does the organization continue generating favorable
  outcomes independently of any specific funder or leader? Ask: "When
  was this institutional infrastructure built, by whom, and has its
  output been consistently aligned since?"
- **Power function**: Converts one-time investment into perpetual
  institutional influence. The organizations become self-sustaining:
  endowed chairs produce research that trains the next generation that
  staffs the think tanks that produce the policy papers that the legal
  organizations litigate. The compounding nature means the initial
  investment's returns accelerate over time. Connects to
  [Peacetime Ratchet](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#the-peacetime-ratchet).
- **Documented in**: Powell Memo P4 (proposed endowed chairs, permanent
  Chamber staff, standing legal organizations — subsequently constructed
  as Heritage Foundation 1973, Cato Institute 1977, Federalist Society
  1982, Pacific Legal Foundation 1973), Ziklag (explicit "30-year
  strategy" organized around Seven Mountain Mandate — arts/media,
  business, church, education, family, government, science/technology)

### Stage 4: Personnel Selection Pipeline

- **Name**: Personnel Selection Pipeline
- **Description**: A privately controlled process identifies, vets,
  grooms, and tracks candidates for institutional positions over years
  or decades before any formal appointment occurs. By the time the
  formal selection process begins, the candidate pool has already been
  filtered through the private pipeline. The formal process selects
  from a pre-curated list.
- **Detection**: Professional networks or organizations that track
  members from early career (student chapters, fellowship programs,
  clerkships) through advancement to positions of institutional
  authority. Nomination lists that draw exclusively from a single
  network. Loyalty screening that evaluates ideological alignment
  rather than professional qualification. Ask: "Who curated the
  candidate pool before the formal selection process began?"
- **Power function**: Controls institutional outcomes by controlling
  who is available to be selected. The formal selection process —
  presidential nomination, Senate confirmation, competitive hiring —
  operates normally, but within parameters set by the prior private
  selection. The capture is invisible because the formal process is
  real; what is invisible is the pre-filtering that determined its
  inputs. Connects to [Capture Pipeline](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#the-capture-pipeline)
  and [Anticipatory Obedience](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#anticipatory-obedience).
- **Documented in**: Whitehouse P2 (Federalist Society maintains a
  network from law school student chapters through professional practice
  to the judiciary — Trump explicitly outsourced judicial selection to
  their list), Project 2025 P1 (Schedule F reclassification + PPO
  centralized vetting + political appointee saturation at every level —
  "whoever holds a government position sets its policy"), Ziklag
  (Barrett confirmation — claims "hugely significant role" in Supreme
  Court nomination process), Variety/Ellison (Bari Weiss installed as
  CBS News editor-in-chief through $150M acquisition of The Free Press)

### Stage 5: Installation

- **Name**: Installation
- **Description**: Selected personnel are placed into institutional
  positions through formal processes — confirmation campaigns, executive
  orders, appointment authority, corporate acquisition. The installation
  uses legitimate institutional mechanisms; what makes it a capture
  stage is that the personnel were pre-selected through Stage 4 and
  their placement serves a coordinated pipeline purpose.
- **Detection**: Funded campaigns supporting or opposing nominees.
  Executive orders issued on Day One that restructure appointment
  authority. Mass replacement of career personnel with political
  appointees. Rapid personnel changes across multiple institutions
  simultaneously. Ask: "Were these appointments coordinated across
  institutions? Were the campaigns supporting them funded through
  Stage 2 infrastructure?"
- **Power function**: Converts the privately curated candidate pool
  (Stage 4) into actual institutional authority. The installation is
  the moment the pipeline transitions from influence to control. Once
  installed, personnel can execute subsequent stages (oversight
  elimination, outcome signaling) from inside the institution.
  Connects to [Institutional Abdication](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#institutional-abdication)
  (the installation creates pressure for existing personnel to abdicate).
- **Documented in**: Whitehouse (dark money-funded confirmation
  campaigns — Judicial Crisis Network spent $17M+ across Gorsuch,
  Kavanaugh, Barrett confirmations), Project 2025 (Day One executive
  orders, political appointee saturation — "make every effort to obtain
  the resources to support a vast expansion of the number of appointees
  in every office and component across the department"), Ziklag
  (Steeplechase church voter mobilization, Checkmate voter purge
  targeting Biden's Arizona margin of 10,447 with a goal of 10,640),
  Guriev-Rachinsky (oligarchs captured RSPP governance, created
  taskforces, secured regular presidential meetings)

### Stage 6: Oversight Elimination

- **Name**: Oversight Elimination
- **Description**: The institution's internal oversight mechanisms —
  independent legal counsel, civil rights offices, privacy officers,
  ombudsmen, inspector-general functions, advisory committees — are
  eliminated or subordinated to political control. These mechanisms
  constitute the institutional immune system; they must be neutralized
  before the institution can be redirected.
- **Detection**: Absorption of independent offices into politically
  controlled directorates. Elimination of ombudsman positions.
  Dismissal of advisory committee members en masse. Transfer of
  independent legal counsel functions to politically appointed General
  Counsel. Removal of civil rights or privacy review authority over
  operational outputs. Ask: "Which internal mechanisms existed to
  check institutional redirection, and have they been eliminated,
  subordinated, or defunded?"
- **Power function**: Removes the capacity for institutional
  self-correction. An institution with independent oversight can resist
  capture because the oversight bodies can flag, report, delay, or
  legally challenge redirection. Eliminating oversight converts the
  institution from a self-correcting system to a directable one. The
  elimination is often framed as "streamlining" or "reducing
  bureaucratic redundancy" — [Inversion of Stated Purpose](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#inversion-of-stated-purpose)
  applied to institutional self-defense.
- **Documented in**: Project 2025 P3 (systematic targeting across every
  agency — "The Office of the Immigration Detention Ombudsman should be
  eliminated"; "The Office of General Counsel should absorb both CRCL's
  and PRIV's necessary functions"; "Streamline the non-law enforcement
  functions within the FBI, such as its Office of General Counsel";
  "CRCL and PRIV staff should no longer review intelligence products"),
  Variety/Ellison (60 Minutes investigation into El Salvador detention
  center killed after editorial installation)

### Stage 7: Outcome Signaling

- **Name**: Outcome Signaling
- **Description**: Formal institutional channels — amicus briefs, public
  comment periods, regulatory review processes, legislative testimony,
  editorial guidelines — are repurposed as coordination mechanisms.
  These channels appear to serve their stated function (public input,
  regulatory oversight, editorial independence); in practice they
  transmit desired outcomes from pipeline operators to captured
  institutional actors.
- **Detection**: Coordinated filings from nominally independent
  organizations funded through common sources. Regulatory review
  processes where the outcome is predetermined by political directive.
  Editorial promises to regulators as condition of approval. Trade
  associations drafting legislation for governmental adoption. Ask:
  "Are these formal input channels carrying organic, independent
  input — or coordinated signals from a common source?"
- **Power function**: Satisfies the formal requirements of institutional
  process (public input was received, regulatory review was conducted,
  editorial standards were referenced) while controlling substantive
  outcomes through the inputs. The institution appears to operate
  normally because the formal process ran normally; the capture is in
  the inputs, not the process. Connects to
  [Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#democracy-oligarchy-coexistence)
  (formal process handles the appearance; coordinated signals handle the
  substance).
- **Documented in**: Whitehouse P3 (coordinated amicus briefs from dark
  money-funded organizations — 80+ cases decided 5-4 where filings
  predicted the winning side; majority opinions adopted language
  directly from amicus briefs), Project 2025 (OMB regulatory review as
  "commander's intent" directive), Guriev-Rachinsky P1 (RSPP created
  taskforces → drafted legislation → secured regular presidential
  meetings → provided "advice" on legislative changes), Variety/Ellison
  (editorial promises to FCC Chair Carr as condition of regulatory
  approval)

### Stage 8: Input Manufacturing

- **Name**: Input Manufacturing
- **Description**: The raw material for institutional outputs — cases,
  plaintiffs, ballot initiatives, voter registrations, public comments,
  research studies — is manufactured to produce specific predetermined
  outcomes. The institution processes these inputs through its normal
  procedures, producing outputs that appear organic but were
  predetermined by the manufactured inputs.
- **Detection**: Organizations created shortly before filing a case or
  launching an initiative. Plaintiffs whose standing is funded or
  constructed by third parties. Legal theories developed in aligned
  publications before the case is filed. Ballot initiatives designed
  primarily as voter turnout mechanisms rather than policy goals.
  Research studies commissioned by parties with a stake in the outcome.
  Ask: "Did this input arise organically, or was it manufactured to
  produce a specific institutional output?"
- **Power function**: Subverts the purpose of institutional processes
  (which assume organic inputs) by controlling what enters the process.
  The institution's neutrality is technically preserved — it processed
  the inputs it received through standard procedures — while its
  outputs are predetermined by whoever controls the inputs. This is the
  deepest form of capture: the institution is not corrupted, redirected,
  or hollowed out — it functions exactly as designed, on manufactured
  inputs. Connects to
  [Formal Transformation, Structural Continuity](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#formal-transformation-structural-continuity)
  (the institutional form is unchanged; the substance is controlled).
- **Documented in**: Whitehouse P4 (case manufacturing — finding
  plaintiffs, funding litigation, steering cases through favorable
  courts to create vehicles for desired Supreme Court precedents; Janus
  v. AFSCME documented as following this pattern), Ziklag (Watchtower —
  anti-trans ballot initiatives in 7 states designed as voter turnout
  strategy; Checkmate — EagleAI targeting ~1M voter registrations in 4
  swing states, Arizona target of 10,640 matching Biden's 2020 margin)

### Stage 9: Enforcement Nullification

- **Name**: Enforcement Nullification
- **Description**: A regulatory regime is rendered structurally
  unenforceable while remaining formally intact. The law exists, the
  agency exists, but enforcement cannot occur because the architectural
  conditions for enforcement have been disabled. This produces the
  appearance of governance without its substance.
- **Detection**: Even-number commission structures that guarantee
  partisan deadlock. Appointments of commissioners selected for
  opposition to enforcement. Executive directives prohibiting criminal
  prosecution when the civil agency deadlocks. Removal of independent
  litigation authority. Budget cuts that eliminate inspection or
  enforcement capacity while preserving the agency's formal existence.
  Ask: "Does this regulatory agency have the structural capacity to
  enforce — not merely the legal authority?"
- **Power function**: Creates the most durable form of deregulation —
  one that preserves the formal framework. Legislation can be repealed,
  but that requires congressional action and generates political
  opposition. Enforcement nullification achieves the same outcome
  invisibly: the law remains on the books, the agency remains funded,
  but enforcement is architecturally impossible. Citizens believe
  they are protected; they are not. Connects to
  [Appearance as Structural Terrain](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#appearance-as-structural-terrain)
  (the regulatory framework is the appearance; nullified enforcement is
  the reality) and
  [Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#democracy-oligarchy-coexistence)
  (the democratic law exists; oligarchic exemption operates through the
  structural gap).
- **Documented in**: Project 2025 P4 (FEC enforcement nullification
  chain — even-number commission guarantees deadlock on enforcement
  votes, DOJ directed not to prosecute when commission deadlocks,
  independent litigation authority transferred to politically controlled
  DOJ; "The current requirement of four votes to authorize an
  enforcement action... ensures that there is bipartisan agreement
  before any action is taken")

### Stage 10: Ratchet Mechanisms

- **Name**: Ratchet Mechanisms
- **Description**: Structural features are built into the capture that
  resist reversal. The capture, once achieved, becomes the new baseline
  — restoring the prior state requires more effort than achieving the
  capture did. This asymmetry between the ease of capture and the
  difficulty of reversal is the ratchet.
- **Detection**: Lifetime appointments of captured institutional actors.
  Employment reclassifications that can only be reversed by new
  legislation. Eliminated offices that require new appropriations to
  re-establish. Self-sustaining institutional infrastructure (Stage 3)
  that continues generating favorable outputs regardless of political
  changes. Precedents that can only be overturned by the same captured
  institution. Ask: "What would it take to reverse this change? Is the
  reversal structurally harder than the change itself?"
- **Power function**: Converts temporary political advantage into
  permanent structural change. The ratchet ensures that even if the
  capturing faction loses power, the institutional changes persist
  because reversal requires overcoming structural barriers the capture
  created. This is why lifetime judicial appointments are the
  highest-value capture target (Powell P3) — a captured judge produces
  favorable outcomes for decades regardless of subsequent elections.
  Connects to [Peacetime Ratchet](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#the-peacetime-ratchet).
- **Documented in**: Project 2025 P6 (Schedule F reclassification
  requires new legislation to reverse; eliminated oversight offices
  require new appropriations; consolidated authority becomes the new
  baseline — "boldness to bend or break the bureaucracy to the
  presidential will"), Whitehouse (lifetime judicial appointments —
  captured judges serve 30+ years, producing favorable precedent
  regardless of subsequent elections; each precedent becomes the
  baseline for the next), Powell P4 (institutional infrastructure that
  generates returns autonomously — the Heritage Foundation, Federalist
  Society, and allied organizations continue operating regardless of
  which party holds power)

**Coverage**: Every stage has at least 2 independent source domains
except Stage 9 (enforcement nullification), which is documented only in
Project 2025. Stage 9 is retained because (a) the mechanism is described
in operational detail by its own architects and (b) analogous mechanisms
exist in other regulatory domains (SEC underfunding, NLRB quorum
prevention, EPA inspection cuts) that have not yet been formally
extracted but are observable.

**Strongest stages**: Stages 4 (selection) and 5 (installation) are
documented across 4 independent domains each. Stage 7 (signaling) is
documented across 4 domains. These three stages are the pipeline's most
visible and most consistently replicated elements.

**Pipeline observation**: No single source documents all ten stages.
The Powell Memo describes the strategy (Stages 1, 3, 10). Whitehouse
documents judicial execution (Stages 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, 10). Project 2025
documents executive execution (Stages 1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10). Ziklag
documents electoral/religious execution (Stages 2, 3, 4, 5, 8). The
complete pipeline is visible only through cross-referencing — which is
itself analytically significant: no individual observing one domain would
see the complete capture apparatus.

## How to apply this instrument

### During ANALYZE Step 3 (LOCATE):

1. When institutional changes are present in the material, scan each of
   the ten stages. Ask: "Which stages are active, documented, or implied?"
2. Use the detection indicators to classify what is observed. Not every
   capture involves all ten stages — partial pipelines are common and
   still diagnostically significant.
3. Map active stages to the cross-reference matrix: does the pattern
   match a documented domain? If the material shows stages 4, 5, 6, and
   7 active simultaneously, this matches the Project 2025 / executive
   capture template.

### During ANALYZE Step 4 (CONNECT):

1. Trace connections between stages. If Stage 4 (selection) is visible,
   ask whether Stage 2 (funding) can be traced. If Stage 7 (signaling)
   is visible, ask whether Stage 5 (installation) preceded it.
2. The pipeline's stages are sequential but overlapping — later stages
   often begin before earlier stages are complete. Installation (5) may
   begin while long-term investment (3) is still building. Oversight
   elimination (6) may be simultaneous with installation (5).
3. Cross-reference with the [Capture Pipeline](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns.md#the-capture-pipeline)
   pattern for the overarching structural description.

### During ANALYZE Step 6 (ABSENT):

1. If some stages are visible but others are not, the absent stages are
   the highest-priority questions. If installation (5) is visible but
   the personnel selection pipeline (4) is not, ask: "Who curated the
   candidate pool, and through what process?"
2. The most analytically dangerous absence is Stage 2 (funding
   infrastructure) — by design, the funding opacity makes this stage
   invisible. When other stages are visible but funding is opaque, flag
   this as a structural absence, not a gap in the material.
3. Stage 1 (legitimation framing) is often the most visible stage
   because it operates through public narrative. If no legitimation
   framing is present but other stages are active, this may indicate
   the capture is operating without public justification — a higher-
   confidence indicator that the operators prefer invisibility over
   legitimacy.

### Threshold assessment:

Not every institutional change is capture. Use these thresholds:

- **1-2 stages active**: May indicate normal institutional dynamics.
  Apply the null case (IC-2): legitimate democratic accountability,
  bureaucratic reform, policy disagreement.
- **3-5 stages active**: Diagnostically significant. The probability
  of independent coincidence decreases as stages accumulate. Document
  the stages and their connections.
- **6+ stages active simultaneously**: Strong indicator of coordinated
  capture. The null case (independent reform, democratic accountability)
  becomes increasingly difficult to sustain as more stages are present
  and interconnected.
- **All 10 stages active**: Only observed in Project 2025, which is a
  blueprint rather than a documented execution. If all 10 stages are
  observed in an active institutional context, this is the most
  operationally complete capture documented in the framework's corpus.

## Limitations

- **Selection bias**: All six source domains are from or about the
  American and Russian political contexts. The instrument's detection
  indicators are shaped by these contexts and may miss capture
  mechanisms in systems with different institutional structures
  (parliamentary systems, one-party states, transnational institutions).
- **Conservative-movement overrepresentation**: Five of six source
  domains document conservative or oligarchic capture. The instrument
  may be better calibrated for detecting right-of-center institutional
  capture than left-of-center variants. Equivalent mechanisms likely
  exist in progressive institutional campaigns (Demand Justice,
  Arabella Advisors network) but are not yet documented in the corpus.
  IC-5 applies.
- **Blueprint vs. execution**: Project 2025 is a blueprint, not a
  completed execution. Stages documented only from that source (notably
  Stage 9) have weaker empirical grounding than stages documented from
  observed execution.
- **Coordination vs. emergence**: The instrument assumes coordinated
  pipeline operation. Some captured institutions may exhibit these
  stages through emergent alignment of interests rather than
  centralized coordination. The distinction matters normatively
  (conspiracy vs. structural tendency) but may not matter
  diagnostically (the institutional effect is the same).
- **Not exhaustive**: Ten stages represent what six sources make visible.
  Additional sources may reveal additional stages. The instrument should
  be extended when new capture domains are documented.
