# Instrument: Accountability Dismantlement Checklist

> Role: Use this instrument to detect whether an accountability
> architecture is being systematically removed, assess how many stages
> are active, and distinguish dismantlement from legitimate reform. The
> checklist catalogs five stages that collectively remove the structural
> conditions under which institutional actors face consequences for
> harmful outcomes. Each stage appears defensible in isolation —
> "streamlining," "restoring operational tempo," "eliminating
> bureaucracy" — but the dismantlement is visible when the stages are
> recognised as a coordinated removal of layered safeguards.
>
> Related to but distinct from the Institutional Capture Playbook:
> capture *redirects* institutional function; dismantlement *eliminates*
> accountability function. They often co-occur — capture installs the
> personnel who then execute the dismantlement.
>
> Derived from: The documented dismantlement of the US Civilian Harm
> Mitigation and Response (CHMR) programme under Defense Secretary
> Hegseth (2025-2026), cross-referenced with structural parallels in
> the framework corpus.

SOURCE: ProPublica, "The U.S. Built a Blueprint to Avoid Civilian War
Casualties. Trump Officials Scrapped It" (2026);
Heritage Foundation, *Mandate for Leadership 2025* (2023);
Church Committee, *Book III: COINTELPRO* (1976);
Timothy Snyder, *On Tyranny* (2017);
Defense One, "Hegseth orders 'ruthless' review of JAGs" (2026)

TAXONOMY LAYERS: Institutional (primary), Legal & Regulatory,
Thought & Narrative, Surveillance & Information

ANALYTICAL USE: During ANALYZE steps 3 (LOCATE — identify which
dismantlement stages are active), 4 (CONNECT — trace the sequence
across stages and compare to documented instances), 5 (INVERT — ask
whether the dismantlement reflects legitimate reform or systematic
removal), and 6 (ABSENT — identify which stages are operating but
not visible in the material)

## Stages

### Stage 1: Personnel Selection

- **Name**: Personnel Selection (the enabling condition)
- **Description**: A leader is installed who views accountability
  mechanisms as constraints on institutional purpose rather than as
  components of it. The leader's prior statements, career, and
  professional identity signal that oversight is an obstacle. This
  stage is the precondition for all subsequent stages — without a
  leader who views accountability as antagonistic to the mission,
  the remaining stages lack institutional authorisation.
- **Detection**: Leader's public record includes defence of
  personnel accused of institutional violations; rhetoric framing
  oversight as weakness, obstruction, or political correctness;
  professional identity centred on the mission the accountability
  system constrains. Ask: "Does this leader's record suggest they
  view the accountability system as an obstacle to be removed rather
  than a function to be maintained?"
- **Power function**: Personnel selection determines institutional
  output without changing institutional rules. The rules remain on
  paper; the person who decides whether to enforce them has changed.
  Connects to [Capture Pipeline](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/capture-pipeline.md)
  (Stage 2: personnel installation) and
  [Personnel as Primary Instrument](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/principles/project-2025-mandate-for-leadership.md).
- **Documented in**: Hegseth as Defense Secretary (defended Navy SEAL
  accused of war crimes; described JAGs as "roadblocks"; made
  "lethality" a top priority over compliance); Project 2025 P1
  (personnel as primary instrument of institutional control —
  "the single most important thing" is "the people")

### Stage 2: Legal Oversight Removal

- **Name**: Legal Oversight Removal
- **Description**: The lawyers, inspectors, or compliance officers
  who provide guidance on whether institutional actions comply with
  law are fired, reassigned, or marginalised. This removes the
  institutional mechanism that translates legal obligations into
  operational constraints. Without legal advisers in the decision
  chain, operations proceed without legal review.
- **Detection**: Firings or reassignments of legal counsel,
  inspectors general, compliance officers, or ombudsmen. Rhetoric
  characterising legal oversight as obstruction ("roadblocks,"
  "jagoffs," "chilling effect"). Reduction in legal review
  requirements for operational decisions. Ask: "Has the
  institutional mechanism that checks whether actions comply with
  law been removed, reduced, or bypassed?"
- **Power function**: Legal oversight is the mechanism through which
  external obligations (international law, statutory requirements,
  constitutional limits) are translated into internal constraints.
  Removing it does not change the law — it removes the institutional
  actor who would flag violations. The law continues to apply; no
  one inside the institution applies it. Connects to
  [Institutional Abdication](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/institutional-abdication.md)
  (the institution stops performing its structural function).
- **Documented in**: Hegseth's firing of top JAGs (2025); Church
  Committee documented how FBI operated without legal review for
  decades (the "unexpressed major premise" — no one asked whether
  operations were legal because the question was not part of the
  institutional process); Project 2025 P3 (oversight elimination
  as precondition for executive control)

### Stage 3: Operational Staff Elimination

- **Name**: Operational Staff Elimination
- **Description**: The personnel who implement the accountability
  function — the specialists, advisers, investigators, and analysts
  whose job is to prevent harm, assess outcomes, and incorporate
  lessons — are eliminated through layoffs, reassignment, or
  defunding. The programme may formally continue to exist (especially
  if closure requires external approval), but its staff is gone.
- **Detection**: Personnel reductions in accountability-specific
  roles; programmes that continue on paper but lack staff, budget, or
  mandate; advisory teams reduced from functional size to token
  presence (e.g., 10 to 1). Ask: "Does the programme have the
  personnel to perform its stated function? If not, does it formally
  exist despite lacking the capacity to operate?"
- **Power function**: This stage exploits the gap between formal
  existence and operational capacity. A programme that exists on
  paper satisfies the formal requirement (congressional approval,
  policy mandate, public commitment) while eliminating the actual
  function. The form persists; the substance is gone. Connects to
  [Formal Transformation, Structural Continuity](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/formal-transformation-structural-continuity.md)
  (inverse variant: form preserved, function eliminated) and
  [Appearance as Structural Terrain](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/appearance-as-structural-terrain.md).
- **Documented in**: CHMR programme reduced by ~90%; Central Command
  team cut from 10 to 1; Civilian Protection Center of Excellence
  continues to exist (congressional approval required to close) but
  has "no mission or mandate or budget"; State Department layoffs
  before Iran war (documented in "no clear goal" analysis)

### Stage 4: Threshold and Scope Changes

- **Name**: Threshold and Scope Changes
- **Description**: The rules governing when institutional action is
  authorised are changed to lower the bar for action and broaden
  what qualifies. Authorisation levels are lowered (decisions pushed
  down the chain of command), target categories are broadened
  (more things qualify for action), and threat assessments are
  inflated (the justification threshold is reduced). These are
  technical changes that do not announce themselves as accountability
  reduction — they appear as operational adjustments.
- **Detection**: Changes to authorisation levels, targeting criteria,
  rules of engagement, or risk-acceptance thresholds. Broadening
  of categories that trigger institutional action. Inflation of
  threat assessments that justify action. Ask: "Have the rules for
  when the institution may act been changed to make action easier
  and restraint harder? Are there quantifiable changes in the volume
  of institutional action following the threshold changes?"
- **Power function**: Threshold changes are the least visible stage
  because they operate through technical parameters rather than
  personnel or organisational changes. They produce increased volume
  (more strikes, more enforcement, more action) without any single
  decision appearing unusual. The system operates "normally" under
  new parameters that produce structurally different outcomes.
  Connects to [Anticipatory Obedience](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/anticipatory-obedience.md)
  (operators adjust to new parameters without needing explicit
  instruction on each case).
- **Documented in**: Trump administration lowered authorisation
  levels for lethal force, broadened target categories, inflated
  threat assessments (ProPublica, 12+ sources); US strikes surpassed
  four Biden years before Iran campaign; Somalia strikes 125 vs 21

### Stage 5: Audit and Investigation Removal

- **Name**: Audit and Investigation Removal
- **Description**: The personnel and mechanisms that would detect,
  investigate, and report on institutional failures after the fact
  are removed. Inspectors general are fired. Post-action assessment
  requirements are eliminated. Classification is invoked to prevent
  external scrutiny. The institution loses the capacity to learn
  from its own failures — not because learning is explicitly
  prohibited but because no one remains whose job it is to look.
- **Detection**: Inspector general firings or vacancies; elimination
  of post-action review requirements; increased classification of
  outcomes; refusal to release operational details ("operational
  security"); absence of public statements acknowledging harmful
  outcomes. Ask: "If the institution produces a harmful outcome,
  does any internal mechanism exist to detect, investigate, report,
  and incorporate lessons? If not, when was that mechanism removed?"
- **Power function**: This stage closes the feedback loop. Stages
  1-4 produce increased harmful outcomes; Stage 5 ensures those
  outcomes are not detected, investigated, or learned from. Without
  post-action review, the institution cannot correct course even if
  individual operators recognise problems — there is no institutional
  channel for correction. Connects to
  [Division of Learning](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/division-of-learning.md)
  (the institution knows less about its own outcomes than external
  monitors like Bellingcat) and
  [Inversion of Stated Purpose](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/patterns/inversion-of-stated-purpose.md)
  (the institution designed to learn from failure is reconfigured to
  prevent learning).
- **Documented in**: Trump administration fired inspectors general
  (ProPublica); CHMR post-strike assessment requirements eliminated;
  US military cited "operational security" to withhold details of
  Somalia strike that killed clan mediator; Trump administration's
  response to Minab described as "shameful" — no investigation, no
  acknowledgment (contrast with Obama's Kunduz apology and Biden's
  Kabul apology)

## How to apply this instrument

**When to invoke**: During ANALYZE steps 3-6 when the material
involves an institution whose accountability mechanisms are being
reduced, reformed, or restructured.

**Application procedure**:

1. **Identify the accountability architecture**: What mechanisms
   existed to prevent, detect, investigate, and learn from
   institutional failures? Map them: legal review, operational
   staff, threshold rules, audit/investigation capacity.

2. **Check each stage**: For each of the five stages, determine
   whether it is active, completed, or not present. Record evidence
   for each active stage.

3. **Assess sequence and completeness**: How many stages are active?
   Are they sequential (each enabling the next) or parallel? Full
   dismantlement requires all five; partial dismantlement (e.g.,
   personnel cuts without threshold changes) may produce different
   outcomes.

4. **Distinguish dismantlement from reform**: Legitimate reform
   restructures accountability to make it more effective — it
   replaces mechanisms, does not simply remove them. The test: after
   the change, does the institution have *more* or *fewer* mechanisms
   to detect and learn from harmful outcomes? If fewer with no
   replacement, this is dismantlement regardless of how it is framed.

5. **Check for co-occurrence with Capture Pipeline**: Dismantlement
   often follows capture — the captured institution's first action
   is to remove the mechanisms that would constrain its new
   direction. If both instruments are active, the capture installed
   the personnel (Stage 1 of this checklist = Stage 2 of the
   Capture Pipeline) and the dismantlement removes the oversight.

**Diagnostic questions**:
- "If this institution produces a harmful outcome tomorrow, what
  mechanism exists to detect it, investigate it, and prevent
  recurrence?"
- "Has that mechanism been reduced, and if so, by how much?"
- "Was the reduction framed as reform, streamlining, or operational
  efficiency? If so, what was the replacement mechanism?"
- "Who decided to reduce the accountability mechanism, and what is
  their record on accountability?"

## Documented instances

### Primary instance: CHMR dismantlement (2025-2026)

All five stages documented with specificity:
1. Hegseth selected (defended war crimes accused, "lethality" doctrine)
2. JAGs fired ("roadblocks," "jagoffs")
3. ~90% of CHMR staff eliminated; Center of Excellence emptied
4. Authorisation lowered, targets broadened, threat assessments inflated
5. Inspectors general fired; no post-strike investigation mechanism

**Outcome**: US strikes surpassed four Biden years before Iran campaign;
1,200+ civilian casualties in Iran; Minab school strike (165+ dead,
most under 12); Yemen detention centre (61 dead); Somalia clan
mediator killed with no investigation.

**Source**: `analyses/2026-03-18-propublica-investigation-chmr-dismantlement-civilian-harm.md`

### Partial instance: NSC/State Department gutting (2025)

Stages 1, 3, and 4 documented:
1. Personnel selected for loyalty over expertise (Kushner, Witkoff)
3. NSC gutted, State Department underwent major layoffs
4. Decision-making concentrated in small circle without institutional input

**Outcome**: Iran war launched without interagency process; diplomatic
alternative foreclosed; serial goal-shifting (four stated aims).

**Source**: `analyses/2026-03-13-guardian-article-iran-war-no-clear-goal.md`,
`analyses/2026-03-17-guardian-article-uk-nsa-iran-talks-deal-within-reach.md`

### Historical parallel: FBI pre-Church Committee (1956-1971)

Stages 1, 2, and 5 inverted (accountability never existed rather
than being dismantled):
1. Hoover's institutional culture viewed legal constraints as irrelevant
2. No legal review of COINTELPRO operations (the "unexpressed major
   premise" — the question of legality was never asked)
5. No audit mechanism; oversight committees existed but were captured

**Outcome**: Systematic civil liberties violations over 15 years;
discovered only through external break-in (Media, PA) and subsequent
congressional investigation.

**Source**: `principles/church-committee-cointelpro.md`
