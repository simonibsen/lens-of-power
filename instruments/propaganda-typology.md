# Instrument: Propaganda Typology

> Role: Use this instrument to classify propaganda encountered during
> analysis. The typology provides five independent axes for characterizing
> any piece of propaganda, plus a pre-conditions diagnostic for assessing
> why the propaganda is effective in its context. What distinguishes this
> from a generic propaganda taxonomy is the power-function annotation on
> each type — documenting how it operates as a mechanism of control.
>
> Derived from: Jacques Ellul, *Propaganda: The Formation of Men's
> Attitudes* (1962); Jason Stanley, *How Propaganda Works* (2015)

SOURCE: Ellul (1962) provides the four structural axes; Stanley (2015)
provides the fifth axis (undermining/supporting) and the philosophical
mechanism for how propaganda exploits democratic ideals
TAXONOMY LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Institutional,
Surveillance & Information, Economic
ANALYTICAL USE: During ANALYZE steps 1 (DECOMPOSE — classify the
propaganda type), 2 (SOURCE — identify origin as political or
sociological), 5 (INVERT — test whether material is propaganda at all
and whether the null case applies), 6 (ABSENT — identify pre-propaganda
conditioning and what the propaganda makes unthinkable)

---

## Axis 1: Purpose — Agitation vs. Integration

Classifies what the propaganda is trying to achieve.

### Agitation propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda that mobilizes for action — subversion, revolution, war, protest, upheaval. It stirs discontent with the existing order and channels it toward specific action. High emotional intensity, short-term, often crisis-driven.
- **Detection**: Urgency language, calls to action, enemy identification, crisis framing, moral outrage, us-vs-them rhetoric, mobilization appeals ("stand up," "fight back," "we must act now")
- **Power function**: Serves those seeking to *change* the distribution of power. Used by revolutionary movements, populist leaders, and governments mobilizing for war. Creates the emotional conditions for collective action that would not occur through rational calculation alone. Agitation breaks stability — it is the offensive weapon.

### Integration propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda that adjusts people to conform to the existing patterns of society. It does not mobilize for change but produces acceptance, participation, and compliance within the current system. Low emotional intensity, long-term, ambient. The more stable and dangerous form.
- **Detection**: Normalcy framing, participation encouragement ("vote," "engage," "be a responsible citizen"), lifestyle aspiration, institutional trust messaging, consensus language, "reasonable people agree" framing. Integration propaganda often does not *look* like propaganda — it looks like culture.
- **Power function**: Serves those who benefit from the current distribution of power. Produces conformity through participation — the integrated subject feels free *because* they participate, and the participation itself is the mechanism of control. This is the Inversion of Stated Purpose at the system level: participation presented as agency, functioning as conformity. Integration propaganda is the dominant form in stable democracies.

## Axis 2: Origin — Political vs. Sociological

Classifies where the propaganda comes from.

### Political propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda produced by an identifiable actor (government, party, organization, corporation) with an identifiable objective. Has an author, a campaign, a strategy. Can in principle be traced, exposed, and countered.
- **Detection**: Identifiable source, messaging consistency, campaign structure, talking points, coordinated distribution, funded amplification, traceable organizational backing
- **Power function**: The visible exercise of narrative power. Political propaganda is what most people mean by "propaganda." Its limitation is its visibility — once identified, it can be resisted. Its power function is direct: shape opinion on specific issues through deliberate intervention. Operates primarily at the Thought & Narrative and Institutional layers.

### Sociological propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda produced diffusely by the total cultural environment — advertising, entertainment, news, education, social media, lifestyle imagery — without any single propagandist directing it. Has no identifiable author, no discrete campaign, no visible apparatus. It *is* the environment. The most pervasive and durable form.
- **Detection**: Difficult to detect precisely because it has no discrete source. Indicators: ambient assumptions that "everyone knows" (but that serve identifiable interests), lifestyle norms that produce economic compliance, conceptual categories that constrain what is thinkable, the feeling of "common sense" about contested matters. Ask: "Who benefits from this being considered normal?"
- **Power function**: The invisible exercise of narrative power. Sociological propaganda operates through Axiom 3's mechanism — power most effective when its contingency is invisible — but goes further: there is no mechanism to make visible because the propaganda is the environment itself. There is no outside vantage point. Produces the deepest form of manufactured compliance (Axiom 9) because the subject experiences the propaganda as their own free thought. This is the structural condition that makes political propaganda effective — sociological propaganda prepares the ground.

## Axis 3: Direction — Vertical vs. Horizontal

Classifies how the propaganda travels.

### Vertical propaganda
- **Description**: Top-down propaganda from a source of authority (government, institution, media outlet, platform) to a mass audience. One-to-many communication. The classic form studied by most propaganda research.
- **Detection**: Identifiable authority source, broadcast distribution, consistent messaging, institutional amplification, press conferences, official statements, media campaigns, algorithmic curation by platforms
- **Power function**: Direct deployment of institutional authority at the Thought & Narrative layer. The source's institutional position provides the credibility that makes the propaganda effective (connects to logical fallacy: appeal to authority). Vertical propaganda requires institutional infrastructure — it cannot operate without media, platforms, or state apparatus. Its power function is to convert institutional authority into narrative control.

### Horizontal propaganda
- **Description**: Peer-to-peer propaganda that spreads through social networks, group dynamics, and community pressure. Many-to-many communication. Operates through conversation, social media sharing, group norms, and the desire to belong.
- **Detection**: Peer pressure dynamics, social media sharing patterns, "everyone is saying" effects, group identity markers, viral spread, community norm enforcement, social cost for non-conformity
- **Power function**: Converts social bonds into compliance mechanisms. Horizontal propaganda is self-reinforcing — each person who adopts the message becomes a transmitter, creating social proof that recruits more adopters. The power function is decentralized enforcement: no authority needs to punish deviance when the social group does it. Connects to Anticipatory Obedience — the individual conforms not because an authority demands it but because the peer group expects it.

## Axis 4: Appeal — Rational vs. Irrational

Classifies what cognitive faculty the propaganda targets.

### Rational propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda that operates through facts, data, arguments, expert opinion, statistics, and policy analysis. It does not lie — it selects, frames, and sequences true information to produce a desired conclusion. The more dangerous because it cannot be debunked.
- **Detection**: Data-heavy framing, expert citation, policy analysis, "evidence-based" language, statistical arguments, research references, think tank reports. Ask: "Is the conclusion determined by the evidence, or was the evidence selected to support the conclusion?"
- **Power function**: Exploits the educated audience's trust in rational argument (connects to Ellul's education paradox — the educated are *more* susceptible to rational propaganda). Operates through cherry picking, framing effects, and the selection of which questions to ask and which data to present. The power function is epistemic: it controls not what people believe but what they believe *based on evidence* — making the controlled conclusion feel self-derived. Connects to the Division of Learning — the party that controls which research is funded controls the evidence base that rational propaganda deploys.

### Irrational propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda that operates through symbols, myths, emotions, images, spectacle, and appeals to identity. It bypasses rational evaluation entirely. Faster-acting but less durable than rational propaganda.
- **Detection**: Symbolic imagery, emotional appeals (fear, pride, anger, belonging), mythological narrative, spectacle, ritual, identity markers, charismatic delivery, music and visual atmosphere
- **Power function**: Short-circuits analytical processing (connects to logical fallacy category: emotional override). Irrational propaganda works faster than rational propaganda but is less stable — it requires continuous reinforcement. Its power function is to produce action before thought: the subject acts on feeling, then rationalizes afterward. Most effective when combined with rational propaganda (emotional mobilization + evidence-based justification = compound propaganda).

## Axis 5: Function — Undermining vs. Supporting

Classifies whether the propaganda operates with or against its stated ideals. This axis is derived from Stanley (2015) and is independent of Ellul's four axes.

### Undermining propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda that invokes an ideal (freedom, equality, fairness, democracy) in a way that structurally degrades the conditions necessary for that ideal's realization. The ideal is used to destroy itself. The speaker may be entirely sincere — they genuinely believe they are defending the ideal — which makes this form resistant to exposure.
- **Detection**: An ideal invoked to block the conditions the ideal requires. "Free speech" invoked to protect unlimited corporate political spending (which destroys equality of access free speech depends on). "Equal opportunity" invoked to block affirmative action (which addresses the structural barriers that prevent equal opportunity). "Democracy" invoked to defend systems that prevent effective democratic participation. Ask: "Does invoking this ideal protect or undermine the conditions the ideal requires?"
- **Power function**: The most dangerous form because it is structurally self-concealing. The invocation *appears* to be a straightforward defense of the ideal. Criticism appears to oppose the ideal itself. The propagandee who sincerely holds the ideal becomes the mechanism of the ideal's destruction. Connects to the Inversion of Stated Purpose — the ideal is stated, and its function is to undermine itself. Resistant to fact-checking (no factual claim is false), hypocrisy exposure (no hypocrisy exists — the speaker is sincere), and debunking (the mechanism operates at the structural level, not the claim level).

### Supporting propaganda
- **Description**: Propaganda that explicitly endorses the dominant ideology or interests without pretending to serve a broader ideal. Straightforward advocacy for a position, interest, or group. Less sophisticated but more honest — the propaganda and its purpose are aligned.
- **Detection**: Explicit advocacy, transparent interest alignment, "this is good for X and here's why" framing, direct appeals to group interest
- **Power function**: Functions through volume, repetition, and institutional amplification rather than through structural concealment. Less dangerous than undermining propaganda because it can be identified and contested. Its power function is primarily economic: those with more resources can produce more supporting propaganda, drowning out alternative positions through volume rather than structural self-concealment. Connects to the Division of Learning — the asymmetry is in amplification capacity, not in the structure of the argument.

## Pre-Conditions Diagnostic

Before classifying propaganda, assess why the target population is receptive.
Ellul identifies four structural pre-conditions for propaganda effectiveness.
If these conditions are absent, even well-crafted propaganda will fail.

### 1. Information environment
- **Condition**: Literacy, media access, and information abundance — the population must be reachable through mass communication channels
- **Diagnostic question**: "Can messages reach this population at scale? Through which channels?"
- **Power implication**: Paradoxically, *more* information access increases propaganda susceptibility (Ellul's education paradox). Information abundance overwhelms synthesis capacity, creating demand for pre-digested interpretations.

### 2. Atomization
- **Condition**: Breakdown of traditional communities, weakened social bonds, individualization — the population must be sufficiently isolated to seek belonging through mass identification
- **Diagnostic question**: "Does this population have strong community structures, or are individuals isolated and seeking group identity?"
- **Power implication**: Atomized individuals are receptive to propaganda that offers belonging. Strong communities provide alternative interpretive frameworks that resist propaganda. This explains why authoritarian movements target community institutions — they are structural defenses against propaganda.

### 3. Shared myths and stereotypes
- **Condition**: A pre-existing reservoir of cultural narratives, stereotypes, and emotional associations that propaganda can activate — the product of pre-propaganda conditioning over years or decades
- **Diagnostic question**: "What myths, stereotypes, or cultural narratives exist that this propaganda activates? When were they installed?"
- **Power implication**: No propaganda campaign works in a vacuum. Current propaganda activates dispositions installed by prior conditioning. Identifying the pre-propaganda reveals the long-term investment in narrative infrastructure.

### 4. Absence of critical distance
- **Condition**: No structural conditions enabling the population to step outside the propaganda environment and evaluate it — no independent media, no comparative framework, no tradition of critique
- **Diagnostic question**: "Does this population have access to perspectives outside the propaganda environment? Do they have structural conditions for critical distance?"
- **Power implication**: Critical distance is not a skill — it is a structural condition. The individual who lacks comparative frameworks, independent information sources, or social support for dissent cannot achieve critical distance through education alone (the education paradox). Counter-propaganda requires structural support, not just better arguments.

## How to apply this instrument

### During ANALYZE Step 1 (DECOMPOSE):
1. After stripping claims from the material, classify the material on each of the five axes
2. Use the format: `PROPAGANDA CLASSIFICATION: [agitation/integration], [political/sociological], [vertical/horizontal], [rational/irrational], [undermining/supporting/N/A]`
3. Note compound types — most real propaganda operates on multiple axes simultaneously. Integration + sociological + horizontal + rational + undermining is the signature of stable democratic propaganda
4. If the material does not appear to be propaganda, note this — the null case applies

### During ANALYZE Step 2 (SOURCE):
1. Use Axis 2 (origin) to determine whether the propaganda has an identifiable source
2. If sociological, note that the SOURCE step may not yield a single actor — the "source" is the cultural environment
3. Cross-reference with the positional lens: political propaganda from Position 1 (ruler) operates differently than from Position 3 (revolutionary)

### During ANALYZE Step 5 (INVERT):
1. Test the propaganda classification against the null case: "Is this actually propaganda, or is it genuine deliberation, education, information, or persuasion?"
2. The distinguishing criterion: propaganda serves the interests of the propagandist (political) or the existing power structure (sociological) at the expense of the audience's autonomous judgment. Genuine persuasion enhances autonomous judgment; propaganda replaces it.
3. Apply Axis 5 (function) with particular care — undermining propaganda is the hardest to distinguish from genuine advocacy because the speaker may be sincere

### During ANALYZE Step 6 (ABSENT):
1. Run the pre-conditions diagnostic to identify what structural conditions make the propaganda effective
2. The pre-conditions reveal what is *absent* — missing community structures, missing critical distance, missing alternative frameworks
3. Identify the pre-propaganda: what myths, stereotypes, or categories is the current propaganda activating? When were they installed?

### Compound detection:
The most effective propaganda combines multiple axes. Watch for these high-potency compounds:

- **Integration + sociological + rational + undermining**: The signature of stable democratic propaganda. No identifiable propagandist, no emotional manipulation, ideals invoked sincerely. Virtually invisible. Example: "meritocracy" as a framework that uses the ideal of equal opportunity to legitimize structural inequality.
- **Agitation + political + vertical + irrational**: Classic authoritarian propaganda. Identifiable leader, emotional mobilization, top-down messaging. Visible and therefore less durable. Example: wartime mobilization speeches.
- **Integration + horizontal + irrational + supporting**: Peer-enforced conformity through social identity. Example: consumer culture — brand loyalty, lifestyle aspiration, social status through consumption, enforced through peer evaluation.
- **Agitation + sociological + horizontal + undermining**: The most destabilizing compound. Grassroots mobilization that uses democratic ideals to undermine democratic institutions, spreading peer-to-peer through cultural environment. Example: populist movements that invoke "the people" to concentrate power.

> When propaganda combines three or more axes, flag it as structurally
> significant. Multi-axis propaganda is more resistant to counter-measures
> because each axis requires a different response — exposing the source
> (counters political origin) does nothing against sociological origin;
> fact-checking (counters rational appeal) does nothing against irrational
> appeal; exposing insincerity (counters supporting propaganda) does
> nothing against undermining propaganda where the speaker is sincere.
