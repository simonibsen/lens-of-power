# Instrument: Circumvention Typology

> Role: Analytical checklist for identifying observed circumventions
> in material under analysis. Consult `circumventions.md` for the full
> typology; this instrument provides detection guidance and the
> observational language convention.

SOURCE: Cross-extraction synthesis (Church Committee, Scott, Fanon, Graeber, Scheidel, Snyder, Alley, Piketty, Whitehouse, Hartmann, Cobb)

TAXONOMY LAYERS: All layers

ANALYTICAL USE: During ANALYZE Step 7 (SO-WHAT) and EXTRACT Step 3
(IDENTIFY PATTERNS) — check whether the material documents resistance,
counterforce, or circumvention of power mechanisms. During READ mode,
note observed circumventions in prose.

---

## Detection guide

For each circumvention type, what to look for in material:

### Transparency / Naming
- **Detection**: Investigation, exposé, leak, declassification, whistleblower action, FOI request, audit finding, journalism that names a hidden mechanism
- **Check**: Did the naming produce observable change? Was the naming captured or co-opted? Did formal response match structural change?

### Legal Challenge
- **Detection**: Lawsuit, constitutional challenge, regulatory enforcement action, statutory claim, injunction, settlement, precedent-setting ruling
- **Check**: Did the legal victory produce structural change or formal change only? Was the legal system itself the contested terrain (captured courts)?

### Collective Action
- **Detection**: Strike, boycott, mass protest, mutual aid network, coordinated refusal, union action, community organizing
- **Check**: Did collective action shift the compliance gradient? Was it disrupted through infiltration, co-optation, or selective concession?

### Institutional Reform
- **Detection**: New oversight body, transparency mandate, procedural safeguard, structural reorganization, accountability mechanism, inspector general action
- **Check**: Did reform persist beyond the political moment that created it? Was the reformed institution subsequently captured or defunded?

### Cultural Production
- **Detection**: Literature, art, film, music, intellectual work, or media that names power arrangements or produces alternative frameworks
- **Check**: Did the cultural production create shared vocabulary or remain confined to elite audiences? Was it commodified?

### Everyday Resistance
- **Detection**: Foot-dragging, false compliance, feigned ignorance, work slowdowns, gossip, anonymous acts, hidden transcript activity
- **Check**: Is the resistance individually deniable? Does it operate below the threshold of formal confrontation? Does it negotiate terms rather than transform structure?

### Economic Alternative
- **Detection**: Debt cancellation, commons governance, cooperative ownership, mutual aid, alternative currency, non-market exchange, progressive taxation
- **Check**: Does the alternative operate outside the dominant accumulation logic? Has it been enclosed or absorbed?

### Electoral / Democratic
- **Detection**: Electoral mobilization, voting rights campaign, ballot initiative, legislative action, democratic reform, popular referendum
- **Check**: Did democratic action produce substantive policy change or was it absorbed through implementation capture?

### International Pressure
- **Detection**: Sanctions, treaty invocation, ICC proceedings, UN action, transnational advocacy, diplomatic isolation, international media attention
- **Check**: Was pressure applied consistently or selectively? Was it circumvented through sovereignty claims or sanctions evasion?

### Catastrophic Leveling
- **Detection**: Mass mobilization war, revolution, state collapse, pandemic, or other catastrophic disruption that compressed inequality
- **Check**: This type is descriptive — it documents an outcome, not a strategy. Note whether the material describes catastrophic compression and whether post-catastrophe institutions maintained the compressed distribution.

## Language convention

When documenting circumventions, use observational language:
- "has been observed to" (not "works" or "is effective")
- "may" (not "will" or "does")
- "appeared to" (not "succeeded in")
- "conditions under which X was observed" (not "requirements for X")
- "has been observed to fail when" (not "fails when")

This convention serves two purposes:
1. **Epistemic honesty**: The framework documents what has been observed, not what is guaranteed. Circumventions operate in complex systems with unpredictable outcomes.
2. **Non-prescription**: The framework is an analytical tool, not a manual. It describes what has been seen, not what should be done.

---

## Integration with analysis

When a circumvention is identified in material:

1. **Name it**: Identify which type(s) from `circumventions.md` apply
2. **Assess outcome**: Did the circumvention appear to succeed, fail, or produce mixed results?
3. **Check failure modes**: Does the material document any of the known failure modes for this type?
4. **Check power response**: Does the material document the power system's response?
5. **Flag new types**: If the material documents a circumvention that does not map to any existing type, flag it as a candidate for addition to `circumventions.md`
6. **Cross-reference patterns**: Link the circumvention to the specific patterns it counteracted (COUNTERACTS field)
