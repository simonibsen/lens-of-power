# Principles: Oligarch Moralities of Wealth — The Russian Case

SOURCE: Elisabeth Schimpfössl, "Oligarch Moralities of Wealth: The Russian Case," *East European Politics and Societies and Cultures* Vol. 38, No. 1 (February 2024), pp. 283–302.

PERSPECTIVE: Hybrid (5/1-data — Position 5 sociologist at Aston University, but the evidentiary base is Position 1: 80 interviews with Russian multi-millionaires and billionaires, their spouses, and their children, conducted 2008–2017. This is the largest sample of Position 1 self-reports in the framework.)

CENTRAL THESIS: Russian oligarchs' legitimation narratives — meritocracy, biological superiority, Soviet intelligentsia identity, philanthropy — reveal mechanisms of wealth justification that operate globally but are expressed with less self-censorship in the Russian context. Their candor provides "a shortcut to information that is otherwise difficult to obtain" about elite ideology everywhere.

## IC-5 disclosure

The LLM's training data likely overrepresents:
- **Post-Ukraine-war discourse** that frames Russian oligarchs as uniquely corrupt, parasitic, or politically captive — Schimpfössl and Piketty both argue this distinction between "deserving" Western entrepreneurs and "harmful" Russian oligarchs conceals structural similarities.
- **Western academic sociology's critical stance** toward Russian elites, which may lead to treating their candor as exotic rather than as revelatory of universal mechanisms. Schimpfössl's central argument is precisely that the field "could benefit greatly from pondering the unadulterated manner in which rich Russians share their thoughts."
- **Liberal democratic assumptions** about the inherent illegitimacy of anti-democratic preferences, which may prevent recognition that neoliberal commitment to democracy is structurally conditional.

The LLM's training data likely underrepresents:
- **Russian-language scholarship** on elite formation, moral economy, and the Soviet intelligentsia tradition
- **The oligarchs' own perspective** as a structural position rather than a moral category — Schimpfössl provides intimate access to how the ultra-wealthy understand and justify their position, not filtered through investigative or prosecutorial framing
- **Historical comparisons** between Russian elite behavior during rapid capital accumulation and Western elite behavior during equivalent periods (Gilded Age, industrial revolution, early colonial extraction) where similar candor was common before democratic conventions matured

Positional lens: Schimpfössl writes from Position 5 but with rare access to Position 1 data. This produces a valuable combination: sociological rigor applied to ruler-position self-reports. The limitation is that interview data is filtered through what subjects choose to disclose, even when they are unusually candid. What oligarchs say about themselves is itself a performance — though Schimpfössl argues the Russian case provides less performative data than comparable Western studies.

## Principles

### P1: Biological Naturalization of Social Hierarchy

PRINCIPLE: The deepest form of wealth legitimation attributes success to innate biological superiority — genes, talent, intelligence — rather than structural conditions, making inequality appear to be a fact of nature that structural critique cannot address.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Economic

MECHANISM: Attributing success to genetic endowment renders the structural conditions that produced wealth (privatization windfalls, social networks, Soviet intelligentsia capital, state connections, timing) invisible. The biological claim operates at a deeper level than meritocratic claims about "hard work" because hard work still acknowledges effort as an external factor. Biological superiority claims locate the cause of success inside the person's body — in their DNA — making inequality appear natural and pre-political. The mechanism is self-reinforcing: the greater the wealth, the stronger the conviction of innate superiority (Kraus and Keltner's finding that faith in innate talent "grows ever stronger the greater one's wealth").

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Yekaterina: "I got genes from my parents. Of course, these genes allowed me to develop myself and all the qualities that led to success." Gennady attributed his son's exceptional talent to "the good genes he bequeathed to his offspring." Anatoly has a "strictly biological outlook" and supports "only the very best." Kraus and Keltner found that in the United States, those "born into the most powerful dynasties see themselves as simply 'the most capable.'" Cerón observed Mexican elites assuming that "thanks to their genetic disposition, they should excel at golf, whereas indigenous Mexicans will, by their very nature, never get anywhere close." Schimpfössl identifies post-war Soviet biological thinking as a historical driver: once Stalin's ban on genetics was lifted, "biological interpretations of history and human behavior were enthusiastically embraced, especially by the most conservative-leaning parts of the Soviet intelligentsia."

GENERALIZES TO: Silicon Valley ideology attributing success to innate cognitive ability ("10x engineers"); hereditary aristocracy's claims to noble blood; academic elitism attributing intellectual capacity to individual talent rather than institutional access; any context where structural advantage is attributed to the biological constitution of the advantaged.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Piketty P4 (meritocratic myth) to its deepest form — from "I worked hard" (external effort) to "I was born superior" (innate constitution). Confirms Axiom 3 Mode 2 (visible mechanism, hidden contingency — the person's success is visible, but its contingency on structural conditions is concealed by the biological attribution). Confirms Axiom 6 (language as first instrument — the biological vocabulary forecloses structural analysis by relocating causation from social structure to DNA).

STATUS: observed (the beliefs); inferred (their structural function as legitimation)

### P2: Pre-Capitalist Identity as Capitalist Legitimation

PRINCIPLE: Elites who rise through disruptive economic change incorporate pre-capitalist cultural identities into their self-presentation, providing moral depth and historical continuity that the new economic order alone cannot supply.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Institutional

MECHANISM: Neoliberal capitalism provides wealth but not legitimacy depth. The "shady" origins of rapid wealth accumulation (1990s privatization) create a legitimation deficit. By claiming Soviet intelligentsia heritage, Russian oligarchs anchor their status in a pre-capitalist moral tradition — intellectual rigor, cultural refinement, public virtue, "inner strength and decency" — that predates and transcends the methods by which they actually acquired wealth. The pre-capitalist identity performs two functions: (1) historical continuity ("we were always elite — our intelligentsia backgrounds gave us a tangible head-start"), and (2) moral vocabulary ("we are cultured, disciplined, principled") that neoliberalism's market vocabulary cannot provide. The identity synthesis is specific: "rich Russians' identities shifted from a firm sense of belonging to the Soviet intelligentsia to becoming the drivers of neoliberal capitalism. Neoliberal ideologies, however, lacked the historical and symbolic gravitas required for sustainable identity-building. In response, rich Russians incorporated elements of their Soviet past into their twenty-first-century identities."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Aven's father was "a professor of computer science, which came close to the Soviet equivalent of nobility." Yekaterina's parents were scientists whose path was "facilitated by post-war educational reforms." Almost all interviewees "emphasize the legacy of their Soviet intelligentsia upbringing, to which they believe they owe an inner strength, a work ethic, and discipline." "References to intelligentsia backgrounds bestow on the rich an aura of inherited culture and intellectual heft." "Given the prestige the intelligentsia still enjoys in Russian society, reviving Soviet intelligentsia values today allows the rich to apply a righteousness to their status that is supposedly anchored in the past."

GENERALIZES TO: British industrial magnates adopting aristocratic manners and purchasing country estates; Silicon Valley billionaires adopting countercultural or academic identities (Burning Man, Stanford professorships); Gulf state rulers emphasizing Bedouin heritage; any nouveau riche class that stabilizes its position by grafting cultural capital from the tradition it claims to continue onto the economic position it actually occupies.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Appearance as Structural Terrain — the intelligentsia identity IS the appearance, the oligarchic wealth defense is the reality. Extends Moral Infrastructure of Economic Power — the intelligentsia moral vocabulary provides the moral infrastructure for the economic position. Confirms Formal Transformation, Structural Continuity — the formal system changed from socialism to capitalism, but social elites maintained continuity through cultural capital conversion.

STATUS: observed

### P3: Philanthropy as Governance Substitution

PRINCIPLE: Elite philanthropy functions not as a supplement to democratic governance but as a mechanism for replacing it — substituting unaccountable private decision-making for democratic policy processes while gaining tax advantages, social legitimacy, and policy influence.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional, Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: (1) Philanthropy provides tax advantages that reduce the oligarch's contribution to public revenue. (2) Philanthropic spending directs social resources according to the donor's preferences rather than democratic priorities — in Russia, 90% of charitable donations go to children, reflecting elite preferences rather than an assessment of social need. (3) The philanthropic institution becomes a vehicle for social influence without democratic accountability: "philanthropists not only see their tax bills vastly reduced, but they also gain influence on social policy, without ever having been authorized to do so through any democratic procedure." (4) The narrative of "giving back" legitimizes the wealth concentration that produced the need for charity — Aven's view that philanthropy would "change people's attitudes towards private property." (5) "Philanthro-capitalism" creates controlled forms of activism administered from above — Liniya Zhizni charity had super-rich shareholders fundraise among ordinary people to "enlighten and educate them," with the explicit goal of creating "the social institution of charity" as substitute for state provision. The mechanism is self-reinforcing: as philanthropy grows, it justifies further reduction of state social provision, creating more need for philanthropy.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Aven: philanthropy should change "people's attitudes towards private property." Sysuev explained the rationale for having "people of modest means come up with the money": their "involvement would enlighten and educate them." The "core aim is to eventually supplant state provision of social care by creating a controlled form of 'activism' that is instigated and administered from above." Moshkovich spent $50M building a school for gifted children plus $150M endowment; Svetakov built a school for disabled children and was asked by a politician: "Why do you need that? Let's do a school for gifted children." Reich, McGoey, and others cited for systematic critique: "not even the most generously funded large-scale charity foundations are up to scratch with regards to foreseeing the long-term implications of their philanthropic interventions."

GENERALIZES TO: The Gates Foundation influencing global health policy outside democratic oversight; Koch network philanthropy funding ideological infrastructure; Zuckerberg's $100M Newark schools initiative as private governance of public education; Sackler family philanthropic reputation laundering. Any context where private wealth substitutes for democratic decision-making under the banner of generosity.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Piketty P6 (progressive taxation as democratic counterforce) in reverse — philanthropy is the mechanism by which oligarchs circumvent the tax system while gaining social legitimacy. Confirms Inversion of Stated Purpose — philanthropy states generosity, functions as governance capture and legitimation. Extends Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence — philanthropy is one mechanism through which oligarchic preferences substitute for democratic priorities without visible contradiction.

STATUS: observed (the mechanism); inferred (the structural function as governance substitution)

### P4: The Candor Differential and the Ruler's Hidden Transcript

PRINCIPLE: Elites privately hold beliefs — biological superiority, anti-democratic preferences, contempt for democratic process — incompatible with their public legitimation narratives. The degree of suppression varies with the maturity of democratic conventions, producing a "candor differential" that reveals the ruler's hidden transcript when studied comparatively.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Surveillance & Information

MECHANISM: In established democracies, elites learn that certain private beliefs "are not fit for public consumption." U.S. billionaires are "mindful of the chasm between their own political views and U.S. public opinion and, hence, act behind the scenes." In less established democracies (Russia, and to some extent Mexico and Finland), these suppressive conventions are weaker, producing more candid expression of the same underlying beliefs. The ruler's hidden transcript is structurally parallel to but functionally opposite from the subordinate's hidden transcript (Scott): both maintain beliefs that differ from their public performance, but the subordinate conceals resistance while the ruler conceals the actual ideological basis of domination. When the ruler's hidden transcript leaks — through candor, captured documents, or weakened social convention — it reveals what the public legitimation narrative (meritocracy, democratic commitment, philanthropy) was designed to conceal.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: "Rich Russians' adherence to biological supremacy and openly expressed preferences for private capital over democracy — are the central findings of my analysis." "Western elites' guardedness and caution have resulted in empirical accounts that center around only some of the components of their meritocratic beliefs, emphasizing their hard work, willpower, and discipline. The other side of the coin — inherent traits such as intelligence and talent — has received much less scholarly attention." Page et al. documented that U.S. billionaires keep "their funding and lobbying efforts very close to their chests." Finnish tech elites (Kantola and Kuusela) expressed similar anti-democratic sentiments but "elites in the West tend to be far more cautious, nuanced, and subtle in their public presentation, no matter how much they may harbor similar ideas."

GENERALIZES TO: Leaked corporate communications (internal Facebook/Meta memos revealing contempt for user privacy); the Powell Memo (a ruler-position document that was confidential, not public); Ziklag donor planning documents; any context where the private discourse of the powerful differs from their public-facing narrative, and cross-context comparison can reveal the suppressed content.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Hidden Transcript from the subordinate (Scott) to the ruler — the powerful also maintain a hidden counter-discourse that differs from their public performance. The candidness of Russian oligarchs functions methodologically as what leaked documents function investigatively: access to what the public transcript conceals. Confirms Appearance as Structural Terrain — the candor differential IS the terrain; Western elites maintain the appearance, Russian elites partially collapse it. Confirms the Double Performance — both sides are performing, but the candor differential reveals what the ruler's performance conceals.

STATUS: observed (the differential in candor); inferred (the structural parallel to Scott's hidden transcript)

### P5: Neoliberal-Authoritarian Compatibility

PRINCIPLE: Neoliberal economic doctrine is committed to democracy only conditionally — when democratic processes produce outcomes favorable to private capital. When democracy threatens redistribution, regulation, or constraints on accumulation, neoliberal actors prefer authoritarian implementation.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional, Legal & Regulatory, Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: Neoliberalism requires the legal protection of private property, the enforcement of contracts, and the facilitation of capital accumulation. Democracy is instrumentally useful for these goals when the electorate does not demand redistribution. When democratic processes threaten capital — through progressive taxation, nationalization, labor protections, or regulation — neoliberal actors support authoritarian alternatives that can impose market reforms without democratic constraint. The mechanism operates bidirectionally: (1) democracy is acceptable when it protects private capital; (2) dictatorship is acceptable when democracy threatens private capital. The Chilean model (Pinochet's military dictatorship implementing "the world's purest neoliberal experiment") is the paradigm case and the explicit reference point for Russian oligarchs.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Aven "encouraged Putin to embrace Reaganomics combined with Pinochet-type dictatorial control to push through further radical economic reforms." Kokh would change only one thing about the 1990s: "he would base the privatization program around a pension reform, as Pinochet had done." "Neoliberals commit to democracy only as long as it is feasible to do so; if not, then dictatorial measures are considered fair game." Finnish tech elites "were convinced that they would easily do a better job than any democratically elected government." "Nobody ever batted an eyelid regarding his [Aven's] overt flirtation with dictatorial measures." The late-Soviet intelligentsia "closely followed the free-market expansion in Chile in the 1970s and 1980s. They admired General Augusto Pinochet for his radical anti-Communism and the resolute implementation of the world's 'purest' neoliberal experiment."

GENERALIZES TO: IMF structural adjustment programs imposed on unwilling democratic populations; the "Washington Consensus" imposed through conditional lending regardless of democratic preferences; corporate governance structures where shareholder primacy overrides stakeholder democracy; technocratic institutions (central banks, trade courts) designed to insulate economic policy from democratic interference.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence with the critical boundary condition: compatibility holds only while democracy does not threaten capital. When the two conflict, the oligarch chooses authoritarianism. Confirms Winters P5 (civil oligarchy as democracy with oligarchic overlay) but adds what happens when the overlay fails — the oligarch does not accept democratic constraints but seeks an alternative political vehicle. Extends Axiom 7 (stated purpose vs. function) — the stated commitment to "free markets AND free people" reveals its conditionality when tested: democracy is valued instrumentally, not intrinsically.

STATUS: observed (the preference and its historical instantiation); inferred (the structural conditionality as a general property of neoliberal ideology)

## Patterns identified

### Existing patterns confirmed

- **Appearance as Structural Terrain** (ESTABLISHED): the entire paper documents the gap between oligarchic self-presentation (meritocratic, deserving, philanthropic) and the structural conditions of wealth acquisition. The candor differential IS the terrain — Russian oligarchs partially collapse the gap that Western oligarchs maintain.
- **Inversion of Stated Purpose** (ESTABLISHED): philanthropy states generosity, functions as governance substitution and wealth legitimation. "Neoliberal doctrine routinely holds faulty governance responsible for the flaws of capitalism and outsources crisis management to the state while at the same time expecting the very same state to implement policies and regulations that guarantee the stable growth of private capital."
- **Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence** (ESTABLISHED): confirmed with a boundary condition: coexistence holds only while democracy does not threaten capital. "Irrespective of whether in a liberal democracy such as that of the USA or an authoritarian state like Russia's, where neoliberal doctrine gets the upper hand, wealth inequality tends to soar."
- **Moral Infrastructure of Economic Power** (PRELIMINARY): the intelligentsia identity provides the moral vocabulary for the oligarchic position. The "good"/"bad" wealth distinction (Piketty, Sherman, Freund) operates as moral infrastructure that prevents structural analysis by relocating critique from the system to individual moral character.
- **Formal Transformation, Structural Continuity** (SUPPORTED): Soviet intelligentsia → oligarch: the formal system changed from socialism to capitalism, but social elites maintained continuity through cultural capital conversion — intelligentsia backgrounds provided "cultural and social capital through the Soviet education system, their parents' libraries, and their influential networks."
- **The Double Performance** (SUPPORTED): Western elites perform meritocracy and democratic commitment for public consumption while privately holding biological superiority beliefs and anti-democratic preferences. Russian elites partially break the performance.
- **Hidden Transcript** (SUPPORTED): EXTENDED to the ruler's side. Scott documents the subordinate's hidden transcript; Schimpfössl documents the ruler's hidden transcript — the beliefs the powerful hold privately (biological superiority, Pinochet admiration, contempt for democratic process) that cannot be expressed publicly without undermining their legitimating narrative. The candor differential reveals the hidden transcript through cross-context comparison rather than through leaked documents.

### IC-1 flags

- **Axiom 3 (extension)**: The candor differential complicates Mode 2 (visible mechanism, hidden contingency). Russian oligarchs sometimes make the contingency partially visible by openly claiming biological superiority and anti-democratic preferences — yet the system remains stable. This suggests that when biological naturalization succeeds, the contingency is RE-NATURALIZED: the audience may hear the biological claim and accept it as explanation rather than recognizing it as legitimation. The biological framing converts what is contingent (wealth from privatization) into what appears natural (wealth from genes), even when stated openly.
- **Axiom 10 (confirmation of Sincerity Problem)**: These oligarchs sincerely believe in their biological superiority. Understanding this belief as legitimation does not neutralize it because the belief is sincere, not strategic. Strongly confirms Stanley P5 (the sincerity problem) and Ellul P4 (the education paradox) — the most effective legitimation operates through genuine conviction, not cynical manipulation.

## Connections to existing principles

- **Piketty P4** (meritocratic myth): Schimpfössl P1 extends to the biological level — from "I worked hard" to "I was born superior." The same legitimation function (concealing structural determinants) operates through a deeper mechanism (biological naturalization is harder to critique than effort-based claims because it appears to invoke scientific fact).
- **Winters P5** (democracy-oligarchy compatibility): Schimpfössl P5 adds the boundary condition — compatibility is conditional on democracy not threatening capital. Aven's Pinochet advocacy documents what happens at the boundary.
- **Guriev-Rachinsky P3** (illegitimacy as structural vulnerability): Schimpfössl documents the RESPONSE to the illegitimacy Guriev identifies — the 60% of Russians who view oligarch wealth as dishonest are met by oligarchic legitimation narratives (biological superiority, intelligentsia identity, philanthropy) designed to neutralize that perception.
- **Scott P1** (hidden transcript): Schimpfössl P4 extends from the subordinate to the ruler — the powerful also maintain beliefs that differ from their public performance. The structural parallel is exact: both subordinate and ruler have a public and a private discourse, maintained for different strategic reasons.
- **Fanon P4** (relay class): Schimpfössl P2 shows the inverse — where Fanon's relay class inherits the colonizer's position and reproduces extraction, Russian oligarchs inherit the intelligentsia's cultural position and convert it into capitalist legitimation. Cultural capital flows in the same direction (from prior system to current occupants) but serves legitimation rather than extraction.
- **Graeber P2** (debt as moral instrument): Schimpfössl P3 extends the moral infrastructure — where Graeber shows debt framed as moral obligation, Schimpfössl shows philanthropy framed as moral generosity. Both convert structural power relationships into moral narratives that constrain critique.
- **Mishra P4** (the relay of legitimation): Schimpfössl P2 provides a domestic version — where Mishra's postcolonial elites relay Western legitimation, Russian oligarchs relay Soviet intelligentsia legitimation into the capitalist system.
