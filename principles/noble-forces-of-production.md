# Principles: Forces of Production

SOURCE: David Noble, *Forces of Production: A Social History of Industrial Automation* (1984)
POSITION: 5 (historian of technology with explicit political commitment to labour)
SOURCE TYPE: primary_document

---

## P1: Technology Design as Social Choice Presented as Technical Necessity

PRINCIPLE: When multiple viable technical designs exist for a given problem, the design chosen by those with funding power is subsequently presented as the only technically viable option, because the rejected alternatives are denied development resources and thus cannot demonstrate viability.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Economic, Institutional

MECHANISM: Two or more technically feasible approaches exist simultaneously. Institutional actors with funding power (military, large corporations, research universities) select the design that aligns with their control objectives. The chosen design receives massive development funding while alternatives are starved of resources. The chosen design's subsequent dominance is then cited as evidence of its inherent technical superiority — a circular justification.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Record-playback (R/P) and numerical control (N/C) were both viable approaches to programmable machine tool automation. R/P was demonstrated successfully by GE in 1947. The Air Force invested over $62 million in N/C development and created an artificial market by procuring 105 N/C machines at government expense, while providing zero funding for R/P development. Noble: "All technological options are not born equal... only those which do not fit within this larger framework of preferred development are required to pass rigorous tests of immediate technical and economic viability. And, since these are not really technical or economic tests at all, but political and cultural ones, they are predestined to fail."

GENERALISES TO: AI development. Multiple approaches to AI-mediated professional work exist — some that augment worker judgment (the "informate" model from Zuboff's earlier work) and some that replace it (the current dominant model). The approach that receives venture capital and corporate investment becomes dominant and is then retrospectively naturalised as the only viable path. The question of whether AI could be designed to empower rather than deskill workers is foreclosed by the funding pattern, not by technical constraints.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Appearance as Structural Terrain (technical superiority narrative masks social choice). Extends Inversion of Stated Purpose (efficiency rationale masks control motive). Provides the foundational empirical case for the framework's claim that technology's distributional effects are choices, not laws.

## P2: The Control Premium — Management Will Sacrifice Efficiency to Retain Control

PRINCIPLE: Management systematically chooses less efficient technologies when more efficient alternatives would distribute control to workers, because maintaining hierarchical control is valued over maximising productivity.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional, Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: When a technology keeps decision-making on the shop floor (in workers' hands), management rejects it even when it demonstrably reduces costs and increases productivity. Management then obscures the economic sacrifice through manipulated cost projections, concealed data, and ideological justifications about "progress."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: GE consulting engineer Orrin Livingston: "Management liked numerical control better. It meant they could sit in their offices, write down what they wanted, and give it to someone and say, 'do it.' ... With numerical control, there was no need to get your hands dirty, or argue." Earl Troup of GE: "With record playback, the control of the machine remains with the machinist. ... With numerical control, there is a shift of control to management — and why shouldn't we have control over it?" Ford's Ralph Kuhn developed a shop-floor programming method requiring only 20% of the cost and 25% of the time of the computer-based method. Ford management ordered him to abandon it because it "lent itself to shop floor programming by workers rather than engineers and thus strengthened the union's bargaining position." A "massive cover-up" followed; Kuhn's superior was transferred; those who knew "knew also to keep quiet if they wanted to hold on to their jobs."

GENERALISES TO: The choice between AI systems that augment professional judgment and AI systems that replace it. The augmentation model may be more productive (professionals using AI tools to enhance their existing expertise) but gives workers continued autonomy. The replacement model may be less productive in absolute terms but transfers control to the employer who owns the AI system. Noble documents that the control premium — the efficiency sacrifice management will accept to retain hierarchical authority — is a consistent historical pattern.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Axiom 7 (stated purpose ≠ actual function). Strongest Position 1 evidence in the corpus: the actors who made the choice state the control motive explicitly, on the record.

## P3: The Twin Wars Convergence

PRINCIPLE: External military threats and internal labour resistance converge to produce technology designed for worker displacement, because the overlapping elite that manages foreign military operations also manages domestic production, and both problems are solved by removing human judgment from the process.

LAYERS: All six

MECHANISM: The "higher circles of the military, the corporations, the universities, and the science establishment had come to overlap extensively and to coalesce into an elite stratum." This overlapping elite confronted both problems simultaneously and saw automation as solving both: military command requires removing intermediary judgment; management requires removing worker discretion. The same technology (servomechanisms, computers, numerical control) serves both purposes. Charles E. Wilson's formulation: "Russia abroad, labor at home."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Extensive personnel overlap documented: Forrestal (Dillon Read to Secretary of Defense), Charles Wilson (GM to SecDef), military officers to corporate boards. "Generals talked, when on the subject of labor, of the necessity of securing the home front for the (cold) war abroad." Cold War propaganda equated labour militancy with communist threat. Harold Laski: "the engineering factory is a unit of the Army, and the worker may be in uniform without being aware of it."

GENERALISES TO: Contemporary AI development funded by both military (defence contracts, DARPA) and corporate interests, with the same personnel rotating between defence, tech companies, and policy positions. The AI surveillance-industrial complex documented in the framework's DHS analysis is a direct descendant of the military-industrial automation Noble describes.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Perpetual Threat as Perpetual Justification (external threat justifies domestic control). Extends The Boomerang (military command structures applied domestically). Provides historical depth for the Capture Pipeline.

## P4: Military Subsidy as Market Creation

PRINCIPLE: State procurement creates artificial markets for technologies that serve military and managerial control objectives but would not survive commercial cost-benefit analysis, because public funding insulates the technology from market discipline and shifts development costs to the public while concentrating benefits privately.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional

MECHANISM: The Air Force did not merely subsidise existing N/C development — it created demand where none existed. Through the Machine Tool Modernisation programme, the Air Force procured 105 N/C machines at $62 million in public funds, installed them in contractor plants at government expense, and paid contractors to learn to use them. The machine tool industry's "passivity was transformed into active interest" overnight. The Air Force further made N/C adoption a condition of defence contracts: "If a firm wanted to stay in the defense business, it had to subscribe to the command performance." Military specifications (five-axis control, interchangeability) drove the technology toward complexity that served military logistics but excluded 95% of commercial metalworking firms.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: "Whatever the problems in production, whatever the cost, the public would pay the bill." Jack Rosenberg: "no aerospace company... risk their own funds in the purchase of a numerical control system." By the mid-1960s, machine tool costs inflated by the military market outpaced labour costs. Japanese and German manufacturers, locked out of US military contracts, developed simpler systems for the commercial market. By 1978 the US was a net importer of machine tools.

GENERALISES TO: Government AI procurement (documented in the framework's DHS surveillance analysis and AI lobbying analysis). Public funding of AI development through defence contracts, SBIR grants, and tax incentives creates markets that would not exist commercially and shapes the technology toward military-compatible designs (centralised, surveillance-capable, command-oriented).

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Extends Manufactured Scarcity as Control (inverted — manufactured demand rather than scarcity). Confirms Inversion of Stated Purpose (national defence framing for what also served private control objectives).

## P5: The Suppression of Alternatives

PRINCIPLE: Independent inventors who develop simpler, cheaper, more accessible technological alternatives are systematically marginalised not by competitive failure but by exclusion from the institutional networks that allocate development resources, because the alternative's viability threatens the investment in the dominant approach.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional, Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: Noble documents a repeating cycle: an inventor develops a viable alternative, demonstrates it, encounters hostility or indifference from established institutional actors, fails to secure funding, and disappears from the historical record. The pattern repeats across multiple independent actors over decades, confirming that the suppression is structural rather than incidental.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Sponaugle (1944): patented R/P system, "apparently never actually developed." Thomas (1948-1968): developed simpler digital control, "never commercially developed. He did not have enough money and had even less political clout." When American Machinist omitted his work from a history: "It's fascinating, even though a bit sad, to learn how a good idea faded out of sight... However, we have decided that our readers don't have to know about that part of it." Edwards (1951-1953): Air Force monitor who pushed for R/P, was "just a voice in the wilderness." Gossard (1960s): developed system machinists could learn "in a few hours"; prototype components "gather dust on his office shelf." Later discovered his method was adopted — in Japan. Kuhn (1970s): demonstrated method taking 10 minutes vs. days; ordered to abandon it, superior transferred, "massive cover-up."

GENERALISES TO: Open-source AI development, cooperative AI models, and public AI infrastructure proposals that exist as viable alternatives but cannot compete with the capital concentration behind proprietary models. The pattern Noble documents — viable alternatives suppressed through institutional exclusion rather than competitive failure — applies directly.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Axiom 8 (absence is significant). Provides the strongest empirical documentation in the corpus of how alternatives are foreclosed — not refuted but denied resources.

## P6: Standardisation as Lock-In

PRINCIPLE: Industry standardisation processes convert contingent technology choices into structural constraints that foreclose alternatives, because once a standard is set, the cost of deviating exceeds the cost of complying with an inferior solution.

LAYERS: Institutional, Economic, Legal & Regulatory

MECHANISM: The Aircraft Industries Association standardised N/C around the most complex, expensive configuration and APT as the universal programming language — requiring large computers and professional mathematicians. The DoD then required APT capability as a precondition for government contracts. Alternative programming systems (NUFORM, which required "one-half to one-third the time") were excluded. "The users of continuous path controlled machine tools were now locked into the computer-generated punched paper tape medium."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: McDonnell Automation rejected NUFORM: "APT is the bible here." Access to APT development restricted to consortium members. "Manuals were restricted to authorized personnel, programmers themselves had to sign them out, and they could not be taken out of the plant or copied." Small firms (83% of metalworking industry) owned only 22% of N/C equipment. The panel's justification: "normally any standardization hurts someone initially but... the ultimate benefits of standardization far outweigh the objections of those who chose a different path."

GENERALISES TO: AI model standards, API specifications, and safety frameworks being developed by the dominant AI companies. The firms that set the standard control who can participate and on what terms.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Extends Capture Pipeline (standardisation process controlled by incumbent firms). Confirms Division of Learning (asymmetric access to technical knowledge through restricted standards).

## P7: The Control-Productivity Contradiction

PRINCIPLE: Management's use of technology to maximise control over workers systematically undermines the productivity gains that technology was supposed to deliver, because quality production with complex machinery depends on the skills, initiative, and cooperation of the workers that the control strategy destroys.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional

MECHANISM: At GE-Lynn, management set N/C operator wages two levels below conventional lathe operators, refused to acknowledge operator skills, and attempted to lock overrides. The result: highest scrap rate, highest turnover, lowest productivity, and lowest morale in the plant — "the 'bottleneck' in aircraft engine production." Management "set out to deskill, discipline, and displace the very people upon whose knowledge and goodwill the optimum utilization, and thus cost-effectiveness, of N/C ultimately depended." Machine downtime in 1959 hovered around 80%.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: "The traditional management strategy proved inescapably to be doubly — and literally — counterproductive." Henry Sharpe (Brown and Sharpe): "N/C takes skill out of jobs, but increases worker anxiety, it reduces the labor force, but requires more management. Hence, there is no net gain from N/C." Boeing machinist: "I felt so stifled, my brain wasn't needed any more... I feel like my head's asleep."

GENERALISES TO: The AI deskilling trajectory. If AI is deployed to replace professional judgment rather than augment it, and the deskilled professionals are needed to correct AI errors, review output, and handle edge cases, the same contradiction emerges: the system depends on the very capacity it is designed to eliminate.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Braverman's Destruction of Craft principle with empirical evidence. Strongest documentation in the corpus of how the control imperative is self-defeating.

## P8: The Override as Contested Terrain

PRINCIPLE: When automated systems include manual override capabilities, those overrides become the primary site of struggle between management's drive for control and workers' exercise of skill, because overrides are simultaneously necessary for quality production and the main remaining channel through which workers can influence their work.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional, Surveillance & Information

MECHANISM: At TRW, operators collectively set all overrides at 50% ("the 50 percent syndrome"). Management programmed feeds at 120%. At Brown and Sharpe, overrides at 70%; management compensated at 130%. At Hamilton Standard, management installed hidden switches to disconnect overrides "without the operator knowing it, so he thinks he has reduced the rate... but actually he hasn't." At GE-Lynn, operators used Phillips screwdrivers to reset the override dial face. Management could not simply lock overrides because they were necessary "for the production of quality parts."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: "It's a game," one supervisor pointed out. Boeing machinist: "You have to use overrides to get good parts... Without them, the parts would all be scrap."

GENERALISES TO: AI-mediated professional work. The "override" in the AI context is the human's ability to reject, modify, or override AI output. If this capacity is removed (as efficiency demands), quality may suffer. If it is retained, workers retain a channel of autonomous judgment. The contested terrain shifts from the physical override dial to the professional's editorial authority over AI output.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Compliance Gradient (overrides as the material form of partial compliance). Extends Hidden Transcript (covert collective agreements on override levels). Confirms Double Performance (operators performing compliance while exercising control).

## P9: Technological Determinism as Depoliticisation

PRINCIPLE: The ideology of technological determinism — treating technology as an autonomous force following a singular, inevitable path — depoliticises design choices that are actually social decisions, because when technology appears to have its own logic, the question of who benefits becomes invisible.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional

MECHANISM: Technology serves "at once as convenient scapegoat and universal panacea — a deterministic device of our own making with which to disarm critics, divert attention, depoliticize debate, and dismiss discussion of the fundamental antagonisms and inequities." People "tend to confront technology as an irreducible brute fact, a given, a first cause, rather than as hardened history, frozen fragments of human and social endeavour." The "inevitability" framing demobilises opposition: unions internalized it ("Historic experience dictates that such resistance is futile") and workers "clung to the belief that their sacrifices were made for the sake of progress."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Noble's core formulation: "if a technology develops in one direction and becomes ubiquitous in that form, it is probably less a reflection of its actual technical or economic superiority than of the magnitude of the power which chose it, and of the dominance of the cultural norms which sanction that power." The "Darwinian ideology of technological progress... does not so much side with technical or economic superiority as with social power."

GENERALISES TO: The "AI is inevitable" discourse. Noble's critique applies directly to the contemporary framing where AI deployment under concentrated private ownership is treated as a natural force to which society must adapt. The question "who should control AI and for whose benefit?" is foreclosed by the inevitability frame.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Appearance as Structural Terrain. Extends Fisher's Capitalist Realism (the closure of alternatives through the discourse of inevitability) with specific industrial-historical evidence. Confirms Braverman's Machinery as Capital's Authority (machine fetishism depoliticises ownership relations).

## P10: Wiener's Warning — The Structural Impotence of Individual Dissent

PRINCIPLE: Individual moral dissent by technically knowledgeable actors is structurally insufficient to redirect technology development, because the ideas "are very much in the air" and abstention by one actor merely transfers the work to others.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional, Economic

MECHANISM: Norbert Wiener recognised that automation would be used against workers and tried to intervene: he refused military contracts, published warnings, and wrote directly to UAW president Walter Reuther offering to help labour "steal a march upon existing industrial corporations." But he simultaneously recognised the futility: "my own passiveness in this matter will not, in the face of it, produce a passiveness in other people who may come by the same ideas." Reuther never responded.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Wiener's letter to Reuther: "I do not wish to contribute in any way to selling labor down the river, and I am quite aware that any labor which is in competition with slave labor, whether the slaves are human or mechanical, must accept the conditions of work of slave labor." Wiener adopted "conscientious non-cooperation, which excluded him henceforth from the circles of power."

GENERALISES TO: Individual AI researchers who raise ethical concerns about AI deployment. The structural lesson from Wiener is that individual dissent — even from a figure as prominent as the founder of cybernetics — does not redirect the technology. Only organised institutional counterforce (which Reuther failed to provide) could have changed the trajectory.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: Confirms Axiom 10 (understanding without institutional leverage is structurally impotent). Confirms Gramsci's specification: understanding resists only when organised through institutional infrastructure. Strongest case in the corpus of a technically knowledgeable actor whose perfect understanding produced exclusion from power rather than the capacity to redirect it.
