# Principles: Magnifica Humanitas (Leo XIV on AI)

SOURCE: Pope Leo XIV / The Holy See, Encyclical Letter *Magnifica Humanitas*,
"On Safeguarding the Human Person in the Time of Artificial Intelligence"
(15 May 2026). Principles extracted from Chapters 3–5 (¶90–228).

PERSPECTIVE: Position 1 (institutional sovereign — the magisterium of the
Catholic Church, ~1.4bn members, a diplomatic corps, a 135-year body of
social doctrine), writing in an adopted Position 5 (observer / moral
analyst) voice. The principles below are the framework's reading of the
encyclical's *descriptive* claims about how contemporary power operates;
they are deliberately separated from the encyclical's *normative*
theological principles (dignity as *imago Dei*, subsidiarity, the common
good — Chapter 2), which are prescriptions rather than mechanisms and are
not extracted here.

CENTRAL THESIS (as relevant to power): Technological power — especially
AI — has become predominantly private and transnational, exceeding the
capacity of states to govern it; it operates through opacity, the
architecture of visibility, the imposition of "truth" by the
resource-rich, a hidden chain of exploited labor, and the dematerialized
extraction of data ("colonialism in another form"); and it is accompanied
by a "culture of power" that automates and normalizes force. The
encyclical's distinctive descriptive move is that *technology is never
neutral* and that *technical power is not a title to govern* — both are
naturalizations to be actively contested.

## Principles

### P1: Technology as Non-Neutral Inheritance

PRINCIPLE: A technology is never neutral, because it takes on the
characteristics of those who devise, finance, regulate, and use it. Every
technical tool embodies choices and priorities through what it measures,
ignores, and optimizes, and through how it classifies people and
situations. The "neutral tool" framing is therefore not a description but
a concealment: it hides the embedded interests behind an appearance of
objectivity, and it misdirects ethical scrutiny toward *use* ("a tool to
be used well") and away from *design* (what vision of the person is built
into the data and models).

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Economic, Institutional

MECHANISM: The encyclical distinguishes two ethical questions — whether a
system is used for good or bad purposes, and how the system is designed
and what vision of the human is embedded in it. The "neutral tool" claim
collapses the second into the first, so that the values built into
measurement, optimization, and classification escape examination. Because
the tool is presented as neutral, its outputs inherit an unearned
authority of objectivity (see P3), and the interests of those who built
it operate without being named.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶9 ("technology is never neutral, because it takes on
the characteristics of those who devise, finance, regulate and use it");
¶92–93 (the technocratic paradigm — efficiency, control, profit as the
standard by which everything is judged); ¶104 ("every technical tool
embodies choices and priorities through what it measures, ignores and
optimizes… ethical discernment cannot be limited to asking whether we are
using a system for good or bad purposes; it must also examine how that
system is designed").

GENERALIZES TO: Any artifact or system presented as a neutral instrument.
The principle: when a technology is defended as "just a tool," locate the
priorities embedded in what it measures, optimizes, and ignores, and ask
whose characteristics it carries. Applies to algorithms, metrics (GDP,
credit scores), standards, and infrastructures.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Noble (*Forces of Production*) — technology
design as social choice presented as technical necessity; refines Axiom 7
(the "neutral tool" claim is the stated purpose; the embedded interest is
the function); a Position 1 statement of a Position 5 finding.

STATUS: observed (the encyclical states it directly); inferred (the
generalization beyond AI).

### P2: The Privatization of Technological Sovereignty

PRINCIPLE: When the principal drivers of a general-purpose technology are
private, often transnational, actors whose resources and capacity to
intervene exceed those of many governments, technological power takes on a
predominantly "private" character. Control over platforms, infrastructure,
data, and computing power sets the conditions for access, the rules of
visibility, and the very possibility of participation — functions
historically performed by accountable public institutions, now relocated
to unaccountable private ones.

LAYERS: Economic, Institutional, Surveillance & Information

MECHANISM: The encyclical observes that where the state once guided
innovation, the main drivers are now private parties endowed with
resources and intervention capacity surpassing those of many governments.
Control over the digital substrate (platforms, infrastructure, data,
compute) lets these actors "set the conditions for access, determine the
rules of visibility and shape the very possibilities for participation."
Concentrated, this power "tends to become opaque and evade public
oversight." The mechanism is a transfer of a governing function — setting
the terms of participation — from public to private hands without a
corresponding transfer of accountability.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶5 ("the main drivers of development are private,
often transnational, parties… endowed with resources and the capacity to
intervene that surpass those of many Governments. Technological power thus
takes on an unprecedented, predominantly 'private' aspect"); ¶95 (control
of platforms, infrastructure, data, compute rests "not with States, but
with major economic and technological actors"); ¶108 ("AI tends to amplify
the power of those who already possess economic resources, expertise and
access to data").

GENERALIZES TO: Any domain where a function once performed by accountable
public institutions migrates to private actors whose scale exceeds the
states nominally regulating them (payment rails, search/discovery,
cloud infrastructure, standards-setting).

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Zuboff (Division of Learning) and the
Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence pattern; extends both with the explicit
claim that private technological power now *exceeds state capacity* — a
sovereignty-transfer framing, not merely an influence claim.

STATUS: observed.

### P3: Opacity Voids Accountability

PRINCIPLE: When a system's internal processes cannot be reconstructed —
when systems are "cultivated, not built," such that even their designers
have only limited understanding of their functioning — responsibility for
their outputs cannot be assigned, and the exclusion they produce is
cloaked as neutral objectivity, against which it becomes difficult to
appeal. Opacity is not merely a technical limitation; it is a
redistribution of accountability away from those who deploy the system,
and a shield that converts contestable decisions into apparently
objective facts.

LAYERS: Surveillance & Information, Institutional, Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: Two moves combine. First, the system's opacity ("more
cultivated than built"; internal representations "remain, at present,
unknown") makes it "harder to assign responsibility and correct errors."
Second, the system "present[s] itself as neutral and objective," so that
"the exclusion of the vulnerable becomes cloaked in a veneer of neutrality
and objectivity, against which it becomes difficult to raise objections."
Together: the decision is unaccountable (no one can be made to "account"
for it) *and* unappealable (it appears objective). "Injustice goes
unnoticed."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶98 ("all of us, including those who design them,
possess only a limited understanding of their actual functioning… current
AI systems are more 'cultivated' than 'built'"); ¶103 (exclusion "cloaked
in a veneer of neutrality and objectivity, against which it becomes
difficult to raise objections"); ¶105 (accountability as "the possibility
of identifying who must 'account' for decisions… and, when necessary,
challenge them").

GENERALIZES TO: Any decision system whose opacity prevents the assignment
of responsibility while its outputs carry an aura of objectivity
(algorithmic scoring, actuarial models, bureaucratic black boxes).

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Division of Learning (those who are scored
cannot see the scoring), Inversion of Stated Purpose (a "neutral, objective"
system functioning to exclude without appeal), and Appearance as Structural
Terrain (objectivity as the operating terrain).

STATUS: observed.

### P4: Technical Power Is Not a Title to Govern

PRINCIPLE: The assumption that technical capability automatically confers
the authority to set the rules — that those who can build and control a
technology are thereby entitled to govern its use — is a naturalization
that must be actively contested. The capacity to build a thing and the
authority to govern its use are distinct; conflating them transfers a
political question (who decides?) into a technical one (who can?).
"Disarming" a technology means breaking the monopolistic control that
makes the conflation seem natural and reopening the technology to political
contestation and the plurality of human cultures.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional, Legal & Regulatory

MECHANISM: The encyclical names the conflation directly and prescribes its
contestation: "To disarm means discrediting the assumption that technical
power automatically confers the right to govern." Left uncontested, the
conflation lets "those who control data, infrastructure and computing
power" impose rules "presented as necessary and inevitable," and lets the
"alignment" of AI to "values" substitute for any politically contestable
decision — so that "those who control AI will impose their own moral
vision, which will become the invisible infrastructure of these systems."
The counter-move is to re-separate capacity from authority and return the
governing question to political contestation.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶106 (change otherwise "governed only by technocratic
thinking and presented as necessary and inevitable, ultimately imposing
rules shaped by those who control data, infrastructure and computing
power"); ¶107 (the "alignment" warning — "those who control AI will impose
their own moral vision, which will become the invisible infrastructure of
these systems… A more moral AI is not enough if that morality is
determined by a few"); ¶110 ("To disarm means discrediting the assumption
that technical power automatically confers the right to govern").

GENERALIZES TO: Any domain where expertise or capability is treated as
self-authorizing — technocracy generally; the "the engineers will figure
it out" deferral; expert bodies whose technical competence is taken to
settle normative questions that are properly political.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: novel framing — isolates the slippage from *capacity*
to *authority* that the framework had not named as such. Relates to
Axiom 3 (hegemonic naturalization — the conflation is "prior to challenge")
and to The Unprecedented as Sanctuary (technical novelty as a claim to
operate outside political constraint). Counter-power principle: it names
the move to be resisted and the form of resistance ("disarming").

STATUS: observed (the encyclical's claim); inferred (the generalization).

### P5: The Architecture of Visibility

PRINCIPLE: Control is exercised not only through explicit prohibition but
through the architecture of visibility — what is amplified or rendered
invisible, what is rewarded or penalized. By shaping what can be seen and
said, this architecture shapes opinion and choice and produces conformity
and self-censorship without any command being issued. The attention
economy is its engine: platforms designed to capture time and attention by
exploiting psychological vulnerability treat the person as a means, making
dependency the product rather than a side effect.

LAYERS: Surveillance & Information, Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: "Control is exercised not only through explicit prohibitions,
but also through the architecture of visibility: what is amplified or
rendered invisible, what is rewarded or penalized, ultimately shapes
opinions and choices, fostering conformity and self-censorship." Because no
prohibition is issued, the control is deniable and appears as the neutral
operation of a platform; because it operates on what is *visible*, it
shapes the inputs to judgment rather than overriding judgment directly.
The attention-economy business model supplies the incentive: systems
"designed to capture users' time and attention, exploiting their
vulnerabilities and weakening their inner freedom," so that "when business
models thrive on human weakness, the person is treated as a means rather
than as an end."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶171 ("control is exercised not only through explicit
prohibitions, but also through the architecture of visibility… fostering
conformity and self-censorship"); ¶170 (the "digital attention economy";
"when business models thrive on human weakness, the person is treated as a
means rather than as an end"); ¶136 (those who control platforms can
"present a particular vision of reality as desirable").

GENERALIZES TO: Any curated information environment where the curator
controls salience without issuing commands (feed-ranking, search,
recommendation, editorial gatekeeping). The principle: where there is no
explicit prohibition, examine what is made visible and invisible, rewarded
and penalized.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Division of Learning, Anticipatory Obedience
(self-censorship as pre-compliance), and Real-Time Narrative Saturation;
sharp, citable Position 1 phrasing of the curation-as-control mechanism.

STATUS: observed.

### P6: Resource-Enabled Imposition of Truth

PRINCIPLE: Actors commanding sufficient technological, economic, and
human-capital resources can impose what others accept as true — about
humanity, the world, the meaning of existence, even God — independent of
the truth of the claims. This "pure power detached from truth" degrades the
shared factual ground that democracy requires, tending toward a condition
in which the distinction between fact and fiction, true and false, no
longer functions for the governed (Arendt).

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: "Those who command powerful technological and economic
resources, along with substantial human capital for intervention, possess
significant capabilities for influencing cultural change… This is pure
power detached from truth, which subtly or overtly imposes what it wishes
others to accept as true." The capacity to set the terms of the true is
distributed by resources, not by argument; truth becomes a function of
who can amplify and repeat rather than of verification. The encyclical
quotes Arendt: the ideal subjects of such a regime are those "for whom the
distinction between fact and fiction… and the distinction between true and
false… no longer exist."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶133 ("This is pure power detached from truth, which
subtly or overtly imposes what it wishes others to accept as true"); ¶134
(the Arendt citation; "indifference to the truth leads, slowly but surely,
to a descent into totalitarianism").

GENERALIZES TO: Any information environment where the capacity to set what
counts as true tracks resources rather than evidence — propaganda systems,
saturated advertising, well-funded disinformation, and the manufacture of
manufactured consensus.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Arendt (ideology as a closed system; the
destruction of the fact/fiction distinction) and Stanley (material
inequality produces epistemic inequality); refines Axiom 6 (the first
instrument of control is the capacity to set the terms of the true).

STATUS: observed.

### P7: Data Colonialism — Extraction Under the Pretext of Aid

PRINCIPLE: Health, genetic, and demographic data extracted from
structurally fragile, geopolitically marginal regions — often gathered
"under the pretext of aid, research or innovation" — become "the new rare
earths of power," conferring structural leverage over those populations'
futures: the power to anticipate, to shape needs and markets, and to decide
"to whom medicines, investments and protections will be allocated." The
digital age reproduces colonial extraction in dematerialized form; it
ceases to dominate bodies and instead appropriates lives as exploitable
information.

LAYERS: Economic, Surveillance & Information, Physical & Coercive

MECHANISM: "Colonialism assumes new forms. It no longer dominates only
bodies, but appropriates data, transforming personal lives into exploitable
information." The extraction targets "regions, especially those marked by
structural fragility and limited geopolitical relevance," and the data,
"once aggregated and analyzed, can be used to train predictive models,
guide investment strategies, anticipate crises and, above all, determine
who and what is deemed to matter." The aid/research framing is the cover
that legitimizes collection; the structural leverage is the function.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶178 ("colonialism assumes new forms… the new 'rare
earths' of power… Those who control the health data of entire peoples —
often collected under the pretext of aid, research or innovation — possess
a structural leverage over the future… Otherwise, the digital age will not
be post-colonial, but colonial in another form").

GENERALIZES TO: Any extraction in which a small visible benefit (aid,
free service, research participation) provides cover for a larger
appropriation that confers structural leverage over the providing
population (data-for-services platforms; biobanking; "free" digital
infrastructure in exchange for data rights).

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Hickel et al. (the Aid-Drain Asymmetry — a
small visible transfer concealing a larger extraction, here in data rather
than value) and The Boomerang (extraction methods concentrated at the
periphery); confirms Division of Learning (the extracted population cannot
see or audit the models built on its data).

STATUS: observed (the encyclical's claim); inferred (the causal leverage
and the generalization).

### P8: The Constitutive Labor Chain of the Digital Economy

PRINCIPLE: Every "immediate and flawless" computational output rests on a
hidden chain of human labor — data-labelers, model-trainers, and
content-moderators (disproportionately young women, often handling
disturbing material), and the miners (including children) who extract the
rare-earth materials — whose "bodies are scarred, injured and worn down so
that computational flow may continue uninterruptedly." The concealment of
this chain is what permits the system to present itself as immaterial and
advanced; the apparent immateriality is an *effect of hiding the labor*,
not evidence of its absence.

LAYERS: Economic, Physical & Coercive, Surveillance & Information

MECHANISM: "Nothing in the world of AI is immaterial or magical. Every
seemingly immediate and flawless response is the result of a long chain of
mediation, involving vast networks of natural resources, energy
infrastructure and, above all, people." The labor is structurally
necessary ("a significant part of the digital economy's functioning relies
on the silent work of millions") and structurally hidden ("largely unseen
activities… deliberately hidden"). The concealment is load-bearing: it is
what lets "advanced" and "civilized" societies experience the output as
clean. The bodies bearing the cost are the constitutive substrate of the
apparently immaterial system.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶173 ("Nothing in the world of AI is immaterial or
magical… data labeling, model training and content moderation, often
involving disturbing material… young people, predominantly women… children
and adolescents work in dangerous conditions… The bodies of these people
are scarred, injured and worn down so that computational flow may continue
uninterruptedly… built on a chain of exploitation that remains deliberately
hidden").

GENERALIZES TO: Any system whose appearance of seamless automation depends
on hidden human labor (the "automation last mile"; gig work behind
"AI" services; the supply chain behind any "clean" consumer product). The
principle: where a system appears immaterial or fully automated, locate the
hidden labor whose concealment produces that appearance.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Constitutive Violence (the system is constituted
through the bodily cost borne by the labor chain, not merely correlated
with it) and complements Braverman (labor as the indeterminate substrate
that control regimes exploit); a vivid Position 1 statement.

STATUS: observed.

### P9: Threshold-Lowering by Automation of Force

PRINCIPLE: Delegating lethal or otherwise irreversible decisions to
automated and opaque systems lowers the threshold for resorting to force.
Automation does so by accelerating decisions past the moral timeframe that
judgment requires, by reducing victims to data and removing "the face" of
the human from target selection, and by diffusing responsibility so that no
identifiable agent must "account" for the act ("the machine" absorbs the
blame). Automation does not remove the inhumanity of force; it makes force
more feasible and accustoms a population to its inevitability.

LAYERS: Physical & Coercive, Surveillance & Information, Institutional

MECHANISM: Three pathways combine. (1) **Speed past judgment** — "speed
and efficiency should never be the supreme motivating force for the
irreversible decisions made in the context of war"; automation
"expedite[s] the decision-making processes" past the moral timeframe.
(2) **De-facing the target** — "any technology that facilitates attacks
without seeing the face of human beings lowers the moral threshold of
conflict," "reducing victims to data." (3) **Responsibility diffusion** —
"when a decision to strike becomes automated or opaque, the risk of
abdicating responsibility increases"; accountability collapses "into 'the
machine'." The combined effect is that "AI… can only bring about conflict
more quickly and render it more impersonal, lowering the threshold for
resorting to violence."

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶197 ("the growing ease with which autonomous weapons
systems can be deployed makes war more 'feasible' and less subject to human
control"); ¶198 ("lowering the threshold for resorting to violence… accustom
us to the idea that violence is inevitable and needs only to be optimized");
¶199–200 (the three criteria — personal responsibility, moral timeframe,
identification/protection of civilians — and the requirement that lethal
force "cannot be delegated to opaque or automated processes").

GENERALIZES TO: Any automation of a consequential, irreversible decision
where speed, abstraction, and responsibility-diffusion combine to lower the
threshold for taking the action (automated trading, algorithmic sentencing
or benefit-denial, automated content takedown). The principle: automation
of a grave decision tends to make the decision easier to take, independent
of whether it makes the decision better.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: novel distinctive principle for the Physical & Coercive
layer; confirms Inversion of Stated Purpose (defense reframed as threat
prediction). It relates thematically to the Economy of Violence (both
concern the calibration of force) but describes a distinct mechanism —
threshold-lowering through speed, abstraction, and responsibility-diffusion,
not the concentrate-then-withdraw calibration of that pattern — so it is
*not* counted as corroboration of it (the Economy of Violence pattern
carries a standing retirement review; a thematic link should not be used
to prop it up). The "threshold-lowering" mechanism is new to the corpus.

STATUS: observed (the encyclical / Holy See claim); inferred (the
generalization beyond warfare).

### P10: False Realism as Manufactured Resignation

PRINCIPLE: The claim that war — or domination more generally — is an
inevitable feature of human nature functions not as a description of the
world but as a mechanism that produces resignation. By recasting the
question from "whether" to "how and when," dismissing peace and
alternatives as naïve, and foreclosing the search for them, "false realism"
(Realpolitik) manufactures the acquiescence it claims merely to observe. It
is anticipatory obedience at civilizational scale: the inevitability
framing is the instrument, not an observation.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: The encyclical isolates the framing as the mechanism: "a false
realism, based not only on the prevailing mentality of force, but on the
cultural and anthropological belief that war is an inevitable part of human
nature… the concern is no longer the search for peace… but rather how and
when to take military action." The framing produces a "polite form of
resignation, often disguised as realism," which "dismisses peace and
dialogue as utopian or irrational." The naturalization ("things have always
been this way… it will always be so") forecloses the alternative before it
can be considered — the move is prior to challenge.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: ¶205 ("a false realism… the cultural and anthropological
belief that war is an inevitable part of human nature… what is truly
irresponsible is Realpolitik, the form of political 'realism' that sows in
consciences and in society an attitude of resignation to the inevitability
of war"); ¶212 ("a subtle temptation… a polite form of resignation, often
disguised as realism").

GENERALIZES TO: Any "there is no alternative" framing that naturalizes a
contingent arrangement and produces resignation (capitalist realism;
"that's just how politics works"; "you can't fight city hall"). The
principle: inevitability claims about human affairs are frequently
mechanisms of resignation rather than descriptions, and should be examined
for what alternative they foreclose and whom the foreclosure serves.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: confirms Anticipatory Obedience (pre-compliance with an
order before it is demanded) and Fisher's Hegemonic Realism (the
foreclosure of alternatives from the thinkable); refines Axiom 3
(naturalization as the deepest mode of invisible power). The "false realism"
naming, from a Position 1 source, sharpens the mechanism.

STATUS: observed.

## New patterns identified

### Technical-Authority Conflation (candidate — not created)

The naturalized slippage from *technical capability* to *governing
authority* — the assumption, underlying P4, that those who can build and
