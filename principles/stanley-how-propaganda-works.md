# Principles: How Propaganda Works

SOURCE: Jason Stanley, *How Propaganda Works* (2015, Princeton University Press)

PERSPECTIVE: Position 5 (external observer/academic) — Yale philosopher of language. Works from the analytic philosophy tradition with extensive engagement with Du Bois, Charles Mills, and social epistemology.

CENTRAL THESIS: The most dangerous form of propaganda is not false speech but speech that uses democratic ideals to undermine the conditions necessary for those ideals to function. This "undermining propaganda" is effective because it exploits the gap between what a society believes about itself and how it actually operates — and is made possible by "flawed ideologies" that produce insensitivity to the evidence that would expose the gap.

## IC-5 disclosure

The LLM's training data likely overrepresents Stanley's generally favorable academic reception and the broadly progressive framework he operates within. Critiques likely underrepresented:
- **Conservative and classical liberal critiques**: Stanley's framework implies that "free speech" invocations protecting corporate spending are inherently propaganda. From a classical liberal perspective, this begs the question — it assumes the progressive interpretation of free speech is correct and the libertarian one is ideological distortion. Stanley's own framework could be applied to his own position.
- **Post-structuralist critiques**: Stanley assumes there are non-ideological positions from which propaganda can be identified. A Foucauldian critique would argue all discourse is ideology — there is no epistemic vantage point from which "flawed" ideology can be distinguished from "correct" ideology without reproducing a new form of epistemic authority.
- **Empirical gaps**: Stanley works from philosophical argument rather than empirical study. The mechanisms he identifies are plausible but the evidence is largely illustrative rather than systematic.

Positional lens: Stanley writes from epistemic privilege — a tenured philosopher at an elite university — analyzing how epistemic systems fail for those with less privilege. This produces theoretical rigor but means the experience of being *subject to* propaganda is reconstructed philosophically rather than reported directly.

## Principles

### P1: Undermining Propaganda

PRINCIPLE: The most dangerous form of propaganda uses an ideal to undermine the conditions necessary for that ideal's realization. "Undermining propaganda" invokes a value (freedom, equality, fairness) in a way that structurally degrades the conditions the value requires. It is more dangerous than simple deception because it can be sincerely held — the speaker genuinely believes they are defending the ideal — making it resistant to fact-checking, exposure, or counter-argument.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Institutional, Legal & Regulatory

MECHANISM: The mechanism operates through the gap between a society's stated ideals and its actual practices: (1) A society endorses an ideal (e.g., free speech, equal opportunity). (2) The ideal requires certain conditions to function (e.g., free speech requires rough equality of access to discourse). (3) Speech invoking the ideal is used to block the conditions the ideal requires (e.g., "free speech" invoked to protect unlimited corporate political spending, which destroys the equality of access free speech depends on). (4) The invocation is structurally self-defeating — the ideal is used to destroy itself — but this is invisible to participants because the invocation *appears* to be a straightforward defense of the ideal. The sincerity is genuine. The structural effect is destructive.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: "Free speech" invoked to defend Citizens United; "equal opportunity" invoked to block affirmative action; "fairness" invoked to require "balanced" coverage treating science and denial as equivalent.

GENERALIZES TO: Any invocation of an ideal that structurally undermines its conditions — "transparency" invoked to produce information overload; "choice" invoked to produce markets so complex that meaningful choice is impossible; "democracy" invoked to defend systems that prevent effective democratic participation. The mechanism: *the ideal is the weapon against itself*.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: provides the philosophical mechanism for the Inversion of Stated Purpose pattern — explains *how* the inversion works through sincere invocation that structurally defeats itself; extends Axiom 7: the gap between stated purpose and function need not involve deception — it can be produced by sincere belief within a flawed ideology

STATUS: observed (examples documented); inferred (philosophical mechanism)

### P2: Flawed Ideology as Epistemic Closure

PRINCIPLE: A "flawed ideology" is a belief system that produces insensitivity to the evidence that would falsify it — self-sealing epistemic closure. It is the *enabling condition* for undermining propaganda: without it, the structural self-defeat in P1 would be visible. Meritocratic ideology, for example, makes structural inequality unrecognizable as structural — evidence of discrimination is reinterpreted as evidence of individual failure.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative

FRAMEWORK STATUS: refines Axiom 3 — invisibility of contingency produced not by concealment but by epistemic closure where evidence is available but unrecognizable; extends Axiom 6 — ideology operates through conceptual vocabulary determining what counts as evidence

STATUS: inferred

### P3: Material Inequality Produces Epistemic Inequality

PRINCIPLE: Wealth concentration determines not just who speaks but what is thinkable. Material inequality produces epistemic inequality because those with more resources have disproportionate capacity to shape public discourse — funding research, controlling media, defining the terms of debate, and determining what questions are asked.

LAYERS: Economic, Thought & Narrative, Institutional

MECHANISM: Material resources translate into epistemic influence through: (1) **Research funding**: Think tanks, endowed chairs, and grants shape which questions are investigated and which answers amplified (connects to Powell Memo P4). (2) **Media control**: Ownership and advertising determine which perspectives are broadcast. (3) **Term-setting**: The wealthy fund the conceptual infrastructure — vocabulary, think tank reports, expert testimony — defining the parameters of "reasonable" discourse. (4) **Epistemic authority**: Wealth confers credibility; the wealthy are assumed competent, their perspectives treated as more authoritative. The result: the epistemic environment in which democratic deliberation occurs is itself shaped by the inequality deliberation is supposed to address.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Citizens United allowing unlimited corporate political spending; Koch brothers' funding of academic programs; the revolving door between industry and regulatory agencies shaping what regulators consider relevant.

GENERALIZES TO: Any system where material concentration shapes the epistemic environment — corporate influence on scientific research, pharmaceutical industry influence on medical knowledge, tech industry influence on AI regulation discourse. *Whoever funds the questions determines the answers.*

FRAMEWORK STATUS: connects Piketty P8 (political capture feedback loop) to the epistemic domain — wealth shapes discourse, which shapes policy, which shapes wealth; provides philosophical grounding for the Division of Learning pattern — asymmetric access to the means of producing knowledge and defining relevance

STATUS: inferred

### P4: Reasonableness as Exclusion Mechanism

PRINCIPLE: The demand for "reasonable" discourse can function as exclusion — defining structural critique as "unreasonable" protects the status quo by excluding challenges before they are heard. What counts as reasonable reflects what is thinkable within the dominant ideology, not what is analytically valid. The mechanism is self-reinforcing: those with institutional power define the range, and that range excludes challenges to their power.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Axiom 6 to the meta-level — the *rules about what language is acceptable* shape what thoughts enter public discourse; provides philosophical grounding for the Newspeak mechanism in democratic contexts — vocabulary reduced by institutional gatekeeping rather than by fiat

STATUS: inferred

### P5: The Sincerity Problem

PRINCIPLE: Undermining propaganda is resistant to exposure because its practitioners can be entirely sincere. A person who invokes "free speech" to defend Citizens United is not lying — they genuinely believe they are defending free speech. This sincerity makes undermining propaganda impervious to standard counter-propaganda tools (fact-checking, hypocrisy exposure, debunking) because there is no deception to expose.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative

MECHANISM: The standard model of propaganda assumes deception: the propagandist says X while believing not-X. Counter-propaganda therefore exposes the deception. Stanley argues this fails for undermining propaganda: (1) the speaker genuinely endorses the ideal they invoke; (2) the speaker genuinely does not perceive the structural undermining; (3) the flawed ideology operates on the speaker as much as the audience. Fact-checking addresses the wrong level — it checks whether specific claims are true, not whether the invocation of the ideal undermines the ideal's conditions. Hypocrisy exposure fails because there is no hypocrisy. *Sincerity is not evidence of non-propaganda.*

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Defenders of Citizens United genuinely believe they defend free speech; opponents of affirmative action genuinely believe they defend equal opportunity; "colorblind" racial ideology sincerely held by people who genuinely believe they oppose racism.

GENERALIZES TO: Any context where debunking fails because belief is sincere and structurally functional — climate denial by sincere free-market believers, vaccine hesitancy by sincere autonomy advocates, austerity by sincere fiscal conservatives.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: critical challenge to Axiom 10 (understanding as resistance) — if undermining propaganda operates through sincere belief, naming the mechanism may be insufficient because the mechanism does not depend on deception; understanding may need to operate at the level of ideology critique rather than factual correction

STATUS: inferred
