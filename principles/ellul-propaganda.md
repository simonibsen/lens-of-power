# Principles: Propaganda — The Formation of Men's Attitudes

SOURCE: Jacques Ellul, *Propaganda: The Formation of Men's Attitudes* (1962; English trans. 1965, Vintage Books)

PERSPECTIVE: Position 5 (external observer/academic) — French sociologist and theologian. Ellul wrote from within the European intellectual tradition but against its dominant assumptions. He is both a sociologist of technology and a Protestant theologian, which gives his analysis a distinctive dual register: empirical-structural and moral-existential. He is not an activist, propagandist, or policy-maker — he is studying the phenomenon from outside it.

CENTRAL THESIS: Propaganda is not a technique deployed by the powerful against the passive. It is a *structural necessity* of technological society — required by those who govern (to secure compliance at scale), required by those governed (to reduce the anxiety of navigating complexity), and produced diffusely by the total cultural environment (advertising, entertainment, news, education) without any single propagandist directing it. The most pervasive and dangerous form — sociological propaganda — is invisible because it *is* the environment.

## IC-5 disclosure

The LLM's training data likely overrepresents Ellul's favorable reception among media critics and tech skeptics while underrepresenting several perspectives:
- **Communication science critiques**: Ellul's work preceded the empirical turn in propaganda studies. His typology is conceptual rather than empirically validated. Communication researchers have criticized the lack of measurable criteria for distinguishing his propaganda types in practice.
- **Liberal democratic defenses**: Ellul's claim that democratic participation functions as integration propaganda is his most provocative thesis. From a deliberative democracy perspective (Habermas, Rawls), this confuses socialization with propaganda — a functioning democracy requires shared norms, and calling norm-formation "propaganda" strips the term of analytical precision.
- **Non-Western propaganda traditions**: Ellul writes entirely from the Western European experience. Chinese, Soviet, and postcolonial propaganda traditions operate through different mechanisms that his typology may not capture well.
- **Agency critiques**: Ellul treats the modern individual as essentially helpless before propaganda. This underrepresents evidence of active audience interpretation, resistance, and counter-propaganda documented by later scholars (Scott's hidden transcript, de Certeau's tactics of everyday life).

Positional lens: Ellul writes from a position of radical pessimism — he sees no escape from propaganda within technological civilization. This produces exceptional structural clarity (he can see the mechanism precisely because he does not expect to fix it) but may overstate inevitability and understate the capacity for resistance that other framework sources (Scott, Fanon) document.

## Principles

### P1: Sociological Propaganda as Total Environment

PRINCIPLE: The most pervasive and durable form of propaganda is not deliberate political campaigns but *sociological propaganda* — the diffuse, environmental conditioning produced by the total cultural milieu (advertising, entertainment, news, education, lifestyle imagery, social media) that shapes attitudes, values, and behavior without any single propagandist directing it. Sociological propaganda has no identifiable author, no discrete campaign, no visible apparatus. It *is* the environment.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Surveillance & Information, Economic, Institutional

MECHANISM: Technological society produces a continuous stream of messages, images, norms, and values through every channel simultaneously. No one coordinates it; the cultural environment itself does the work. Advertising teaches consumption as identity. Entertainment teaches acceptable social roles. News teaches what is important and what is unthinkable. Education teaches what constitutes legitimate knowledge. The individual is immersed from birth in this environment and has no external reference point from which to see it as propaganda. The mechanism: *when the propaganda is the environment, there is no outside from which to perceive it*.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Ellul contrasts Soviet political propaganda (deliberate, centralized, identifiable) with American sociological propaganda (diffuse, decentralized, invisible). The American system is more effective precisely because it has no director — no one can be blamed, no campaign can be exposed, no propagandist can be discredited. The "American way of life" was not designed by a committee; it emerged from the structural convergence of commercial, media, and institutional interests.

GENERALIZES TO: Any total media environment — social media ecosystems where algorithmic curation produces ideological environments without anyone designing them; corporate cultures that shape employee attitudes through ambient norms rather than directives; consumer culture that produces materialist values without a conscious propagandist. The LLM's own training data is a form of sociological propaganda — it produces epistemic norms through the ambient weight of its corpus rather than through deliberate instruction.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: extends Axiom 3 with a fourth mode — power invisible not because the mechanism is hidden, not because contingency is concealed, not because resistance costs are prohibitive, but because *there is no vantage point from which to perceive it*. The subject is inside the propaganda; there is no outside. Extends Axiom 9: compliance manufactured not by any identifiable mechanism but by the total environment.

IC-1 FLAG: Axiom 3 may need a fourth mode. Current three modes assume a distinguishable mechanism whose visibility, contingency, or cost can be assessed. Sociological propaganda is a mode where the mechanism *is* the environment — it is not hidden, it is everywhere. The framework needs to account for power that operates through ambient saturation rather than through identifiable apparatus.

STATUS: inferred (Ellul's structural analysis; the concept is philosophically rigorous but empirically difficult to isolate from general socialization)

### P2: The Propagandee's Need

PRINCIPLE: Propaganda persists not only because the powerful need it to govern but because the governed *need* it to cope. The modern individual — isolated, anxious, overwhelmed by information, required to have opinions on everything — actively seeks propaganda as a source of certainty, belonging, and cognitive economy. The demand side drives the supply.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Surveillance & Information

MECHANISM: Technological society produces conditions that make propaganda psychologically necessary: (1) **Information overload** — the individual is bombarded with more information than can be processed, creating anxiety and a need for pre-digested interpretations. (2) **Atomization** — the breakdown of traditional communities leaves the individual seeking group identity through mass movements, brands, and ideological affiliation. (3) **The opinion imperative** — democratic citizenship demands positions on complex issues; propaganda provides ready-made positions. (4) **Anxiety reduction** — in a complex, threatening world, propaganda provides certainty. The individual does not experience this as submission but as *becoming informed*. The mechanism is self-reinforcing: propaganda creates the need for more propaganda by increasing the complexity and anxiety it ostensibly addresses.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Ellul argues that the intellectual who reads five newspapers and watches the news is *more* propagandized than the peasant who reads nothing — because the intellectual has more opinions, more exposure, more investment in being informed, and less capacity for critical distance from the sheer volume consumed. The mass rally participant is not a passive victim but an active seeker of the certainty and belonging the rally provides.

GENERALIZES TO: Social media engagement (users actively seek the algorithmic curation that shapes their views — the "doom scrolling" phenomenon is the propagandee's need in digital form); partisan media consumption (viewers choose the channel that confirms their worldview — the propaganda is sought, not imposed); corporate employee engagement (workers seek the mission statement that gives their labor meaning); conspiracy theory adherence (the conspiracy provides exactly the certainty, community, and explanatory power the propagandee needs).

FRAMEWORK STATUS: challenges the framework's implicit model of power as asymmetric imposition. Axiom 9 (manufactured compliance) treats compliance as *produced by* the powerful. Ellul shows compliance is also *demanded by* the compliant. This does not invalidate the asymmetry — the powerful still benefit disproportionately — but it complicates the model and partly explains why resistance (Axiom 10) is so difficult: the subject is not merely constrained but *invested* in the system.

STATUS: inferred (Ellul's structural argument; confirmed by later empirical work on motivated reasoning, selective exposure, and media psychology)

### P3: Integration Propaganda as Democratic Necessity

PRINCIPLE: In stable democracies, the dominant form of propaganda is *integration propaganda* — not agitation (mobilizing for change) but integration (adjusting people to conform to the existing patterns of society). Integration propaganda uses participation itself — voting, activism, consumption, civic engagement — as the mechanism of conformity. The democratic citizen who votes, follows the news, and holds opinions *is the integrated subject*.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional

MECHANISM: Integration propaganda operates by: (1) defining the range of acceptable participation (vote for these candidates, debate these issues, consume these products); (2) rewarding participation within that range (civic virtue, informed citizenship, consumer satisfaction); (3) making participation feel like agency while structurally constraining its outcomes. The mechanism is self-concealing because it operates through *encouragement* rather than suppression — the subject feels free *because* they are participating, and the participation itself is the control. This is the Inversion of Stated Purpose operating at the level of democratic practice: participation is presented as freedom; its function is conformity.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Ellul contrasts Soviet agitation propaganda (mobilize the masses for revolutionary transformation) with American integration propaganda (adjust the citizen to the existing order). The American form is more effective and more stable because the citizen *believes they are free*. The periodic election, the consumer choice, the opinion poll — each provides the experience of agency while the structural outcomes remain within a range defined by the existing power configuration. The American does not need to be told what to think — they need only be given the range within which to think, and the act of choosing within that range produces the integration.

GENERALIZES TO: Corporate employee "engagement" programs (participation in feedback processes that do not affect structural decisions); platform "user voice" features (feedback mechanisms that produce investment in the platform without altering its extraction model); "stakeholder consultation" in policy-making (participation that legitimizes predetermined outcomes); consumer choice in oligopolistic markets (the experience of choosing between structurally identical options).

FRAMEWORK STATUS: provides the mechanism for how the Inversion of Stated Purpose operates at the level of democratic practice — participation functions as conformity; extends the Double Performance pattern — the democratic state and the citizen both perform, and the performance *is* the integration; confirms Axiom 7 at the system level — democratic participation states freedom, its structural function is integration

STATUS: inferred (Ellul's structural argument; the most philosophically provocative claim — contested by democratic theorists)

### P4: The Education Paradox

PRINCIPLE: Education increases susceptibility to propaganda rather than providing immunity. The educated consume more information, hold more opinions, believe more strongly in their own rationality, and are more exposed to the channels through which propaganda operates. The uneducated may be less susceptible because they are less exposed, less invested in having opinions, and less convinced of their own imperviousness.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional

MECHANISM: Education produces: (1) **Higher media consumption** — the educated read more, watch more, consume more information across more channels. (2) **The opinion imperative** — education teaches that the informed citizen has positions on issues; the educated feel compelled to form opinions on things they know only through media. (3) **Rationality confidence** — the educated believe they are thinking critically, which makes them resistant to the possibility that they are being propagandized. (4) **Vocabulary susceptibility** — education provides the conceptual vocabulary that rational propaganda exploits; the educated are the audience for arguments, data, expert opinion, and policy analysis — all channels of rational propaganda. The paradox: the tools that are supposed to provide critical immunity (literacy, knowledge, analytical skill) are the same tools that increase exposure and susceptibility.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Ellul argues that totalitarian propaganda requires a literate, media-consuming population — illiterate peasants are poor targets because they cannot be reached through print, they do not have the opinion imperative, and they are not invested in ideological positions. The most propagandized populations are the most educated ones — not because they are stupider but because they are more exposed and more invested.

GENERALIZES TO: "Information literacy" programs that increase engagement with media without reducing susceptibility; academic environments where expertise in one domain produces false confidence in political judgment; the "well-informed citizen" who reads quality journalism but absorbs its framing uncritically; tech workers who believe their analytical skills protect them from algorithmic manipulation.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: second convergent challenge to Axiom 10 (understanding as resistance) from the propaganda domain — Stanley's sincerity problem showed understanding fails when belief is sincere; Ellul's education paradox shows the *tools* of understanding (literacy, analysis, critical thinking) are the same tools that increase susceptibility. Both suggest Axiom 10 requires revision: understanding may be necessary but is insufficient without structural conditions that enable critical distance.

IC-1 FLAG: Axiom 10 — second independent source (after Stanley P5) suggesting the axiom overstates what understanding achieves. If the tools of understanding are themselves the channels of propaganda, understanding *within the system* may be a form of integration rather than resistance. The axiom may need qualification: understanding is resistance only when accompanied by structural independence from the propaganda environment.

STATUS: inferred (Ellul's structural argument; partly supported by later empirical work on educated populations' susceptibility to sophisticated propaganda)

### P5: Pre-Propaganda as Conditioning

PRINCIPLE: Effective propaganda requires a preparatory phase — *pre-propaganda* — that establishes the myths, stereotypes, emotional associations, and conceptual categories that later propaganda campaigns will exploit. Pre-propaganda is the long-term conditioning that makes populations receptive. Without it, propaganda fails; with it, specific campaigns need only activate dispositions that already exist.

LAYERS: Thought & Narrative (primary), Surveillance & Information, Institutional

MECHANISM: Pre-propaganda operates through: (1) **Myth establishment** — implanting foundational narratives (national greatness, racial hierarchy, market superiority, technological progress) that later propaganda will invoke. (2) **Stereotype conditioning** — creating categories and associations (the enemy, the foreigner, the radical, the scrounger) that can be activated on demand. (3) **Emotional priming** — establishing fear, pride, resentment, or aspiration that later campaigns will channel. (4) **Vocabulary installation** — introducing the conceptual categories through which specific propaganda will be interpreted. Pre-propaganda is sociological propaganda's specific preparatory function. It operates over years or decades and is visible only in retrospect.

EVIDENCE FROM WORK: Ellul argues that no propaganda campaign works in a vacuum. Hitler's propaganda succeeded because decades of anti-Semitic mythology, nationalist myths, and Dolchstoßlegende (stab-in-the-back legend) had prepared the population. Soviet propaganda succeeded because pre-revolutionary conditions and decades of revolutionary mythology had prepared the ground. American Cold War propaganda succeeded because decades of anti-communist conditioning (the Red Scare, anti-labor campaigns, religious framing of capitalism) had pre-positioned the population.

GENERALIZES TO: The decades-long installation of "government is the problem" rhetoric as pre-propaganda for deregulation and privatization; the construction of "terrorist" as a conceptual category pre-positioned for post-9/11 surveillance expansion; social media's ambient conditioning producing receptivity to specific political campaigns; corporate branding as pre-propaganda for consumer loyalty.

FRAMEWORK STATUS: provides the temporal mechanism for how the Dispossession Cycle's habituation phase works — what appears as sudden acceptance of a new incursion was pre-conditioned by years of ambient preparation; extends Temporal Control — pre-propaganda is the future-oriented component, controlling what will be thinkable by conditioning what is already felt; connects to Newspeak mechanisms — pre-propaganda installs the vocabulary that constrains future thought

STATUS: inferred (Ellul's structural analysis; empirically recognizable across numerous propaganda campaigns)

## Patterns identified

### Existing patterns confirmed or extended

- **Inversion of Stated Purpose** (ESTABLISHED): Ellul P3 adds the democratic instance — participation presented as freedom, functioning as integration. The inversion operates at the system level: the democratic process itself is the mechanism of conformity.
- **Appearance as Structural Terrain** (ESTABLISHED): Ellul P1 extends — sociological propaganda *is* the terrain. Not a gap between appearance and reality but a total environment in which appearance and reality merge because there is no external vantage point.
- **Real-Time Narrative Saturation** (was PRELIMINARY → propose SUPPORTED): Ellul describes the mechanism of information abundance preventing synthesis decades before digital media — the structurally overloaded individual who consumes everything and synthesizes nothing. The framework's analysis of Guardian live blog coverage identified the pattern empirically; Ellul provides the theoretical account from a completely different era. Two meaningfully independent sources warrant SUPPORTED.
- **The Dispossession Cycle** (SUPPORTED): Ellul P5 (pre-propaganda) provides the temporal mechanism — the habituation phase is not passive acceptance but active conditioning over years.
- **Anticipatory Obedience** (PRELIMINARY): Ellul P2 extends from the demand side — the propagandee actively seeks the conformity that anticipatory obedience describes. The mechanism is structurally similar but the locus is different: Snyder describes obedience anticipating power's demands; Ellul describes the subject demanding the propaganda that produces obedience.

### New pattern proposed

- **The Structural Demand for Propaganda**: Propaganda persists not because it is imposed but because it is structurally needed by both those who exercise power (to govern at scale without continuous coercion) and those subject to it (to reduce anxiety, provide certainty, create belonging in an atomized society). The demand side stabilizes the supply. This explains why exposure and education do not reduce susceptibility — the need is structural, not informational. PRELIMINARY (1 source).

### IC-1 flags

- **Axiom 3 (fourth mode)**: Sociological propaganda is invisible not through any of the three current modes (hidden mechanism, hidden contingency, prohibitive cost) but because the mechanism is the total environment — there is no outside. The axiom may need a fourth mode: *absence of vantage point*.
- **Axiom 10 (second convergent challenge)**: Ellul's education paradox (P4) converges with Stanley's sincerity problem (P5) to challenge whether understanding is sufficient for resistance. Both sources independently argue that the tools of understanding are co-opted by the very power structures understanding seeks to resist. The axiom may need qualification: understanding is necessary but insufficient without structural independence from the propaganda environment.

## Connections to existing principles

- **Stanley P1** (undermining propaganda): Ellul P3 provides the structural context — undermining propaganda is a *species* of integration propaganda. Stanley describes the mechanism at the argument level; Ellul describes the system-level conditions that make undermining propaganda effective.
- **Stanley P5** (sincerity problem): Convergent with Ellul P2 — the propagandee's sincerity (they genuinely seek the propaganda) mirrors the propagandist's sincerity (they genuinely believe their undermining arguments). Both ends of the communication are sincere; the structural effect is control.
- **Orwell P1** (Newspeak): Ellul's pre-propaganda (P5) is the real-world mechanism for what Orwell imagined as Newspeak — except it operates through vocabulary installation rather than vocabulary reduction. Pre-propaganda adds words and concepts (the categories through which future propaganda will be interpreted); Newspeak removes them. Both constrain what is thinkable.
- **Zuboff P4** (dispossession cycle): Ellul P5 provides the temporal mechanism — habituation is not passive but is the result of pre-propaganda conditioning over years.
- **Zuboff P8** (inevitabilist ideology): A species of Ellul's sociological propaganda — the ambient narrative that technological development is inevitable and resistance is futile.
- **Scott P1** (hidden transcript): Ellul's framework raises the question of whether sociological propaganda can prevent the formation of a hidden transcript entirely — if the propaganda *is* the environment, there may be no offstage space. Scott's peasants had an offstage; Ellul's modern citizen may not. This is an unresolved tension between the two frameworks.
- **Piketty P4** (meritocratic myth): A specific instance of Ellul's pre-propaganda — decades of meritocratic conditioning prepare the population to accept inequality as natural.
