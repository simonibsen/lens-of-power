# The Double Performance

STATEMENT: In most stable power relationships, both the dominant and the
subordinate perform for each other — the ruler performing legitimacy, the
subordinate performing compliance — and both sides are aware the other is
performing, yet maintain the mutual fiction because the cost of breaking it
is asymmetric.

MECHANISM: The ruler performs virtue, benevolence, or meritocratic fairness
(Machiavelli: the prince must appear merciful, faithful, humane). The
subordinate performs deference, gratitude, or consent (Scott: the public
transcript of compliance). Both sides know the performance is a performance.
The ruler knows the subordinate's deference is strategic; the subordinate
knows the ruler's virtue is a facade. But neither side can afford to say so:
the ruler because acknowledging the fiction would undermine the legitimacy it
produces; the subordinate because breaking the performance invites reprisal.
The public transcript — the official version of the relationship — is therefore
a jointly produced fiction maintained for different strategic reasons by both
parties. Stability depends not on either side believing the fiction but on
neither side breaking it.
