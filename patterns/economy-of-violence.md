# The Economy of Violence

STATEMENT: Violence is most effective when concentrated at the moment of
acquisition and absent thereafter. The temporal calibration of coercion
determines whether it produces stability or resistance.

MECHANISM: Injuries concentrated at one point are tasted once and fade from
memory. Benefits distributed gradually create ongoing loyalty. Escalating
cruelty produces escalating resistance because subjects have no reason to
believe it will stop. The optimal pattern is: decisive force, then investment.

NOTE: [principles/fanon-wretched-of-the-earth.md](https://github.com/simonibsen/lens-of-power/blob/main/principles/fanon-wretched-of-the-earth.md) P1 challenges this pattern
from the subjected perspective: colonial violence is total and constitutive,
not calibrated. The pattern describes the ruler's optimal strategy but Fanon
argues that from below, there is no "well-used" foundational violence — all
constitutive violence delegitimizes the system it establishes. The pattern
holds as an empirical description of the ruler's calculus but carries no
normative weight.
