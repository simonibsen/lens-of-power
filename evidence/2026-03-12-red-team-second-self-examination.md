### Taxonomy applied to framework

**Thought & Narrative**: The framework frames everything as power by
design. The analytical question is whether it can distinguish "power
dynamics are present" (true of most social phenomena) from "power
dynamics are the analytically significant dimension" (stronger claim).
The null case discipline enforces this distinction — but 47% null case
rejection rate during normal practice, with all accepted null cases
from adversarial input, suggests the distinction is not yet sharp.

**Institutional**: The framework's infrastructure (32 patterns, 26
principles, 10 circumventions, build pipeline, viewer, automated tests,
YAML data layer) creates institutional momentum. Each addition
increases the cost of questioning the framework. The infrastructure
creates authority even when the content should be provisional.

**Surveillance**: The framework makes control mechanisms visible
everywhere. The Anticipatory Obedience inter-state extension
illustrates this: the analytical move is legitimate (structural
parallel exists) but the framework made it easy — too easy. A
non-framework analysis of the same material might focus on economic
pragmatism, diplomatic strategy, or domestic political calculation.

### Falsifiability assessment

Axioms 1, 2, 4, 6, 7 have never been challenged. Of these:
- Axiom 4 may be structurally unfalsifiable (LLM training data bias)
- Axiom 7's falsification condition (system whose stated purpose
  matches function) has never been tested by deliberate analysis
- Axioms 1, 2, 6 have limited evidence overall (2 entries each)

No analysis has ever concluded "Pattern X was expected here but the
evidence doesn't support it." Patterns are always confirmed or
extended, never disconfirmed.

### Recommended adjustments

1. **Test Axiom 4**: Analyze a genuinely novel mechanism (AI deepfake
   political intervention, algorithmic radicalization) and honestly
   assess whether it has structural parallels. If the framework forces
   parallels where none exist, the axiom is distorting.

2. **Challenge Axiom 7**: Analyze a system where stated purpose ≈
   actual function (emergency dispatch, air traffic control, open-source
   governance). If the framework cannot produce a clean Axiom 7 null,
   the axiom is too broad.

3. **Pattern pruning**: Review 14 PRELIMINARY patterns. Patterns with
   <3 sources after 36 analyses should be flagged for potential
   retirement.

4. **Extension discipline**: New pattern extensions must include
   explicit falsification conditions specific to the extension. "What
   would demonstrate that this extension doesn't apply?" without an
   answer, the extension is assertion not analysis.

5. **Position 2 acquisition**: Analyze material authored by tenants,
   workers, detainees, or communities subject to the power structures
   the framework documents.

6. **YAML migration check**: Verify that pattern notes and variant
   annotations survived the migration intact. Confirm no analytical
   nuance was lost in the markdown-to-YAML transition.

addressable. The framework is not ideological — it responds to
self-examination. But it must continue to do so.
