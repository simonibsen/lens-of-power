## Content

Axiom 7 states that "the stated purpose of a system rarely describes
its function." The directive asks: "What does this system actually do,
as distinct from what it claims to do?"

Two independent sources document a variant where the stated purpose
IS the function — but the function produces systematic bias through
ideological framing rather than through a gap between statement and
reality.

**Case 1: Justice Field's property absolutism (Gates, 1971)**

Field stated his purpose openly: "I endeavored, whenever the occasion
presented itself... to protect the Mexican grantees." His stated
purpose accurately described his function. He sincerely believed in
absolute property rights and consistently acted on that belief. The
power operation lay not in a gap between purpose and function but in
the ideological framing that made one value (property) override all
others (equity, fraud prevention, settler claims, Mexican legal
provisions). Field accepted testimony from officials proved to have
falsified documents, relaxed documentation requirements, and declared
confirmed decisions "no longer open for consideration, whether right
or wrong." The mechanism: sincere ideology as a filter that resolves
every ambiguous case in the same direction.

**Case 2: EU rule-of-law monitoring (Liberties, 2026)**

The Commission's monitoring cycle states its purpose as promoting
rule of law through recommendations. Its function is also promoting
rule of law through recommendations. But: 93% of recommendations are
repeated across cycles, 61% show no progress, zero are fully
implemented. The monitoring system's failure is not a gap between
stated purpose and function — the system sincerely monitors. The
power operation is that monitoring-without-enforcement produces the
appearance of accountability that substitutes for accountability
itself. The stated purpose (monitor) and the function (monitor) are
identical; the structural effect (legitimation of erosion) is a
product of the system's design, not of a purpose-function gap.

## Significance

These cases complicate Axiom 7's framing. The axiom assumes power
operates through a gap between stated purpose and actual function.
These cases document power operating through sincere alignment of
purpose and function where the purpose itself — applied as an
absolute or without enforcement mechanism — produces systematic bias.
The axiom may need to account for cases where the stated purpose is
accurate but ideologically filtered (Field) or structurally incomplete
(EU monitoring).

This connects to Stanley's Sincerity Problem (sincere belief makes
propaganda resistant to exposure) and Schimpfossl's candor differential
(genuine conviction makes the mechanism invisible to those who operate
it). The common thread: sincerity is not the absence of power but a
specific mode of its operation.
