## Facts

- Rubio stated the US attacked Iran "preemptively" after learning
  Israel was going to strike
- Rubio: "There absolutely was an imminent threat. And the imminent
  threat was, that we knew that if Iran was attacked, and we believed
  that they would be attacked, that they would immediately come after
  us."
- Rubio: "We were not going to sit there and absorb a blow before we
  responded."
- The "imminent threat" to US forces was created by Israel's decision
  to strike, not by an Iranian action
- US mission stated as: destruction of ballistic missile capabilities,
  navy threat to shipping
- Rubio: "We would not be heartbroken" if Iranians overthrew their
  government
- Six US service members killed since Saturday

## Significance

The structural implication of Rubio's justification: a foreign
government's unilateral decision to go to war created the "imminent
threat" the US cites for entering that war. The causal chain is:
Israel decides to strike → Iran expected to retaliate → US forces
at risk → US strikes "preemptively." The "preemptive self-defense"
framing converts an offensive war of choice into an apparent defensive
necessity. The justification addresses the immediate tactical question
(protect US forces) while obscuring the strategic one (why US forces
were positioned such that Israel's decision created the threat).

This is a documented instance of Inversion of Stated Purpose:
"preemptive self-defense" describes the opposite of what occurred.
It also represents a structural mechanism — alliance entrapment as
justification — worth monitoring as a potential pattern.
