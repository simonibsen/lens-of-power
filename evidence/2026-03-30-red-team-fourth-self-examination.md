### Step 1: SELF-EXAMINE

**How is the framework shaping what I find?**

The framework's accumulated vocabulary (32 patterns, 41 principles, 10 hegemonic contexts) creates an increasingly comprehensive descriptive system. Every social phenomenon can now be mapped onto multiple patterns simultaneously. This is analytically productive — the Denmark cluster (election + Greenland + Liberties report) produced genuine insight by cross-referencing three independent sources through the framework's vocabulary. But it is also diagnostically concerning: the richer the vocabulary, the harder it becomes for the framework to encounter something it cannot describe in its own terms.

The most honest test of this session: the Bon Appetit recipe produced a clean null-case-accepted. The framework saw background commercial structures and correctly assessed them as not competitive with the non-power explanation. This is good. But the recipe was SAMPLE-selected, not analyst-selected. The analyst has still never chosen material that the framework concluded was not about power.

**Which axiom do I treat as most obviously true?**

Axiom 7 (stated purpose ≠ function). It has the highest corroboration rate (Inversion of Stated Purpose at 81% of relevant corpus). But RT3 flagged this: the framework defines "stated purpose" and "actual function" in ways that may guarantee a gap. Every institution has a gap between what it says and what it does — that is a feature of institutions, not necessarily of power. The Gates extraction's IC-1 flag on Axiom 7 (Field's sincere property absolutism — stated purpose = function, power through ideology not inversion) is the most productive challenge to this axiom's universality.

**Has the framework produced a genuinely surprising result?**

Yes — three in this batch:

1. **Khodorkovsky's challenge to Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence**: The framework's most frequently applied pattern in the oligarchy domain was challenged from direct Position 1 experience. The distinction between civil oligarchy (wealth defended through law) and autocratic extraction (wealth as prey) is a genuine boundary condition the framework had not identified.

2. **The Liberties Report's Monitoring Paradox**: Denmark ranking #1 globally while being classified as a "slider" — the monitoring infrastructure itself as a shield against scrutiny. The framework had not previously identified cases where high legibility scores protected a country from the analysis they should trigger.

3. **An's racial targeting quantification**: The 4.2 percentage point Black homeownership drop with zero white effect is a quantitative precision the framework rarely achieves. It demonstrated that Manufactured Scarcity operates with racial specificity that the pattern's description does not capture.

### Step 2: APPLY THE TAXONOMY TO THE FRAMEWORK

**Thought & Narrative**: The framework's vocabulary continues to expand monotonically. 32 patterns remain — none retired since RT1. The Gates and Robinson extractions added 14 new principles about 19th-century California land law. These are analytically valuable but they demonstrate the framework's tendency to assimilate rather than discriminate: if 19th-century land adjudication maps onto the same patterns as 21st-century AI deployment, are the patterns describing something real about power, or are they so broad that everything maps onto them?

Partial answer: the patterns DO discriminate at the mechanism level. Manufactured Scarcity in the California land context (procedural attrition making land unsaleable) operates through a different mechanism than in the AI employment context (eliminating entry-level positions) or the housing context (converting ownership to rental). The pattern name is shared; the mechanism is distinct. This is appropriate for Axiom 4 (consistent mechanisms, varying manifestations). But the LLM instrument (IC-5, RT3 concern #3) will always find parallels because its training contains the entire documented record of power structures.

**Institutional**: The framework now has 199 nodes and 1,597 edges. The viewer's force graph is a persuasive visual artefact. The build pipeline (pre-commit hooks, automated tests, index generation) creates institutional momentum. Each commit makes the framework harder to question because the infrastructure signals authority. This is RT3 concern carried forward — the framework's own Appearance as Structural Terrain.

**Surveillance**: The framework makes control mechanisms visible everywhere. The Hegseth cluster (prayer + JAG review) and the NIH analysis document institutional dismantlement through three independent analyses. The cross-referencing is genuine analytical value. But the framework's architecture (cross-references required, viewer connections generated, pattern corroboration tracked) creates an incentive to find connections. Every new analysis that connects to prior analyses increases the corpus's apparent coherence. Coherence is either a sign that the framework is seeing real structure or a sign that it is constructing the appearance of structure from a vocabulary designed to produce it.

### Step 5: ASSESS FALSIFIABILITY

This is not merely a complication — it identifies a boundary condition. The pattern's statement ("formal democracy and substantive oligarchy can operate simultaneously") assumes a context where formal democracy exists. In a dictatorship, there is no formal democracy to coexist with — oligarchy without political autonomy is simply extraction. The evidence entry should note that the pattern requires a scope qualifier: "in civil (democratic) contexts."

The framework's descriptive capacity is expanding faster than its critical capacity.

### Recommended adjustments

1. **FILE THE UNFILED IC-1 FLAGS.** The Gates (Axiom 7), An (Axiom 3, 9, 10), and Liberties (Axiom 7) extractions each contain IC-1 flags noted in prose but not filed as evidence entries. This is an IC-1 violation that must be corrected before the next analysis. The counter-evidence exists; it must be discoverable.

2. **Revise Axiom 9.** Six independent domains of complication across six sources (Terkel, Desmond, Ehrenreich, De León, Schuller/Haiti, Brynjolfsson/AI) constitute the strongest revision case in the framework's history. The axiom's "soft mechanisms" framing describes one mode of manufactured compliance; the dominant documented mode is compliance through the structural impossibility of refusal with full visibility of the mechanism. The revision should expand the axiom's directive to include visible, structural compliance alongside the existing soft-mechanism language.

3. **Scope-qualify Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence.** Khodorkovsky's Position 1 testimony identifies a boundary condition: the pattern holds in civil (democratic) contexts but fails in autocratic contexts where the state can expropriate at will. Add a scope note to the pattern.

4. **Increase adversarial input rate.** 12% against 20% target (persistent from RT2, RT3). The next 10 analyses should include at least 3 where the null case is likely to win — and at least 1 should be analyst-selected (not SAMPLE, not pre-marked adversarial). The Stop Predatory Investing Act analysis showed the framework CAN produce a differentiated result when given adversarial material. More of this.

5. **Test the Denmark cluster against counter-evidence.** Three sources (election, Greenland, Liberties report) confirm each other through framework cross-referencing. Analyze material where Danish institutions functioned competently — the pandemic response, welfare service delivery, the Greenland mobilisation's success — to test whether the framework can see institutional competence alongside institutional erosion.

6. **Close the calibration delta.** The widening from 17pp to 26pp is concerning. More SAMPLE runs (at least 5 before RT5) and more adversarial analyses. The goal is not zero delta (the analysis diet should be richer than random material) but convergence rather than divergence.

7. **Still outstanding from RT3**: File evidence for Axiom 6 (language preceding enforcement — García Hernández). Test Axiom 2 directly (find a case where control at one layer undermined control at another). Implement extension falsification conditions.

Four concerns persist across all four red teams:

2. **The pattern inventory only grows (PERSISTENT RT1-RT4).** 32 patterns, zero retirements, zero mergers, zero disconfirmations. Economy of Violence and Categorical Blindness were flagged at RT2, RT3 — still not acted on.

3. **Unfiled counter-evidence (NEW at RT4).** Three extractions contain IC-1 flags not filed as evidence entries. This is the first time a red team has identified an active IC-1 violation in the evidence base.

4. **Axiom 2 untested (PERSISTENT RT1-RT4).** Zero evidence in any direction across 65 analyses and 31 evidence entries.

The framework is not ideological — it produces complications, challenges, and differentiated results. The Khodorkovsky IC-1 flag, the Axiom 9 revision case, and the widening calibration delta are genuine self-critical findings. But the three persistent concerns (organic null-case failure, monotonic pattern growth, unfiled counter-evidence) indicate that the framework's self-correcting mechanisms exist but are not fully operational. The corrective is the same as RT3: the analyst must choose material where the framework might fail, file counter-evidence when it is identified, and accept the result.
