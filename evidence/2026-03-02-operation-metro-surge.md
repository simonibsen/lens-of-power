## Facts

- DHS deployed ~3,000 agents (2,000 ICE + 1,000 CBP) to Minneapolis
- Stated purpose: immigration enforcement targeting undocumented
  immigrants involved in fraud
- 3,000 people arrested
- Only 23 of 3,000 were Somali; none had ties to fraud under
  investigation
- 5% had violent criminal records (ICE's own figure)
- Judge Blackwell: "overwhelming majority" of cases involved people
  lawfully present
- At least several listed arrestees were transferred from state custody
  before operation began, including one from 2003
- Two US citizens killed by federal agents (Renee Good, Alex Pretti)
- One detainee died in custody
- Video evidence contradicted official self-defense accounts in all
  three shooting cases
- Chief Judge Schiltz documented 96+ court order violations in January
- 344 wrongful detention lawsuits filed December-January 21 (vs. 128
  in all of 2025, vs. 375 in 2016-2024 combined)
- $203.1 million economic impact in January (city assessment)
- 76,200 people experienced food insecurity; 8,713 children needed
  food services
- Mental health service contact dropped 50%
- ICE internal memo (May 2025) asserted warrantless home entry authority
- AG Bondi demanded voter rolls, Medicaid data, SNAP records hours
  after Pretti killing
- 1,500 active-duty soldiers (11th Airborne) on prepare-to-deploy
  orders
- Grand jury subpoenas issued to governor, mayor, AG for public
  statements
- General strike January 23: 50,000-100,000 marched; 700+ businesses
  closed; 1,000+ unions endorsed
- 8 Senate Republicans broke ranks to block DHS funding
- After "withdrawal" announced, ICE continued operating in suburbs
  using drones, plain clothes, and door-to-door canvassing
