## Content

Axiom 6 states that "language is the first instrument of control" —
whoever controls the terms of discourse controls the boundaries of
thought, and policy follows narrative.

García Hernández documents that the linguistic shift from "immigrant"
to "illegal alien" preceded and enabled the construction of the
immigration enforcement architecture. The term "illegal" applied to
border crossing was a policy choice, not a natural description —
border crossing was not always criminalised, and the specific legal
category of "illegal entry" was constructed through legislation that
followed the linguistic redefinition.

The sequence documented: (1) linguistic redefinition (immigrant →
illegal alien), (2) legal category construction (criminalisation of
border crossing), (3) enforcement architecture (detention system,
immigration courts, border infrastructure), (4) industrial complex
(detention-industrial complex with private profit incentive).

This is Axiom 6's mechanism in its clearest documented form: the
language created the category; the law followed the language; the
enforcement followed the law; the industry followed the enforcement.
At each stage, the prior stage was necessary for the next. Without
the linguistic category "illegal," the enforcement architecture
would lack its foundational justification.

García Hernández also documents the historical parallel: the
criminalisation of migration parallels vagrancy laws, pass laws,
and fugitive slave laws — all of which criminalised movement by
specific populations to maintain labour control. The linguistic
mechanism is consistent across these cases (Axiom 4): define
movement by a specific population as criminal, then build the
enforcement apparatus.
