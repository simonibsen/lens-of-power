# Counter-evidence: propaganda demand is symmetrical, complicating asymmetry model

DATE RECORDED: 2026-03-09
SOURCE: Ellul, *Propaganda* (1962), P2; Atlantic "gullicism" analysis — via patterns-detail.md NOTE
SOURCE TYPE: case study

## Tags
AXIOMS: 5, 9
PATTERNS: The Structural Demand for Propaganda
LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional
RELATIONSHIP: complicates

## Content

The framework's implicit model treats power as asymmetric imposition:
Axiom 5 (power concentrates unless counteracted) and Axiom 9 (compliance
is manufactured, not voluntary) both position the governed as recipients
of power's effects. The Structural Demand for Propaganda pattern
complicates this model at the mechanism level.

Ellul's P2 (the propagandee's need) documents that the governed actively
seek propaganda — for certainty, belonging, cognitive economy, and
anxiety reduction in a complex, atomized society. The demand is not
imposed; it arises from the structural conditions of modern life
(information overload, opinion imperative, social fragmentation). The
intellectual who reads five newspapers is more propagandized than the
peasant who reads nothing, because exposure and the felt obligation to
have opinions increase susceptibility.

The Atlantic's "gullicism" essay documents this demand in real time:
Americans simultaneously reject empirically validated claims and accept
extravagant assertions from commercial and political actors. The "One
True Reason" syndrome (autism is vaccines, no housing is immigrants) is
the demand for cognitive economy Ellul describes. A commercial
infrastructure (supplements, crypto, sports betting) has emerged to
serve this demand.

The tension: power is asymmetric in its *distribution of benefits* (the
powerful benefit disproportionately from propaganda), but the *mechanism*
of propaganda is partly symmetrical in demand — both governors and
governed need it. The governors need it to govern at scale without
continuous coercion; the governed need it to cope with complexity and
atomization. This symmetry of need explains propaganda's durability:
it cannot be eliminated by exposing the supply side because the demand
side regenerates it.

## Significance

This complicates Axioms 5 and 9 without invalidating them. The asymmetry
in benefits remains — rulers gain disproportionately. But the mechanism
is not pure imposition; it is a co-produced structure where the governed
are active participants in their own subjection. This has practical
consequences: counter-propaganda strategies focused on debunking, media
literacy, or exposure will fail unless they also address the structural
conditions (atomization, complexity, opinion imperative) that produce
the demand. The axioms may need refinement to distinguish asymmetry of
benefit from asymmetry of mechanism.
