# Axiom 9 complication: structural violence — compliance through impossibility of alternatives (fifth domain)

DATE RECORDED: 2026-03-15

TIME RECORDED: ~16:00 UTC

SOURCE: Lumen Learning / SUNY ESC, *Cultural Anthropology — Economics* (OER textbook chapter), drawing on Mark Schuller's fieldwork in Haiti

SOURCE TYPE: case_study (mediated through textbook)

## Tags

AXIOMS: 9

PATTERNS: compliance_gradient, manufactured_scarcity_as_control, constitutive_violence

LAYERS: economic, institutional, physical_coercive

RELATIONSHIP: complicates

## Content

Axiom 9 states that "compliance can be manufactured without explicit
coercion" and lists mechanisms (dependency, precarity, social pressure,
algorithmic curation, default settings, bureaucratic friction). The
directive asks what "soft mechanisms are producing conformity."

The Haiti case documented by Schuller shows compliance produced not
through soft mechanisms or concealment but through the structural
impossibility of alternatives. The population sees the extraction clearly
(richest 20% hold 64%+ of wealth; aid goes to NGOs and the US military,
not to displaced persons). The mechanisms are not soft — they are
institutional arrangements that prevent basic needs from being met
(structural violence). The population complies because the choice set
has been constrained to survival.

This is the fifth independent domain confirming that Axiom 9's current
framing overemphasises the invisibility and "softness" of compliance
mechanisms:

1. Workplace (Terkel): workers see dependency, name it, comply because
   cost of refusal is prohibitive
2. Housing (Desmond): tenants see exploitation, comply because
   alternative is homelessness
3. Low-wage service (Ehrenreich): workers see coercion clearly, comply
   because unemployment is worse
4. Border crossing (De León): migrants see lethal risk, cross because
   the alternative is worse
5. Structural violence (Schuller/Haiti): population sees extraction,
   complies because institutional arrangements prevent basic needs
   from being met

The convergence across five independent domains — workplace, housing,
low-wage service, border crossing, and post-colonial structural
violence — suggests the axiom needs revision. The "soft mechanisms"
framing captures one mode of manufactured compliance (the mode most
visible in affluent, technology-mediated contexts). The dominant mode
in most of the world may be simpler: compliance through the structural
impossibility of refusal, with full visibility of the mechanism.

## Significance

Fifth independent complication of Axiom 9 across a fifth domain. The
convergence strengthens the case that the axiom's current framing
(emphasising invisibility and softness) describes a specific subset
of compliance manufacture — the subset most relevant to affluent,
technology-mediated societies — rather than the general mechanism.
