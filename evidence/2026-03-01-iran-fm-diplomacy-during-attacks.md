# Iran FM: US attacked during active negotiations twice in 12 months

DATE RECORDED: 2026-03-01
SOURCE: Guardian live blog (citing Abbas Araghchi interview on ABC's This Week)
SOURCE TYPE: quote

## Tags

## Content
Iran's foreign minister Abbas Araghchi stated on ABC's This Week on
March 1, 2026:

"We negotiated with the United States twice in the past 12 months. And
in both cases, they attacked us in the middle of negotiation. And that
has become a very bitter experience for us."

He added: "A deal was at our reach, and we left Geneva happily with the
understanding that we can reach a deal next time we meet."

## Significance

vs. function): the stated purpose of negotiations was to reach a
diplomatic resolution; the actual function may have been to maintain the
appearance of diplomatic effort while military planning continued. This
would also instantiate P6 (controlled opposition as a trap) at the state
level — diplomacy as a mechanism that channels the opponent into a
cooperative posture while the attacking party prepares force.

VERIFICATION STATUS: Unverified. This is a claim by an interested party
(Iran's government) during an active conflict. It requires independent
verification — specifically, the diplomatic timeline, what was offered,
and whether the US initiated military action during or immediately after
active negotiation periods. If corroborated, this should be elevated
to HIGH significance. If contradicted, record as challenged.
