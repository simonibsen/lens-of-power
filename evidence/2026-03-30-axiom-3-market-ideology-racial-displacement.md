# Axiom 3 complication: market ideology as hegemonic naturalisation of racially targeted displacement

DATE RECORDED: 2026-03-30

TIME RECORDED: ~23:00 UTC

SOURCE: Brian Y. An, "The Influence of Institutional Single-Family Rental Investors on Homeownership" (2024)

SOURCE TYPE: case_study

## Content

Axiom 3 states that "power is most effective when its contingency is
invisible." The fifth mode (hegemonic naturalisation) describes how
the framework within which mechanism, contingency, and perception
operate is itself a product of the power structure.

An's research documents institutional investors purchasing single-family
homes with racial specificity: twice as likely to buy in Black
neighbourhoods, producing a 4.2 percentage point drop in Black
homeownership with zero effect on white homeownership. The mechanism
operates through formally neutral instruments — all-cash offers,
algorithmic property identification, technology-enabled rapid
purchasing — that produce racially differential outcomes without any
individual decision-maker intending racial targeting.

The hegemonic naturalisation operates through market ideology: the
displacement is described as "market forces," "investment activity,"
or "housing market dynamics." The racial specificity — documented
quantitatively — disappears within the market frame because market
categories (price, return, risk) are defined without racial content.
The Stop Predatory Investing Act's press release, written by
senators seeking to address the problem, does not mention race at all
despite the academic documentation that the mechanism operates with
racial precision.

This is Axiom 3's fifth mode in a specific register: the framework
within which the displacement is understood (market economics) is
itself structured to make the racial dimension invisible — not through
concealment but through categorical incompatibility. Market categories
cannot express what the mechanism does because the mechanism's racial
operation is orthogonal to market vocabulary.
