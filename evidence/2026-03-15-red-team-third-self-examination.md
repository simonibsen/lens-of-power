4. **Pattern extensions lack falsification conditions.** RT2

   rather than boundary testing.

6. **Anglophone monoculture persists.** ~80% US-focused, zero
   non-English sources. The framework claims civilisational scope

### Taxonomy applied to framework

**Thought & Narrative**: The framework's analytical vocabulary creates
a closed descriptive system. Every social phenomenon maps onto 6
layers and 32 patterns. The vocabulary makes power-dynamics readings
linguistically easy and non-power readings linguistically awkward.
The calibration boundary sharpening (this session) is the first
attempt to create vocabulary for "this is not about power." The
hegemonic context system (IC-7) adds analytical depth but also makes
the framework more totalising — every analysis must now name the
hegemonic background, which reinforces the framework's tendency to
find power everywhere by requiring it to be named everywhere.

**Institutional**: The framework now has: a constitution, a taxonomy,
a methodology, 32 patterns, 34 principles, 7 instruments, 12
circumventions, 10 hegemonic contexts, 48 analyses, 28 evidence
entries, a viewer with 171 nodes and 1,156 edges, automated tests,
a build pipeline, and a pre-commit hook. This infrastructure creates
authority. Each addition increases the cost of questioning the
framework. The viewer — with its force graph, corroboration matrix,
and integrity dashboard — makes the framework look scientific. This
appearance may be structural terrain (Axiom 3, fifth mode applied
reflexively).

**Surveillance**: The framework makes control mechanisms visible
everywhere. Is it seeing what is there, or constituting what it
claims to observe? (Browne's Racializing Surveillance principle
applied reflexively.) The Inversion of Stated Purpose pattern at
65% corroboration may reflect a genuine structural feature or may
reflect that the framework defines "stated purpose" and "actual
function" in ways that guarantee a gap.

### Falsifiability assessment

Axioms 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8 have never been challenged. Of these:
- Axiom 2 has zero evidence in any direction (untested)
- Axiom 4 may be structurally unfalsifiable by an LLM instrument
- Axiom 6 has zero evidence entries despite supporting findings in
  extractions (unfiled)

IC-7 (hegemonic complicity) is definitionally unfalsifiable. It
asserts the framework operates within the hegemonic order it studies.
What would falsify this? An analysis conducted from outside the
hegemonic order — but IC-7 says this is impossible. This is a
feature (prevents the framework from claiming a god's-eye view), not
a bug, but it should be named: IC-7 is performative (position-naming)
rather than operational (output-constraining).

No pattern has ever been disconfirmed. Patterns are confirmed,
extended, or left at PRELIMINARY. The pattern inventory only grows.

### Recommended adjustments

1. **Test Axiom 2 directly**: Find a case where control at one layer
   undermined rather than reinforced control at another. If the
   framework cannot even imagine such a case, the axiom is a
   definitional truth, not a falsifiable claim.

2. **File evidence for Axiom 6**: The García Hernández finding
   (language preceded enforcement architecture) should be recorded.
   Seek counter-examples where power shifted without preceding
   language change.

3. **Retire or merge PRELIMINARY patterns**: Economy of Violence
   (2 sources, flagged at RT2) and Categorical Blindness (2 sources,
   proposed merger into Division of Learning) have not gained evidence
   in 12 analyses. Apply the discipline.

4. **Implement extension falsification conditions** (RT2 #4, still
   outstanding): Every pattern extension must state what would
   demonstrate it doesn't apply.

5. **Produce an organic null-case-accepted**: Analyze current-events
   material NOT pre-selected as adversarial that the framework
   genuinely concludes is not about power. After 48 analyses, this
   has never happened.

6. **Document IC-7's unfalsifiability**: Note in constitution.md that
   IC-7 is performative (position-naming) rather than operational
   (output-constraining). This is not a defect but should be explicit.

7. **Increase adversarial input rate**: Currently 14.6% (target 20%).
   The next 10 analyses should include at least 3 where the null
   case is likely to win — and at least 1 should NOT be pre-marked
   as adversarial.

### Self-assessment

The framework has improved structurally since RT1 (confirmation bias
HIGH → MEDIUM) and maintained that level through RT2 and RT3.
Position 2 sources have been extracted. The hegemonic context system
is a genuine intellectual advance. Calibration discipline was
sharpened this session.

Three concerns persist from RT1 through RT3:

1. The framework has never been wrong organically. All 6 accepted
   null cases were adversarially selected. In normal analytical
   practice, the framework has produced "plausible" 25 times but
   "accepted" zero times. The calibration delta has narrowed (27pp
   → 17pp) but the core asymmetry remains: the framework finds power
   dynamics in everything it is asked to analyze.

2. The anglophone monoculture persists. Zero non-English sources.
   ~80% US-focused. The framework claims civilisational scope but
   draws almost exclusively from one civilisation's media output.

3. The pattern inventory only grows. No pattern has been retired,
   merged, or disconfirmed. No pattern extension has been reversed.
   The framework's analytical vocabulary expands monotonically.

The framework is not ideological — it responds to self-examination,
it has revised axioms, it has produced complications and challenges.
But it has not yet demonstrated the capacity to fail on its own terms
in the course of normal analytical work. That capacity is the
difference between an analytical framework and a sophisticated
confirmation machine. The corrective is not structural (the integrity
constraints exist) but operational: the analyst must choose material
where the framework might fail, and must accept the result when it
does.

## Significance

Third invocation of IC-3. Triggered at 12 analyses since RT2
(threshold: 5-10). The framework shows genuine structural
improvement in some areas (Position 2 extraction, hegemonic context
system, calibration discipline) while three core concerns persist
unchanged across all three red teams: organic null-case failure,
anglophone monoculture, and monotonic pattern growth. The persistence
of these concerns across three self-examinations suggests they are
structural features of the framework's operation rather than
correctable oversights — they may require changes to how the
framework is used (what material the analyst chooses) rather than
changes to the framework itself.
