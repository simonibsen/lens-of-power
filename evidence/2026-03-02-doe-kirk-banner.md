## Facts

- The US Department of Education hung a banner featuring Charlie Kirk
  on its building in Washington DC
- Kirk was displayed alongside banners of Catharine Beecher and Booker
  T. Washington
- A banner reads: "Empowering our States to tell the Stories of our
  Heroes in American Education"
- The Department called all three "visionary leaders whose contributions
  have shaped the future of education for generations"
- Kirk called for abolishing the Department of Education "as recently
  as last year"
- Kirk called the Civil Rights Act of 1964 a "mistake"
- Kirk was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by
  Trump (October 2025)
- A similar Trump banner was previously unveiled on the DOJ building

## Significance

This is a direct, literal instance of the Inversion of Stated Purpose:
the Department of Education honors someone who called for its abolition.
It is also an instance of the strategic abdication variant of
Institutional Abdication: the institution uses its remaining authority
to legitimate the program that includes its destruction. The
"Empowering our States" banner text is the liberation narrative
accompanying the abdication.

The pairing with Beecher and Washington is a legitimation-by-association
technique that borrows uncontested educational legitimacy for a
contested political figure.

Combined with the DOJ Trump banner, this constitutes evidence of a
pattern: federal buildings being repurposed as surfaces for political
iconography.
