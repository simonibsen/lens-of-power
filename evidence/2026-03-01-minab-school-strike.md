## Content
On Saturday, February 28, 2026, a US-Israeli airstrike hit a girls'
elementary school in Minab, southern Iran, killing 148 people and wounding
95. The school was adjacent to an Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC)
barracks.

Video and photographs from the aftermath were verified as authentic and
geolocated to the site. They show a partially collapsed building, rubble
across the street, people digging for victims, schoolbags and textbooks
in the debris.

CENTCOM spokesperson Capt Tim Hawkins responded: "We are aware of reports
concerning civilian harm resulting from ongoing military operations. We
take these reports seriously and are looking into them."

The strike occurred during "Operation Epic Fury," a US-Israeli military
campaign aimed at regime change in Iran.
