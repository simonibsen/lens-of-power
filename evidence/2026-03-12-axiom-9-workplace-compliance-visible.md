# Axiom 9 complication: workplace compliance with visible coercion

## Content

Axiom 9 states: "Compliance can be manufactured without explicit
coercion." The axiom emphasizes that "the absence of visible force
does not indicate the absence of control." Workers in Terkel's
*Working* complicate this formulation: they are not subjects of
invisible coercion. They see the dependency mechanism clearly, name
it in their own vocabulary, and comply because the cost of refusal is
prohibitive — not because the coercion is concealed.

This is closer to Axiom 3's third mode (visible mechanism, visible
contingency, prohibitive cost) than to Axiom 9's emphasis on
manufactured compliance "without the appearance of coercion." In the
workplace, the coercion is visible: the wage dependency, the
replaceable worker, the absence of alternatives. The compliance is
manufactured through structural constraint, not through concealment.

Axiom 9 may overstate the role of invisibility in workplace compliance.
The governed see the mechanism and comply anyway. The manufacturing
occurs at the level of the choice set (eliminating affordable
alternatives) rather than at the level of perception (hiding the
mechanism).

## Significance

First IC-1 flag from a Position 2 source. The governed perspective
reveals that Axiom 9's emphasis on "without the appearance of coercion"
may not describe their experience accurately. The coercion appears —
they see it — but they cannot afford to act on what they see. This is
a structural observation, not a semantic quibble: it matters whether
compliance is produced by concealment or by constraint, because the
countermeasures are different.
