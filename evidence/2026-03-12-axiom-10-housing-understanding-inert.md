# Axiom 10 complication: housing understanding is structurally complete but operationally inert

SOURCE TYPE: training_data

## Content

Axiom 10 states that understanding resists "when epistemic capacity is
intact, when ideology has not formed a closed logical system, and when
the act of naming cannot be institutionally captured."

Tenants in *Evicted* satisfy all three conditions. Their epistemic
capacity is intact — they see the exploitation clearly. No closed
ideology prevents recognition — they name the landlord's incentives,
recognize the court's function, and understand their structural
position. Their naming has not been captured by institutions.

Yet understanding does not produce resistance. It produces endurance.
The missing condition is the same one identified in the Terkel
complication: leverage. The tenants lack alternative housing, legal
representation, collective organization, and financial reserves.
Understanding without leverage is structurally complete but
operationally inert.

This extends the Terkel complication from the workplace to housing,
reinforcing the argument that Axiom 10 should make explicit a fourth
condition: structural capacity to act on understanding (audience,
organization, or structural position to convert naming into action).

## Significance

Third Position 2 complication of Axiom 10. Combined with Terkel's
workers, a consistent pattern emerges: the governed understand their
situation with precision but cannot operationalize that understanding
because they lack structural leverage. This is not an epistemic failure
(Axiom 10's primary concern) but a structural one — the understanding
is complete but the capacity to act on it is absent.
