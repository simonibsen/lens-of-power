## Content

Axiom 10 states: "Understanding how power operates is the first step
toward resisting it." Ehrenreich's coworkers understand their structural
position — they see that the wages are insufficient, name the
surveillance, recognize the power asymmetry. But understanding does not
produce resistance because they lack structural leverage. At zero
savings, with no union, in an employment-at-will relationship, the
distance between understanding and action is filled by the absence of
capacity.

The anti-union architecture specifically prevents the formation of the
collective leverage that could convert understanding into action. High
turnover prevents relationship formation. Anti-union messaging in
training signals the cost of organizing. Personality testing screens for
compliance attitudes. Employment-at-will enables termination without
stated cause. The architecture makes organizing structurally difficult
before any specific attempt is suppressed.

This is the fifth IC-1 complication of Axiom 10 from a Position 2
source, extending the pattern from the workplace (Terkel) and housing
(Desmond) to low-wage service work — the third independent domain.

## Significance

Third Position 2 domain confirming that Axiom 10's formulation requires
amendment. Understanding is necessary but not sufficient. The missing
condition across all three domains is structural capacity to act —
leverage, organization, resources, or collective identity that converts
understanding into effective resistance. Ehrenreich adds the specific
mechanism of anti-union architecture as a structural barrier between
understanding and action.
