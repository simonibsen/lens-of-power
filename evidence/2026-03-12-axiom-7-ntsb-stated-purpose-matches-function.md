# Axiom 7 challenge: NTSB investigation where stated purpose matches function

January 27, 2026

## Content

Axiom 7 states: "The stated purpose of a system rarely describes its
function." The NTSB's investigation of the January 29, 2025 DCA midair
collision is a case where the stated purpose (determine probable cause
of transportation accidents, issue safety recommendations) accurately
describes the actual function performed.

The NTSB:
- Investigated independently for one year
- Held three days of public hearings (July 30-August 1, 2025)
- Named the FAA directly for systemic failures (route design, data
  failure, non-implementation of prior recommendations)
- Named the US Army for inadequate safety management
- Published 74 findings and 50 recommendations
- Made all investigation materials publicly available
- Reached these conclusions despite political pressure (Trump
  administration's immediate DEI attribution contradicted by the
  findings; subsequent board member firings)

The institution did what it says it does. The Inversion of Stated
Purpose pattern does not apply to the NTSB's investigative function.

### Qualification

Axiom 7 says "rarely," not "never." This finding does not falsify the
axiom but provides the first current-events evidence approaching the
falsification condition (a system whose stated purpose accurately and
completely describes its actual function).

The inversion may apply at the *system level* rather than the
*institution level*: the NTSB recommends but cannot compel; the FAA
failed to implement prior recommendations that would have prevented this
crash. The system's stated purpose (safety through investigation →
recommendation → implementation) diverges from its actual function
(investigation → recommendation → non-implementation). The inversion
occurs at the interface between institutions.

## Significance

First current-events challenge to Axiom 7. Demonstrates that the
framework can identify institutions where stated purpose matches actual
function, addressing the second red team's concern that Axiom 7 may be
too broad to be analytically useful. The qualification (inversion at
system level vs. institution level) suggests the axiom may need
refinement to distinguish between institutions that genuinely perform
their stated function and the broader systems in which those institutions
are embedded.
