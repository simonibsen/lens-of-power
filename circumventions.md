# Circumventions

> Role: This document catalogs observed responses to power concentration
> at two tiers. **Structural circumventions** contest specific power
> arrangements within the existing hegemonic frame — using the system's
> own tools (law, elections, journalism, collective action) to challenge
> specific outcomes. **Hegemonic circumventions** contest the frame
> itself — the background assumptions that determine what counts as
> natural, realistic, or thinkable. The two tiers operate at different
> levels, different timescales, and with different success conditions.
>
> Each type documents what has been seen, under what conditions, and how
> power systems have responded. Uses observational register throughout —
> "has been observed to," "may," "appeared to."
>
> This is the compact reference — load this for every operation. For full
> evidence trails, see `circumventions-detail.md`.

---

## Tier 1: Structural circumventions

> These contest specific power arrangements within the existing hegemonic
> frame. They use the tools the system provides — law, elections,
> journalism, collective action, cultural production — to challenge
> specific outcomes, actors, or mechanisms. They can succeed on their own
> terms (a lawsuit won, an election won, a mechanism named) but they do
> not alter the background assumptions within which power operates. They
> are accessible to individuals and groups within a political cycle.

### Transparency / Naming
LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Surveillance & Information

COUNTERACTS: Inversion of Stated Purpose, Division of Learning, Appearance as Structural Terrain

STATEMENT: Investigations, journalism, whistleblowing, and public exposure have been observed to disrupt mechanisms that depend on concealment.

OUTCOME RANGE: Formal program termination observed (COINTELPRO, 1976). Structural conditions persisted in all documented cases. Naming captured and converted to appearance of accountability in at least one case (Kimberley Process). Most effective when concealment was operationally necessary; least effective against systems that can absorb exposure.

MECHANISM: Naming a hidden mechanism may reduce its effectiveness by making its operation visible to those subject to it. Observed to produce formal change (program termination, policy revision) though structural conditions may persist.

FAILURE MODES: Naming may be captured and converted into a new appearance of accountability (Alley: Kimberley Process). Formal termination may occur without structural change (Church Committee: COINTELPRO). Understanding may be absorbed by closed ideological systems (Arendt). Information overload may neutralize naming through saturation (Real-Time Narrative Saturation).

OBSERVED IN: principles/church-committee-cointelpro.md, principles/alley-very-bad-people.md, principles/snyder-on-tyranny.md

POWER RESPONSE: Co-optation, formal concession with structural persistence, classification/secrecy expansion

### Legal Challenge
LAYERS: Legal & Regulatory, Institutional

COUNTERACTS: The Capture Pipeline, Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence

STATEMENT: Litigation, constitutional challenge, and statutory enforcement have been observed to contest power arrangements through formal legal channels.

OUTCOME RANGE: Binding rulings and statutory enforcement observed. Outcomes appeared highly dependent on judicial independence — captured courts absorbed challenges without structural effect. Access asymmetry (wealth determines who can challenge) observed as persistent constraint. Formal victories without implementation documented.

MECHANISM: Legal systems contain formal commitments (equal protection, due process, statutory rights) that may be invoked against substantive power arrangements. The gap between formal commitment and actual practice creates leverage for challenge. Observed to produce binding rulings, settlements, and precedent.

FAILURE MODES: Legal challenge may be absorbed by captured courts (Whitehouse: the scheme). Access to legal challenge may itself be distributed by wealth, creating asymmetry in who can challenge. Victories may be formal without structural effect. Statutory rights may be narrowed through interpretation.

OBSERVED IN: principles/whitehouse-the-scheme.md

POWER RESPONSE: Forum shopping, judicial capture, legislative override, procedural exhaustion, strategic litigation against public participation (SLAPP)

### Collective Action
LAYERS: Physical & Coercive, Economic, Institutional

COUNTERACTS: Manufactured Scarcity, The Compliance Gradient

STATEMENT: Organized collective refusal — strikes, boycotts, mass protest, mutual aid — has been observed to alter power arrangements by raising the cost of maintaining them.

OUTCOME RANGE: Policy concessions and regime transitions documented. Sustained collective action observed to produce the most durable changes. Infiltration and leadership co-optation disrupted movements in multiple documented cases (COINTELPRO). Selective concessions that fracture solidarity observed as the most common power response.

MECHANISM: Individual compliance is cheap to enforce; collective non-compliance may become prohibitively expensive. The compliance gradient shifts when large numbers move simultaneously. Observed to produce concessions, policy change, and regime transition.

FAILURE MODES: Collective action may be disrupted through infiltration, co-optation of leadership, or selective concession that fractures solidarity. Free-rider dynamics may prevent formation. Surveillance may enable preemptive disruption. Protest may be absorbed into the appearance of democratic functioning (Double Performance).

OBSERVED IN: principles/scott-weapons-of-the-weak.md, principles/church-committee-cointelpro.md

POWER RESPONSE: Infiltration (COINTELPRO), selective concession, leadership co-optation, criminalization, surveillance escalation

### Institutional Reform
LAYERS: Institutional, Legal & Regulatory

COUNTERACTS: Institutional Abdication, The Capture Pipeline

STATEMENT: Formal restructuring of institutions — oversight bodies, transparency requirements, procedural safeguards — has been observed to constrain power concentration when sustained.

OUTCOME RANGE: Durable constraint observed when institutional interests aligned with continued enforcement. Church Committee reforms (FISA, oversight committees) constrained intelligence agencies for approximately 25 years before post-9/11 erosion. Reforms appeared fragile — dependent on sustained political commitment and vulnerable to gradual defunding, personnel replacement, and reinterpretation.

MECHANISM: Institutions may be redesigned to distribute authority, mandate transparency, or create countervailing oversight. Observed to produce durable constraint when institutional interests align with continued enforcement. Effectiveness appeared to depend on sustained political commitment.

FAILURE MODES: Reforms may be eroded through defunding, personnel replacement, or reinterpretation. Oversight bodies may themselves be captured. Reform may produce formal change without operational change (Formal Transformation, Structural Continuity). The institution may strategically abdicate its reformed role.

OBSERVED IN: principles/church-committee-cointelpro.md, principles/snyder-on-tyranny.md

POWER RESPONSE: Gradual erosion, defunding, personnel capture, reinterpretation of mandate, strategic non-compliance

### Cultural Production
LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional

COUNTERACTS: The Hidden Transcript, The Three Phases of Cultural Resistance

STATEMENT: Literature, art, music, film, and intellectual work have been observed to produce alternative frameworks that make existing power arrangements visible and thinkable as contingent rather than natural.

OUTCOME RANGE: Counter-narratives preserved across generations observed (Mississippi Delta oral tradition, Sedaka gossip networks). Vocabulary creation observed to enable subsequent political mobilization. Commodification absorbed critique into market in documented cases. Structural transformation through cultural production alone not documented — appeared to operate as enabling condition rather than sufficient cause. Institutional deployment variant: the AU's integration of arts and culture into the reparations campaign extends Cultural Production from covert transcript preservation to overt narrative construction — the mechanism is instrumentalised for institutional change rather than hidden resistance (Guardian, March 2026).

MECHANISM: Cultural production may create shared vocabulary, preserve counter-narratives, and make structural relations visible. Operates through the thought and narrative layer by producing what Fanon described as the synthesis phase — new frameworks that break existing binaries rather than merely inverting them.

FAILURE MODES: Cultural production may be commodified and absorbed into the market it critiques. It may remain confined to elite audiences. The hidden transcript may be surfaced and neutralized. Co-optation may convert critique into aesthetic consumption.

OBSERVED IN: principles/fanon-wretched-of-the-earth.md, principles/cobb-most-southern-place-on-earth.md, principles/scott-weapons-of-the-weak.md, analyses/2026-03-08-guardian-article-africa-reparative-justice.md

POWER RESPONSE: Commodification, aesthetic absorption, censorship, patronage systems that create dependency

### Everyday Resistance
LAYERS: All layers

COUNTERACTS: The Compliance Gradient, The Hidden Transcript

STATEMENT: Foot-dragging, feigned ignorance, pilfering, false compliance, gossip, anonymous sabotage, and other "weapons of the weak" have been observed to constitute a continuous, low-visibility terrain of power negotiation.

OUTCOME RANGE: Continuous negotiation of domination terms documented across all observed cases. Structural transformation through everyday resistance alone not observed — appeared to set the floor of what power can extract, not to end extraction. Cumulative constraint on power observed (Sedaka: employers could not fully enforce new labor terms). Most durable circumvention type documented — operates without organization, leadership, or public exposure.

MECHANISM: Subordinate populations may exploit the compliance gradient through individually deniable acts that cumulatively constrain the exercise of power. Requires no formal organization, no leadership, and no public declaration. The hidden transcript preserves collective knowledge of these techniques across generations.

FAILURE MODES: Everyday resistance has been observed to rarely produce structural transformation on its own — it may negotiate the terms of domination without ending it. May be misread as passivity or incompetence. Escalation to open resistance carries catastrophic risk.

OBSERVED IN: principles/scott-weapons-of-the-weak.md, principles/cobb-most-southern-place-on-earth.md

POWER RESPONSE: Increased surveillance, tightened controls, exemplary punishment, replacement of resistant populations with more dependent ones

### Economic Alternative
LAYERS: Economic, Institutional

COUNTERACTS: Manufactured Scarcity, The Peacetime Ratchet

STATEMENT: Debt jubilees, commons-based production, cooperative ownership, and alternative monetary systems have been observed to disrupt economic concentration by operating outside the dominant accumulation logic.

OUTCOME RANGE: Periodic accumulation reset sustained for approximately two millennia in Mesopotamian jubilee tradition. Scandinavian social democracy observed to constrain the peacetime ratchet for multiple decades through progressive taxation and social insurance. Scale limitations documented — alternatives that cannot match the coordination capacity of market systems appeared vulnerable to enclosure or absorption.

MECHANISM: Economic alternatives may reduce dependency by providing material security through non-market channels. Jubilee traditions observed to periodically reset accumulation. Commons-based systems may resist enclosure. Cooperative structures may distribute returns differently than investor-owned forms.

FAILURE MODES: Economic alternatives may be enclosed, regulated into conformity, or outcompeted by entities operating under different rules. Scale may be limited by coordination costs. Market systems may absorb alternatives through acquisition. Alternatives may reproduce internal hierarchies.

OBSERVED IN: principles/graeber-debt-the-first-5000-years.md, principles/piketty-capital-in-the-twenty-first-century.md

POWER RESPONSE: Enclosure, regulatory restriction, market absorption, ideological delegitimation ("utopian," "inefficient")

### Electoral / Democratic
LAYERS: Institutional, Legal & Regulatory, Thought & Narrative

COUNTERACTS: Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence

STATEMENT: Electoral mobilization, voting rights expansion, ballot initiatives, and democratic participation have been observed to contest oligarchic arrangements through formal democratic channels — though the relationship between democratic form and substantive change appeared contested.

OUTCOME RANGE: Redistributive policy outcomes documented during periods of high mobilization (Progressive Era, New Deal, Great Society). Gains observed to be systematically rolled back through organized wealth defense (Powell Memo as documented blueprint). The structural condition of democracy-oligarchy coexistence appeared to be the equilibrium rather than the exception — democratic victories may represent temporary deviations.

MECHANISM: Democratic systems contain formal mechanisms (elections, referenda, legislative process) through which popular preferences may be translated into policy. When mobilization overcomes structural advantages of concentrated wealth, democratic processes have been observed to produce redistributive outcomes.

FAILURE MODES: Democracy-Oligarchy Coexistence documents the structural condition in which democratic forms operate alongside oligarchic substance. Electoral victories may be absorbed through implementation capture. Voter suppression, gerrymandering, and campaign finance asymmetry may prevent democratic expression. Policy may be reversed by subsequent administrations.

OBSERVED IN: principles/hartmann-hidden-history-american-oligarchy.md

POWER RESPONSE: Voter suppression, gerrymandering, campaign finance flooding, judicial nullification, implementation capture, procedural obstruction

### International Pressure
LAYERS: Institutional, Legal & Regulatory, Economic

COUNTERACTS: The Boomerang

STATEMENT: International institutions, sanctions, treaty obligations, and transnational advocacy networks have been observed to constrain state-level power concentration by creating external accountability structures.

OUTCOME RANGE: Selective application documented as the norm — geopolitical alignment appeared to determine whether pressure was applied, not the severity of the violation. Accountability mechanisms captured and converted to appearances in at least one documented case (Kimberley Process). Sanctions observed to harm civilian populations while strengthening authoritarian regimes in some cases. Transnational advocacy amplification of domestic resistance documented but outcomes mixed.

MECHANISM: States that violate international norms may face economic sanctions, diplomatic isolation, or legal proceedings. Transnational advocacy networks have been observed to amplify domestic resistance by connecting it to international audiences and institutions. The "boomerang effect" describes domestic groups reaching out to international allies to pressure their own government from outside.

FAILURE MODES: International pressure may be selectively applied based on geopolitical alignment rather than principle. Sovereignty claims may be used to deflect external accountability (Sovereignty as Shield). Sanctions may harm civilian populations while strengthening authoritarian regimes. International institutions may themselves be captured.

OBSERVED IN: principles/alley-very-bad-people.md

POWER RESPONSE: Sovereignty claims, sanctions evasion, counter-narratives of imperialism, institutional veto power, bilateral deals that circumvent multilateral pressure

### Catastrophic Leveling
LAYERS: Physical & Coercive, Economic

COUNTERACTS: The Peacetime Ratchet

STATEMENT: Mass mobilization warfare, transformative revolution, state collapse, and pandemic have been observed to produce significant compression of inequality — through destruction of accumulated wealth rather than redistribution.

OUTCOME RANGE: The most significant and lasting reductions in inequality in the historical record were produced by catastrophic events (Scheidel). Compression observed to be temporary when post-catastrophe institutions did not maintain the new distribution. Revolution observed to produce new concentration through (Relay Class) in multiple cases. This is a structural observation, not a strategy — the human cost was immense in every documented case.

MECHANISM: Catastrophic events may destroy concentrated wealth, shift labor-capital ratios, or collapse institutions that maintained existing distribution. Observed to produce the most significant and lasting reductions in inequality in the historical record. The mechanism is destructive rather than strategic — it is not a tool but a structural observation.

FAILURE MODES: Catastrophic leveling has been observed to produce immense human suffering. The compression may be temporary if post-catastrophe institutions do not maintain the new distribution. Revolution may produce new concentration (Relay Class). The observation that catastrophe reduces inequality does not imply that catastrophe is desirable or strategic.

NOTE: This type is descriptive, not prescriptive. It documents a structural observation from the historical record (Scheidel). The framework records it because omitting it would suppress a significant finding. The observational register is especially important here — "has been observed to produce" is not "should be pursued."

OBSERVED IN: principles/scheidel-great-leveler.md

POWER RESPONSE: Post-catastrophe institutional capture, new concentration via (Relay Class), restoration of prior arrangements through undisrupted layers

## Tier 2: Hegemonic circumventions

> These contest the frame itself — the background assumptions that
> determine what counts as natural, realistic, or thinkable. They do not
> use the system's tools but build different tools, or exploit moments
> when the system's own contradictions expose its contingency. They
> operate at generational timescales rather than political cycles. They
> require institutional infrastructure, sustained commitment, and the
> kind of strategic self-awareness that Gramsci's organic intellectuals
> provide. The accessibility gap between Tier 1 and Tier 2 circumventions
> is itself a structural feature of hegemonic power — the frame is
> hardest to contest precisely because the tools available to most people
> are tools the frame provides.

### Counter-Hegemonic Construction
LAYERS: Thought & Narrative, Institutional, Economic

COUNTERACTS: Hegemonic naturalisation (Axiom 3, fifth mode)

STATEMENT: The slow, deliberate construction of alternative institutions, alternative intellectual personnel, alternative "common sense" — what Gramsci calls the war of position — has been observed to contest hegemonic arrangements at the civilisational timescale by building a parallel institutional and cultural infrastructure within the existing order.

OUTCOME RANGE: Labour movement construction of cooperative institutions, mutual aid networks, and workers' education (19th-20th century) produced durable alternative infrastructure that survived repression. Feminist consciousness-raising (1960s-1970s) transformed common sense around gender across a generation. Decolonial intellectual projects shifted the background assumptions of international relations. All operated across generational timescales. None achieved full hegemonic displacement — partial shifts in common sense with structural arrangements largely intact.

MECHANISM: Counter-hegemonic construction does not attack the existing order directly. It builds alternative institutions (schools, media, associations, cultural organisations) that produce alternative organic intellectuals, who elaborate alternative common sense, which over generational timescales shifts what the population considers natural, inevitable, or without alternative. The mechanism is molecular — it operates through thousands of small changes in institutional practice, educational content, and cultural production rather than through a single confrontation.

FAILURE MODES: Counter-hegemonic construction may be absorbed through passive revolution — the dominant formation incorporates just enough of the alternative to neutralise the challenge while preserving structural arrangements. Alternative institutions may be co-opted, defunded, criminalised, or marginalised. The generational timescale means that counter-hegemonic projects are vulnerable to interruption — a single election, a funding crisis, or a leadership transition can set back decades of molecular work. The construction of alternative common sense may remain confined to intellectual strata without reaching the broader population.

OBSERVED IN: principles/gramsci-prison-notebooks.md, principles/fisher-capitalist-realism.md, principles/fanon-wretched-of-the-earth.md

POWER RESPONSE: Passive revolution (absorption of challenges through partial concession), co-optation of alternative intellectuals, defunding or criminalising alternative institutions, precorporation (pre-formatting oppositional impulses to be compatible with the system)

### Hegemonic Rupture
LAYERS: All layers

COUNTERACTS: Hegemonic naturalisation (Axiom 3, fifth mode)

STATEMENT: Moments when a hegemonic system's internal contradictions become visible because the system fails on its own terms — financial crises, ecological collapse, pandemics, institutional failures — have been observed to create openings in which the background assumptions of the order become temporarily visible as contingent rather than natural.

OUTCOME RANGE: The 2008 financial crisis made market capitalism temporarily visible as a contingent arrangement rather than a natural law — "there is no alternative" was briefly suspended. The COVID-19 pandemic revealed the contingency of the wage-labour relationship and the possibility of state intervention at scales previously deemed unrealistic. In both cases, the rupture was temporary — the hegemonic order reasserted itself through a combination of institutional response, narrative reconstruction, and passive revolution. Durable structural change from rupture alone has not been observed without pre-existing counter-hegemonic infrastructure to exploit the opening (Gramsci: the permanently organised force that can be deployed when conditions become favourable).

MECHANISM: Hegemonic rupture is not a strategy but a structural condition. When a system fails on its own terms — when markets that are supposed to be efficient produce catastrophic failure, when institutions that claim to protect produce harm, when arrangements presented as inevitable are suddenly disrupted — the contingency that is normally invisible becomes temporarily perceptible. The rupture does not by itself produce change; it creates a window during which counter-hegemonic forces, if they exist and are organised, can advance.

FAILURE MODES: Ruptures are absorbed through the hegemonic system's asymmetry of institutional response time — the existing formation reorganises faster than challengers can organise. Narrative reconstruction (hegemonic dreamwork) smooths over contradictions without resolving them. The rupture may produce cynicism and reflexive impotence rather than mobilisation. Without pre-existing counter-hegemonic infrastructure, the opening closes before alternative arrangements can be consolidated.

NOTE: Like Catastrophic Leveling, this type is descriptive. It documents a structural observation about when hegemonic systems become temporarily vulnerable. It is not a strategy — ruptures cannot be manufactured, and exploiting them requires the prior molecular work of counter-hegemonic construction.

OBSERVED IN: principles/gramsci-prison-notebooks.md, principles/fisher-capitalist-realism.md

POWER RESPONSE: Asymmetry of institutional response time (reorganise faster than challengers organise), narrative reconstruction (hegemonic dreamwork), passive revolution (partial concession to re-establish consent), state intervention to stabilise the existing arrangement while framing it as "reform"
